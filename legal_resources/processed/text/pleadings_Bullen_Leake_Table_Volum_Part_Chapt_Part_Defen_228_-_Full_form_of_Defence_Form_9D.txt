1. How much of the claim do you dispute?
We dispute the full amount claimed as shown on the claim form.
2. Do you dispute this claim because you have already paid it?

Defence
1.
The Defendant admits and avers that it entered into the agreement with the Claimant set out in and annexed to the Claim
Form.
2.
The Defendant denies that the agreement was subject to the express or implied terms set out in the Particulars of Claim
or any terms to the like effect. The allegedly express terms do not form part of the written agreement and the allegedly
implied terms do not arise by reason of the business efficacy of the agreement or by any statutory implication.

3.
Even if, which is denied, the said terms of any of them were terms of the agreement, the Defendant denies that it is in

4.
In the premises the Claimant is not entitled to the relief or any relief.
