Introductory remarks
30-1
Article 30 is the gateway for the resolution of “small” claims together with Appendix VI of the 2021 Rules. 635  The 2017 ICC
Rules of arbitration had adopted the new provisions for expedited procedures with the objective of having final awards issued
by sole arbitrators six months after the first case management conference. Article 30 and the Expedited Procedure Rules in
App.VI are defined in art.30(1) as the Expedited Procedure Provisions.
30-2
Subject to the conditions discussed below, for arbitrations commenced after 1 January 2021, the Expedited Procedure Provisions
apply whenever the value of the claims in question does not exceed US$3 million, which is roughly one third of ICC arbitrations.
The parties can opt in or opt out of the expedited procedures provisions, partially or totally, regardless of the amount in
dispute. 636  The ICC has summarized the use of the Expedited Procedures as follows in its statistical report for 2020 637 :
“To date, 261 cases have been or are being conducted under the EPP. The EPP applied automatically in 178 cases
(i.e. in cases not exceeding the monetary threshold and based on contracts concluded on or after entry in force of
the EPP). There have been 291 requests to opt-in to the EPP, of which 83 agreed to by the other party(ies).”
30-3
The decision to institutionalise expedited procedures in the 2017 Rules was an implicit admission that the objectives of the 2012
Rules have not been met when it came to improving the time- and cost-efficiency of smaller ICC arbitrations. The new provisions
offer users of ICC arbitration (who are concerned about increased time and costs) an alternative to expedite the resolution of
their disputes. The provisions of art.30 should be read in the light of the requirements of art.22 in particular and the requirements
of applicable law. In practice, expedited procedures may increase due process challenges by dissatisfied litigants, before and
after awards are issued. For these reasons, more than before, selecting a pro-arbitration place of arbitration will be important.
30-4
In 2002, the ICC Secretariat issued a practical “Note on Expedited ICC Arbitration Procedure”, 638  for parties wishing to
organise an expedited ICC arbitration procedure pursuant to art.32 of the 1998 ICC Rules. That article explicitly allowed parties
to shorten the time limits set out in the ICC Rules. In that note, the ICC reminded users that “expedited or fast-track arbitration
proceedings have always been possible” under the ICC Rules. 639  Indeed, the concept of expedited procedures has not been
foreign to ICC arbitrations, even before the provision allowing parties to shorten deadlines was introduced. Between 2000
and 2005, in the face of mounting criticism about time and costs spent in arbitration proceedings, the ICC Commission on
Arbitration set up a Task Force, which eventually led to the publication in 2007 of its report on “Techniques for Controlling
Time and Costs in Arbitration”. 640  The report recommended the use of various techniques for parties, counsel and arbitrators
to better control time and costs throughout the arbitration process.
30-5
Prior to adopting the 2012 Rules, careful consideration had been given to these issues within the ICC Court and its Secretariat,
and in particular at the ICC Commission. Many debates and enquiries had taken place before coming to the conclusion that no
special rules should be introduced into the ICC Rules for “small” claims. Instead, the ICC adopted two new provisions meant
to address the need of the users when it came to small claims. On the one hand, art.22 required arbitrators and parties to make
every effort to conduct the arbitration in an expeditious and cost-effective manner, having regard to the complexity and value

of the dispute. On the other hand, art.24 required arbitrators to convene a case management conference at the early stage of
the arbitration and to consult the parties regarding the procedural measures, in particular on the basis of the case management
techniques listed in the newly drafted App.IV to the ICC Rules. This approach put flexibility in how to best address the issues
in each case, as well as trust in the ability of arbitrators and parties to make good use of these guidelines, at the forefront.
30-6
The Expedited Procedure Provisions have proven useful in practice. By the end of 2020, 261 cases had been or were being
conducted under the Expedited Procedure Provisions. 641  At the end of 2020, 90 final awards had been rendered under the
Expedited Procedure Provisions. 642  Compared to the overall number of cases registered by the ICC since 2017, the Expedited
Procedure Provisions have not been used as extensively as anticipated—whether they were applied automatically or through
the opt-in provision. 643  This may incidentally be one of the reasons that motivated the ICC to raise the benchmark amount
to US$3 million in the 2021 Rules.
