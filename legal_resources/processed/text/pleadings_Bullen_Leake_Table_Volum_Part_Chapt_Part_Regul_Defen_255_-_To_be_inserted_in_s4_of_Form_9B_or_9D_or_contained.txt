Regulated agreements: events after the making of the contract
Defence in relation to a “distance selling” transaction
To be inserted in s.4 of Form 9B or 9D or contained in a separate document
1.
20-F66
The Defendant admits that he entered into the agreement referred to in the Particulars of Claim (“the credit agreement”).
2.
The credit agreement was made in order to finance a contract between the Defendant and CD whereby CD sold [specify
goods] to the Defendant at a price of £[..........] (“the sale agreement”).
Agreement made before 13 June 2013
3.
The sale agreement was a distance contract within the meaning of the Consumer Protection (Distance Selling) Regulations
2000 (“the 2000 Regulations”) in that [set out the circumstances relied on as showing that this was a distance contract].
Agreement made after 13 June 2013
3.
The sale agreement was a distance contract within the meaning of The Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and
Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (“the 2013 Regulations”) in that [set out the circumstances relied on as showing
that this was a distance contract].
4.
Under [reg.10 of the 2000 Regulations], [reg.29 of the 2013 Regulations] the Defendant had the right to cancel the sale
agreement.
5.
By a notice given [date] a copy of which is annexed to this Defence, the Defendant duly cancelled the sale agreement.
Under [reg.15 of the 2000 Regulations], [reg.38 of the 2013 Regulations], the cancellation of the sale agreement operated
automatically to cancel the credit agreement and the credit agreement ceased to be enforceable.

Or
4.
In breach of the sale agreement, CD failed to deliver the said goods within the date specified for delivery.
5.
Under [reg.20 of the 2000 Regulations], [reg.42 of the 2013 Regulations] such failure operated in the same way as if the
Defendant had given a valid notice of cancellation. The credit agreement was thereby automatically cancelled and ceased
to be enforceable.
6.
In the circumstances, the Defendant is not liable to the Claimant in the sum claimed or any sum.
[Statement of truth]
Note that the assignee of the creditor is to be regarded as the creditor for all purposes was confirmed in Jones v Link
Financial Limited [2012] EWHC 2402.
Note also that an assignee must now arrange for notice of the assignment to be served on the debtor (in general) as soon
as reasonably possible under CCA 1974 s.82A.
