Seller’s Defences
Defence under s.48D of the Sale of Goods Act 1979 or s.23 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F48
The Defendant admits that he entered into the agreement with the Claimant set out in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant admits that the agreement was subject to the conditions implied by [s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979
(“the 1979 Act”)] [ss.9 and 10 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015].
3.
The Defendant denies that the goods failed to conform with the agreement either as alleged in the Particulars of Claim
or at all.
4.
It is admitted that the Claimant asserted that the goods were subject to the defects alleged in the Particulars of Claim and
that he requested the Defendant to [replace][repair] them.
5.
It is also admitted that the Claimant subsequently purported to reject the goods and to terminate the agreement for breach
of condition.
6.
It is denied that the Claimant was entitled to reject or terminate as alleged. The Defendant was at all material times ready,
willing and able to [replace][repair] the goods. A reasonable time for doing so would be [set out time] but before that time
had elapsed from the date of the request to [replace][repair], the Claimant purported to reject and terminate.
7.
In the circumstances, the Claimant is precluded from rejecting or terminating by [s.48D of the 1979 Act] [s.23 of the
2015 Act].
