Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
Inappropriateness to apply Expedited Procedure Rules (lit. c)
30-36
Under art.30(3)(c), the ICC Court, upon the request of a party made before the constitution of the Tribunal, or on its own motion
may decide, depending on the circumstances, that it is inappropriate to apply the Expedited Procedure Provisions. Article 1(4) of
App.VI grants the ICC Court the same power any time during the arbitral proceedings, but it must then consult the Tribunal and
the parties. It further states that when the ICC Court decides to stop applying the Expedited Procedure Provisions, the Tribunal
shall remain in place, unless the court considers that it is appropriate to replace and/or reconstitute the arbitral tribunal.
30-37
The application of art.30(3)(c) will most likely be a difficult task for the ICC Court and constitutes a serious challenge, as it
may well exceed a purely administrative assessment. Indeed, one can expect that some parties will make substantial efforts,
rightly or wrongly, to show to the arbitral tribunal and the ICC Court “that it is inappropriate in the circumstances to apply the
Expedited Procedure Provisions”, for instance through various procedure applications to arbitral tribunals or through the filing
of lengthy submissions accompanied with numerous exhibits.
