Seller’s Defences
Denial that the sale is one by description or sample (model) and denial of non-correspondence with description or
sample
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F44
The Defendant admits that it entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Defendant agreed to sell and
deliver the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
It is denied that the sale was a sale by description either as alleged in the Particulars of Claim or at all. The sale was a
sale of specific goods seen and identified by the Claimant before the sale and no description was attached to them by the
Defendant.
Or
2.
It is admitted that the sale was a sale by description but it is denied that the goods were described in the manner alleged
in the Particulars of Claim. The goods were described as [here set out description relied on by the Defendant] in an oral
conversation between the Claimant and the Defendant on [date] and in a sales brochure delivered to the Claimant before
the sale (copy annexed to this Defence).
3.
It is denied that the goods failed to correspond with the alleged or any description whether as set out in the Particulars
of Claim or at all.
Or
2.
It is denied that the sale was a sale by [sample] [reference to a model] either as alleged in the Particulars of Claim or at
all. The Defendant denies [furnishing the Claimant with the alleged or any sample] [showing the Claimant any model]
prior to the sale agreement.
