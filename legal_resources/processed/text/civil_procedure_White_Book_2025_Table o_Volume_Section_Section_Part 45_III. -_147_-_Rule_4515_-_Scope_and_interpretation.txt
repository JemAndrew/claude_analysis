III. - Fixed Percentage Increase in Road Traffic Accident Claims
 
Related Practice Directions
Rule 45_15
45.15— Scope and interpretation 1
7B-30+
(1) This Section sets out the percentage increase which is to be allowed in the cases to which this Section applies.
(Rule 43.2(1)(l) defines “percentage increase” as the percentage by which the amount of a legal representative’s fee
can be increased in accordance with a conditional fee agreement which provides for a success fee.)
(2) This Section applies where—
(a) the dispute arises from a road traffic accident; and
(b) the claimant has entered into a funding arrangement of a type specified in rule 43.2(k)(i).
(Rule 43.2(k)(i) defines a funding arrangement as including an arrangement where a person has entered into a
conditional fee agreement or collective conditional fee agreement which provides for a success fee.)
(3) This Section does not apply if the proceedings are costs only proceedings to which Section II of this Part applies.
(4) This Section does not apply—
(a) to a claim which has been allocated to the small claims track;
(b) to a claim not allocated to a track, but for which the small claims track is the normal track;
(c) where the road traffic accident which gave rise to the dispute occurred before 6th October 2003; or
(d) to a claim to which Section VI of this Part applies.
(5) The definitions in rule 45.7(4) apply to this Section as they apply to Section II.
(6) In this Section—
(a) a reference to “fees” is a reference to fees for work done under a conditional fee agreement or collective
conditional fee agreement;
(b) a reference to “trial” is a reference to the final contested hearing or to the contested hearing of any issue
ordered to be tried separately;

Effect of rule
7B-30.
1+
This Section was added to Pt 45 by the Civil Procedure (Amendment) Rules 2004 (SI 2004/1306) and came into effect on 1 June
2004. The rules in this Section make provision in road traffic accident claims for fixed percentage increases to apply to legal
representatives’ fees in respect of success fees, where the claimant has entered into a conditional fee agreement or collective
conditional fee agreement which provides for a success fee (as specified in CPR r.43.2(k)(i)). The provisions apply only where
the road traffic accident giving rise to the dispute occurred on or after 6 October 2003. The provisions do not apply to a small
claim, i.e a claim on the small claims track or a claim for which that track is the normal track. If Section II of Pt 45 is applicable
Section III does not apply. Section VI of this Part (referred to in r.45.15(4)) imposes a fixed recoverable costs regime on claims
that have been started (or should have been started) under Practice Direction 8B (Pre-Action Protocol for Low Value Personal
Injury Claims in Road Traffic Accidents—Stage 3 Procedure).
This rule and the other rules in this Section (rr.45.16 to 45.19) apply in relation to a “pre-commencement funding arrangement”;
see para.7B-19+, and paras 48.0.1 to 48.0.4 (“Continuing application of CPR Pts 43 to 48 and Costs Practice Direction as in
force immediately before 1 April 2013”).
The words “at trial” in r.45.15 mean at a contested hearing. It is clear from r.45.17(1)(a) and (b)(i) that the rules recognise
a distinction between a trial and the date fixed for the commencement of the trial. Settlement before a trial commences and
conclusion by settlement after trial commences could both occur on the date fixed for the trial. The trigger for the entitlement
to a 100% uplift in fees is not a settlement on a particular date, but a settlement or conclusion after a trial, i.e a hearing, has
commenced. Where settlement is achieved before the hearing has commenced the uplift is 12.5% (Amin v Mullings [2011]
EWHC 278 (QB), [2011] P.I.Q.R. P10 (Slade J.)). Where on the day fixed as the first day of the trial the court adjourned the
case until 2pm the following day and the case then settled, the claim did not conclude at trial: James v Ireland [2015] EWHC
1259 (QB) (Slade J.).
In Lamont v Burton [2007] EWCA Civ 429; [2007] 1 W.L.R. 2814, CA, a claimant (C) who was injured in a road traffic accident
refused a Pt 36 payment from the defendant (D) who had admitted liability, and, at the subsequent “disposal hearing”, the judge
awarded C less than the Pt 36 payment. The judge awarded C costs up to the latest date on which the payment could have been
accepted, and ordered C to pay the D’s costs from that date. Although C had failed to beat the Pt 36 payment, C claimed, and
the judge granted, a success fee of 100%, because the matter had concluded at trial. On appeal, D argued that since r.44.3(6)
contains a non-exhaustive list of the costs orders than can be made by the court, there was nothing in Pt 44 which denied it
jurisdiction to award such percentage increase as it considered appropriate in all the circumstances, even if such an increase was
at variance with that prescribed by r.45.16, and submitted that in the circumstances an increase of 12.5% would be appropriate.
The Court of Appeal rejected these contentions. The Court found that the words “the percentage increase which is to be allowed”
in r.45.16 (and r.45.17) are mandatory and it was not open to a court to circumvent the mandatory provisions of Pt 45. (The
Court added that the same applied to the comparable rules in Sections IV and v of Pt 45.) In the circumstances provided for by
r.47.18 a court may allow a success fee different from that prescribed by r.45.16 or r.45.17.
