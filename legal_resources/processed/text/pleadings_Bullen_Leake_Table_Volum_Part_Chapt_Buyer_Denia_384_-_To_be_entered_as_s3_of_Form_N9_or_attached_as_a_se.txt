Buyer’s Defences
Denial that payment of the price was due when proceedings were issued (credit not expired or bill of exchange not yet
matured)
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F54
The Defendant admits that it entered into the written contract with the Claimant annexed to the Claim Form whereby the
Claimant agreed to sell and deliver to the Defendant the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
By clause 6 of the contract it was an express term that the purchase price was to be paid within 30 days of the Claimant’s
invoice. The Claimant’s invoice was dated [date] (copy annexed to this Defence) and the time for payment therefore expired
on [date]. The claim in this action was issued on [date] prior to the expiry of the time for payment.
Or
2.
By clause 7 of the contract it was an express term that the purchase price should be paid by means of a 90-day bill
of exchange accepted by the Defendant. On [date] the Defendant delivered to the Claimant a bill duly accepted by the
Defendant in the amount of the purchase price payable in 90 days, namely on [date]. The claim in this action was issued
on [date] prior to the maturing of the bill of exchange.
3.
In the premises, at the date of the issue of the claim in this action no part of the purchase price was due or owing to the
Claimant.
