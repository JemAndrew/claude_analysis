Defence

25-F66
The Defendant admits that it entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Claimant agreed to sell to the
Defendant the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Claimant sold the goods to the Defendant in the course of his business as [identify business].
3.
Prior to the agreement, the Defendant orally informed the Claimant that he was a [state nature of business] and that he
required the goods for [set out purpose communicated to the seller].
4.
By reason of the [Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.14] [the Consumer Rights Act 2015 ss.9 and 10], there were implied into
the agreement terms that:
 
(a)the goods should be of satisfactory condition;
 
(b)the goods should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Defendant required it, namely [set out purpose].
5.
In breach of the said terms, the goods were not of satisfactory condition.
