Chapter 24 - Set-off
Exceptions
Cheques and Bills of Exchange
24-04
A cheque is equivalent to cash. Accordingly, an action upon a cheque will (in the absence of a total failure of consideration)
almost invariably succeed irrespective of any set-off or counterclaim raised by the defendant. A stay of execution pending trial
of the counterclaim will be refused. 10  A direct debit instruction is now regarded as equivalent to a cheque. 11
Freight under a voyage charter
24-05
This exception also applies to contracts for the international carriage of goods by road (CMR) subject to the Geneva Convention
and to domestic carriage. 12
Rent
24-06
A tenant may set up a cross-claim based on the landlord’s breach of covenant as a defence to a claim for rent provided the cross-
claim is sufficiently closely connected. 13  Even where a lease provided for rent to be paid without any deductions, it was held
that this would not ordinarily exclude a right of equitable set-off arising from the landlord’s own breach. 14
Contracting-out
24-07
Parties may contract out of the obligation to permit a set-off but this can only be done by clear and unequivocal words or a clear
implication. 15  Save in the most exceptional circumstances, the court will usually enforce a “no set-off” clause. 16
