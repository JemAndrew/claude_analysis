Appendix 1 - Institutions with Model Rules and/or Model Laws
A1-001 There are many excellent national or regional arbitration centres. The following list should not be read as a recommendation
for any particular institute but can be used as a reference list of significant or growing global institutions.
1. United Nations Commission on International Trade Law
(UNCITRAL):
The UNCITRAL Arbitration Rules are flexible rules widely used for both ad hoc and administered arbitrations. (https://
uncitral.un.org/en/texts/arbitration)
UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration serves as a template for countries to implement modern
arbitration laws. (https://uncitral.un.org/en/texts/arbitration/modellaw/commercial_arbitration)
2. Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (CIArb):
CIArb promotes effective dispute resolution with an emphasis on resolving disputes promptly, effectively and creatively. To do
this it provides education and training, coupled with support, advice and networking for arbitrators, mediators and adjudicators.
It also acts as a global hub for practitioners, policy makers and academics. The CIArb can also provide facilities for hearings.
(https://www.ciarb.org/clauses/)
3. China International Economic and Trade Arbitration Commission
(CIETAC):
CIETAC Current Arbitration Rules are applied to disputes that typically involve one party who is not from mainland China,
including cross-border trade disputes, joint venture conflicts and foreign investment disputes. (http://www.cietac.org/index.php?
m=Page&a=index&id=65)
4. Deutsches Institut für Schiedsgerichtswesen e.V. (German Arbitration
Institute) (DIS):
A leading arbitration institution in Germany that administers domestic and international arbitrations irrespective of industry
under its own rules or the rules of other arbitral institutions. (https://www.disarb.org/en/arbitration-and-alternativedispute-
resolution/arbitration/arbitration-process)

5. Dubai International Arbitration Centre (DIAC):
DIAC administers arbitration proceedings with around 44% 1  of its cases involving international disputes and the rest from the
UAE. The main sources of the disputes are construction, commercial, real estate, corporate, maritime, and oil and gas. (https://
www.diac.com/en/about-diac/)
6. Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre (HKIAC):
HKIAC Administered Arbitration Rules used under HKIAC administration to provide institutional support for HKIAC
arbitrations covering a broad range of cross-border disputes with a strong focus on the Asia-Pacific region. (https://
www.hkiac.org/arbitration/rules-practice-notes/administered-arbitrationrules/hkiac-administered-2018-1)
7. International Arbitration Centre (Astana International Financial
Centre) (IAC)
The IAC’s primary role is to provide a platform for the resolution of civil and commercial disputes within the AIFC. It offers
services including administering arbitrations under its set of rules or other agreed-upon rules (like UNCITRAL) and providing
mediation services to help parties reach a negotiated settlement. Its focus is on making dispute resolution accessible through
the AIFC, Kazakhstan, the Eurasia region and globally. (https://iac.aifc.kz/en)
8. International Chamber of Commerce (ICC):
ICC Rules of Arbitration are designed for complex international commercial arbitration with ICC administration. (https://
iccwbo.org/dispute-resolution/dispute-resolution-services/arbitration/rules-procedure/arbitration-clause/)
9. International Centre for Dispute Resolution/American Arbitration
Association (ICDR/AAA):
ICDR is prominent in the US, offering various arbitration rules and resources. (https://www.adr.org/)
10. International Centre for the Settlement of Investment Disputes
(ICSID):
ICSID specialises in investor-state disputes with its own rules and conventions. (https://icsid.worldbank.org/rules-regulations)

11. London Chamber of Arbitration and Mediation (LCAM):
LCAM administers disputes in accordance with the LCAM Arbitration Rules and focuses on providing straightforward rules in
order that disputes can be managed (through mediation or arbitration) with economy, efficiency, expediency and enforceability.
(https://lcam.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/LCAM-Arbitration-Rules_compressed.pdf)
12. London Court of International Arbitration (LCIA):
LCIA Arbitration Rules are designed for larger, more complex international commercial cases overseen by the LCIA. (https://
www.lcia.org/Dispute_Resolution_Services/lciaarbitration-rules-2020.aspx)
13. Permanent Court of Arbitration/Cour Permanente D’Arbitrage
(PCA/CPA)
The PCA/CPA provides services for the resolution of disputes involving various combinations of states, state entities,
intergovernmental organisations and private parties, and can act as an appointing authority under UNCITRAL rules.
(https://pca-cpa.org/en/services/arbitration-services/pca-arbitration-rules-2012/)
14. Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC):
The SIAC provides services for arbitration of global commercial disputes and is the leading centre in Asia-Pacific. (https://
siac.org.sg/siac-rules-2016)
15. Stockholm Chamber of Commerce Arbitration Institution (SCC):
SCC administers a wide range of disputes with a focus on logistics, acquisitions, service, corporate and construction disputes.
(https://sccarbitrationinstitute.se/en/resource-library/rules-and-policies/scc-rules)
16. Swiss Chambers Arbitration Institution (SCAI):
Around 80% of the cases handled by the SCAI are international with the main areas of dispute being sale of goods and services
contracts in banking and finance, commodity mining and manufacturing.
(https://www.swissarbitration.org/centre/arbitration/arbitration-rules/)
