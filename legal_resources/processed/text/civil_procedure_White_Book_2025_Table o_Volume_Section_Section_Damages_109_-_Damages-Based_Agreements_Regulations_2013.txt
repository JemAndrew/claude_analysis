Section 7A - Litigation Funding after 1 April 2013
Damages-Based Agreements Regulations 2013
Regulation 1
(SI 2013/609)
Introductory note
7A-27. 0 Damages-Based Agreements (DBAs) constitute a significant change to the scope of available forms of “conditional” (or in this
instance effectively “contingent”) fee agreements between lawyers and clients. These Regulations prescribe the requirements
with which a DBA must comply in order to be enforceable under s.58AA of the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990.
The 2013 Regulations came into effect on 1 April 2013, and, as reg.2 states, revoke (subject to transitional provision) the
2010 Regulations. Whereas the 2010 Regulations were limited to employment matters, the 2013 Regulations apply to all civil
litigation cases subject to some exceptions. Under a DBA the lawyer is entitled to receive from the client, in the event of success,
a specific percentage of the damages recovered. The Regulations apply to DBAs entered into on or after the date of coming
into force of the Regulations.
Under the Regulations DBA’s are NOT available for cases to which s.57 of the Solicitor’s Act 1975 applies (reg.1(4)), i.e. non-
contentious business agreements between solicitor and client.
