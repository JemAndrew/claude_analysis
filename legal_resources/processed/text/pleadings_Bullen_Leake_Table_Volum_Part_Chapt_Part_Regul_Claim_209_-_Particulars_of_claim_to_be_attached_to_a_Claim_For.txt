20-F24
The Defendant was at all material times a motor dealer.
2.
In or about [date] the Defendant was offering for sale [identify the goods] (“the goods”).
3.
On [date] PQ made enquiries about the goods and the Defendant orally represented to him [set out representations relied
on].
4.
Acting in reliance on these representations, on [date] PQ entered into a written agreement with the Defendant (“the sale
agreement”) whereby the Defendant sold the goods to PQ for the sum of £[..........]. A copy of the sale agreement is annexed
to this Claim Form.
5.
Pursuant to s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979, there were terms of the sale agreement that:
 
(a)the goods would be reasonably fit for the purposes of PQ, including [specify any particular purpose];
 
(b)the goods should be of satisfactory quality.
[Or, when in force]
5.
Under s.9 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015, it was a term of the sale agreement that the quality of the goods should be
satisfactory [and under s.10 that the goods should be reasonably fit for purpose].

6.
By an agreement in writing dated [address] (“the loan agreement”) the Claimant advanced the sum of £[..........] to PQ for

10.

11.

12.
By reason of s.75(2) the Defendant is liable to indemnify the Claimant in respect of the said sums totalling £[..........].
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)£[..........].
 
(2)Interest pursuant to s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984 at the rate of x% from [date] to the date hereof £[..........]

[Statement of truth]
Note
CCA s.75 applies where the cash price of the goods or services bought with the credit is below £100 or above £30,000. When
the sum is between £30,000 and £60,260 there is, for agreements after 1 February 2011, an analogous right under s.75A to
pursue the lender for the same remedy as is available against the supplier. Section 75A, however, only applies where (for any
of four reasons) no realistic relief can be obtained against the supplier. Section 75A thus contains no express right of indemnity
