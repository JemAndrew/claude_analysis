Order for an Injunction—Important—Penal
Notice to the Defendant
This Order prohibits you from doing the acts set out in this Order. You should read it carefully. You are advised to consult
a Solicitor as soon as possible. You have a right to ask the Court to vary or discharge this Order.
If you the within named Defendant, [Company B], disobey this Order you may be held to be in contempt of court and may
be imprisoned or fined or have your assets seized.
Any other person who knows of this order and does anything which helps or permits the Defendant to breach the terms of
this order may also be held to be in contempt of court and may be imprisoned, fined or have their assets seized.

IT IS DECLARED that until a further hearing in respect of this order on [date] (“the Return Date”) or further order or
declaration of the court or contrary order or declaration of the arbitral tribunal constituted in accordance with the arbitration
agreement in clause [e.g. 28] of the [e.g. Share Sale and Purchase Agreement dated [date] (“the SSPA”)] the Defendant
cannot and could not bring:
 
(i)the claims that are the subject matter of the proceedings commenced by the Defendant against the Claimant in the
[Country] on [date], case No. [---] (“the [Country] Proceedings”); and
 
(ii)[e.g. “any and all disputes, controversies or claims arising under or in connection with the SSPA” or other
formulation used in the relevant arbitration agreement],
otherwise than by commencing arbitration proceedings in London in accordance with clause 28 of the SSPA.
AND IT IS ORDERED that:
1.
The Return Date shall be [date] on which there will be a further hearing in respect of this order.
2.
Pursuant to section 37 of the Senior Courts Act 1981 the Defendant, whether by itself, its directors, officers, employees,
agents or otherwise, be restrained, until the Return Date or further order of the court or contrary order of the arbitral tribunal
constituted in accordance with the arbitration agreement in clause 28 of the SSPA, from:
 
2.1further pursuing, or taking any further steps in, or procuring or assisting in the pursuit of or the taking of steps in,
the [Country] Proceedings to pursue its substantive claims in those proceedings, save for the purposes of dismissing,
withdrawing and/or otherwise discontinuing the said [Country] Proceedings against the Claimant; or
 
2.2commencing or pursuing any other substantive claim or substantive proceedings arising under or in connection
with the SSPA, otherwise than by arbitration in London in accordance with clause 28 of the SSPA.
3.
The Claimant may serve on the Defendant at: [address], the claim form, its supporting evidence, the response pack
(consisting of Forms N8B and N15), this Order, and any other documents requiring service on the Defendant under the
Civil Procedure Rules in regard to this claim.
4.
The Defendant has [number] days after service of the Claim Form in which to respond by filing and serving an
acknowledgment of service.
5.
The Defendant has the right to apply to have this Order set aside or varied, but if it wishes to do so it must firm inform the
Claimant’s solicitors in writing at least 48 hours beforehand. If any evidence is relied upon in support of such application,
the substance of it must be communicated in writing to the Claimant’s solicitors in advance.
