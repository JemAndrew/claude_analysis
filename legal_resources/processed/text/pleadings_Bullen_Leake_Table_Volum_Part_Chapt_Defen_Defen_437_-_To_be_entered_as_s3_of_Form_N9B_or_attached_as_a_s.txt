Defences Based on Loss of Title
Defence of purchase from buyer in possession
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9B or attached as a separate document
1.
26-F24
It is admitted that the Claimant agreed to sell the goods to X under the agreement referred to in paragraph 1 of the Particulars
of Claim and that, upon sale, no title in the goods passed to X.
2.
On a date unknown to the Defendant but after the date of the said agreement and with the Claimant’s consent, X obtained
possession of the goods.
3.
By an oral agreement made on [date], X sold and delivered the goods to the Defendant who received them in good faith
and without notice or any lien or other right of the Claimant in respect of the goods.
4.
In the premises, the Defendant obtained good title to the goods under s.26(1) of the Sale of Goods Act, 1979.
