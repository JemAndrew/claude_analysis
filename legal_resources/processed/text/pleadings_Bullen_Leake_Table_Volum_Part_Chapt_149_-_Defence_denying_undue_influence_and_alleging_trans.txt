Defence denying undue influence and alleging transaction brought about by independent exercise of will by the
claimant
1.
18-E7
The Defendant admits that the Claimant transferred to him (“the transfers”) on or about [date] the property set out in
Schedule 1 to the Particulars of Claim.
2.
It is denied that the transfers were procured by the undue influence of the Defendant. The Defendant did not at any time
make threats or behave in an abusive manner, as is alleged in the Particulars of Claim. The Defendant did not in fact
see the Claimant execute the deeds of transfer in relation to the properties as he was away on holiday in Bermuda at the
relevant time.
3.
The Claimant was at all times advised, independently, by his own solicitors in relation to the transfers, who met with him
on three occasions prior to the execution of the deeds of transfer.
4.
In those circumstances, the Claimant executed the deeds of transfer exercising his own free will in doing so, and after he
had had the benefit of independent legal advice and the Claimant is not entitled to set aside the transfers.
