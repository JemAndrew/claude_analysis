Claim by payee of cheque against the drawer
1.
10-E16
The Defendant drew and delivered to the Claimant a cheque dated [date] drawn upon the ABC Bank Plc in the sum of
£1,000 payable to the Claimant. A copy of the cheque is attached and marked “A”.
2.
Upon due presentation of the cheque for payment it was dishonoured [and returned marked refer to drawer/payment
countermanded, etc.].
3.
Due notice of dishonour was given to the Defendant by the Claimant’s letter dated [date] a copy of which is attached and
marked (B). or
In those circumstances, since [ABC Bank Plc was under no obligation to honour the cheque or payment of the cheque was
countermanded by the Defendant] notice of dishonour was dispensed with.
4.
The cheque remains unpaid.
5.
The Claimant is entitled to and claims:
 
(1)payment of the sum of £1,000, together with
 
(2)interest pursuant to s.57(1) of the Bills of Exchange Act 1882 or alternatively pursuant to s.35A of the Senior
Courts Act 1981 at the rate of [..........] per cent per annum until judgment or sooner payment amounting to £[..........]
at the date hereof and continuing at a rate of £[..........] per day, or alternatively at such rate and for such time as to
the Court shall seem appropriate.
