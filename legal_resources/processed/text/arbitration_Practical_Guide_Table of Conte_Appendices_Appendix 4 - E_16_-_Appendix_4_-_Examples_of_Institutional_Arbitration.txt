Appendix 4 - Examples of Institutional Arbitration Clauses
Introduction
A4-001 Many institutions, including UNCITRAL, CIArb, CIETAC, DIAC, HKIAC, ICC, ICDR/AAA, ICSID, LCIA, PCA, SIAC,
SCC, and SCAI, follow a similar overarching framework for their model arbitration clauses. They generally include provisions
addressing the scope of disputes covered, the governing rules (either their own or another recognised set), the tribunal’s
composition, the arbitration’s location, and the language of the proceedings.
Most arbitration clauses share a core structure that emphasises flexibility, party autonomy and a comprehensive scope for dispute
resolution. However, nuances like the ICSID’s focus on investment disputes, the ICC’s provisions for excluding emergency
procedures, and the PCA’s waiver of recourse option, underscore the importance of selecting an arbitration institution that
aligns with the parties’ specific needs and expectations. The clauses from these various institutions illustrate the international
arbitration community’s efforts to provide adaptable, efficient and effective frameworks for resolving disputes across diverse
legal and commercial landscapes.
Almost all institutions specify that the parties can decide on the seat (legal place) of arbitration and the language of the arbitration
proceedings. This flexibility is a common theme, aimed at accommodating international parties’ diverse needs.
A recurring option across various institutions allows parties to choose between one or three arbitrators, with specific provisions
for their appointment in the absence of agreement or in case of a deadlock.
Whilst most institutions base their clauses on a standard approach, some contain distinguishing features:
 
• The CIArb and ICC clauses include specific mention of emergency arbitrator rules and expedited procedure provisions,
reflecting a trend towards accommodating urgent interim relief and faster resolution in certain cases.
 
• The ICSID clauses are uniquely tailored for investment disputes involving a state or state entity and an investor, reflecting
ICSID’s specialised role in this area.
 
• The ICC’s option to expressly exclude emergency arbitrator provisions stands out as a specific contractual choice not
explicitly mentioned by most other institutions, although most institutions would allow it.
 
• The PCA model clause makes a point of including a provision allowing parties to waive their right to any form of recourse
against an award to the extent permissible by applicable law. This provision is designed to limit post-arbitration litigation,
emphasising finality.
 
• The SIAC clause mentions the possibility of court proceedings in relation to the arbitration being commenced before
the Singapore International Commercial Court (SICC), showing a notable integration with Singapore’s legal system. This
contrasts with other significant institutions that operate neutrally with respect to their own national legal system.

2. CIArb – Model Arbitration Clause
(a) Model arbitration clauses for contracts
 
A4-003
1.Model arbitration clause with emergency arbitrator rules:
“Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this contract, or the breach,
termination or validity thereof, shall be submitted to the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (CIArb)
and settled by final and binding arbitration in accordance with the CIArb Arbitration Rules. Judgment
on any award issued under this provision may be entered by any court of competent jurisdiction.” 2
 
2.Model arbitration clause excluding the emergency arbitrator rules:
“Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this contract, or the breach,
termination or validity thereof, shall be submitted to the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (CIArb)
and settled by final and binding arbitration in accordance with the CIArb Arbitration Rules, except
that the emergency arbitrator rules shall not apply. Judgment on any award issued under this provision
may be entered by any court of competent jurisdiction.”

8. International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) 8
Standard ICC Arbitration
A4-015
“All disputes arising out of or in connection with the present contract shall be finally settled under the
Rules of Arbitration of the International Chamber of Commerce by one or more arbitrators appointed in
accordance with the said Rules.” 9
Parties are free to adapt the clause to their particular circumstances. For instance, they may wish to stipulate the number of
arbitrators, given that the ICC Arbitration Rules contain a presumption in favour of a sole arbitrator. Also, it may be desirable
for them to stipulate the place and language of the arbitration and the law applicable to the merits. The ICC Arbitration Rules
do not limit the parties’ free choice of the place and language of the arbitration or the law governing the contract.
ICC Arbitration Without Emergency Arbitrator
A4-016
If the parties wish to exclude any recourse to the Emergency Arbitrator Provisions, they must expressly opt out by adding
the following wording to the clause above:
“The Emergency Arbitrator Provisions shall not apply.”

Expedited Arbitration
A4-017
The ICC Arbitration Rules provide for use of an expedited procedure in lower-value cases. If parties wish to exclude the
application of the Expedited Procedure Provisions, they must expressly opt out by adding the following wording to the clause
above:
“The Expedited Procedure Provisions shall not apply.”
Parties wishing to avail themselves of the expedited procedure in higher-value cases should expressly opt in by adding the
following wording to the clause above:
“The parties agree, pursuant to Article 30(2)(b) of the Rules of Arbitration of the International Chamber
of Commerce, that the Expedited Procedure Rules shall apply irrespective of the amount in dispute.”
If parties wish the ceiling for the application of the Expedited Procedure Rules to be higher than that specified in those Rules,
the following wording should be added to the clause above:
“The parties agree, pursuant to Article 30(2)(b) of the Rules of Arbitration of the International Chamber
of Commerce, that the Expedited Procedure Rules shall apply, provided the amount in dispute does not
exceed US$ [specify amount] at the time of the communication referred to in Article 1(3) of the Expedited
Procedure Rules.”
Standard ICC Arbitration Clause Without Publication of Awards
A4-018
“All disputes arising out of or in connection with the present contract shall be finally settled under the
Rules of Arbitration of the International Chamber of Commerce by one or more arbitrators appointed in
accordance with the said Rules. No award or procedural order made in the arbitration shall be published.”

“Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of, or in relation to, this contract, including regarding the
validity, invalidity, breach, or termination thereof, shall be resolved by arbitration in accordance with the
Swiss Rules of International Arbitration of the Swiss Arbitration Centre in force on the date on which the
Notice of Arbitration is submitted in accordance with those Rules. 17
The number of arbitrators shall be… (“one”,” three”, “one or three”);
The seat of the arbitration shall be… (name of city in Switzerland, unless the parties agree on a city in
another country);
The arbitral proceedings shall be conducted in… (insert desired language).”
