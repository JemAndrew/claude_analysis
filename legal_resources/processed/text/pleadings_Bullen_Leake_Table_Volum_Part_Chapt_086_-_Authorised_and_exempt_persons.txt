Authorised and exempt persons
12-07
Authorised persons under FSMA are divided into: (i) persons who have permission from the FCA or PRA to carry on one or
more regulated activities 120 ; (ii) persons that are authorised in another EEA State and set up branches in the UK or provide
service under a “passport” derived from a European single market directive 121 ; (iii) EEA firms exercising rights under the
Treaty of Rome 122 ; and (iv) persons otherwise authorised by or under FSMA (for example, the Society of Lloyd’s is treated
as an authorised person (FSMA s.314A)).
Exempt persons include those specified in an order made by the Treasury. 123  This order broadly exempts four main categories:
 
(i)those exempted for all regulated activities except insurance business: it includes the Bank of England and other central
banks of EEA States, the EU, the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development, and the International Monetary
Fund 124 ;
 
(ii)those exempted only in respect of the receipt of deposits: it includes local authorities, charities (subject to certain
restrictions) and industrial and provident societies, in so far as they accept deposits in the form of withdrawable share
capital;
 
(iii)those exempted in respect of investment business: it includes Tourist Boards and persons acting as an official receiver
within the meaning of s.399 of the Insolvency Act 1986; and
 
(iv)those exempted for specified activities: it includes registered social landlords arranging regulated mortgages and
enterprise schemes arranging deals in investments.
Authorisation and exemption are mutually exclusive: the permission to conduct regulated activities under Pt 4A is not superseded
merely because the person in question has exemption under these provisions. 125  For other categories of “exempt person”, see
s.39 (appointed representatives), s.285(2) FSMA (recognised investment exchanges) s.285(3) (recognised clearing houses) and
s.327 (professional firms).
