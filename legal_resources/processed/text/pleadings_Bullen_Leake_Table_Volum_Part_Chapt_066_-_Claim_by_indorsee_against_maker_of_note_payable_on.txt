Claim by indorsee against maker of note payable on fixed date with interest
1.
10-E24
On 31 January 2015 the Defendant made and delivered to AB a promissory note (“the Note”) in the sum of £1,000 payable
with interest at a rate of 2 per cent per month on 31 March 2000. A copy of the Note is attached marked “A”.
2.
On 20 February 2015 AB negotiated the Note to the Claimant, who accepted it in discharge of a debt due from AB to the
Claimant and thereby gave value for it. In the premises the Claimant became a holder in due course of the Note.
3.
On 31 March 2015 the Claimant presented the Note to the Defendant for payment but it was dishonoured, and it remains
unpaid.
4.
In the circumstances the Claimant is entitled to and claims:
 
(1)The amount of the Note being £1,000 together with,
 
(2)interest as provided in it at a rate of 2 per cent per month for [..........] months amounting to £[..........] at the date
hereof.
5.
The Claimant claims continuing interest pursuant to s.57(1) of the Bills of Exchange Act 1882 at the rate of 2 per cent per
month; or alternatively pursuant to s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981 at the same rate, equivalent to a rate of £[..........]
per day.
