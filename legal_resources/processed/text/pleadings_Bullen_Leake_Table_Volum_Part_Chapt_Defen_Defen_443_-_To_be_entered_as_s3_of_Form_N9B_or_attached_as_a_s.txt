Chapter 26 - Title to Goods—Torts Against Goods
Defences Asserting a Right Against the Owner
Defence of trespass by the claimant
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9B or attached as a separate document
1.
26-F30
The Defendant is the owner and entitled to possession of [identify property].
2.
On [date], the Claimant, without the Defendant’s licence or consent, trespassed upon the Defendant’s land and deposited
certain property there, including the goods referred to in paragraph 2 of the Particulars of Claim.
3.
On various occasions between [date] and [date], the Defendant spoke to the Claimant and required him to remove his
property from the Defendant’s land but the Claimant failed or refused to do so, whereupon the Defendant was entitled
to remove it himself.
4.
On [date], the Defendant lawfully removed the Claimant’s goods to open land adjacent to the entrance of the Defendant’s
property and left them there.
5.
By reason of the Claimant’s trespass, the Defendant is not liable to the Claimant for the loss and destruction of his property
as alleged or at all.
