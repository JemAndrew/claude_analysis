Claim for specific performance of obligations to repair or replace
Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
25-F22
On or about [date] the Claimant entered into an oral agreement with the Defendant at the Defendant’s premises at [address]
whereby the Claimant bought [set out the goods bought] for a price of £[..........].
2.
The Claimant bought the goods as a consumer.
3.
Under s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979 (“the 1979 Act”), it was an implied term of the agreement that the goods should
be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Claimant required it and should be of satisfactory quality.
Or
3.
. Under s.9 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (“the 2015 Act”), it was a term of the agreement that the quality of the
goods should be satisfactory and under s.10, that they should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Claimant
required them.
4.
In breach of the agreement, the goods were not reasonably fit for the purpose for which they were required nor were they
of satisfactory quality.
Particulars
 
(a)[Identify the matters relied on].
5.
Under [ss.48A and 48B of the 1979 Act] [ss.19 and 23 of the 2015 Act], the Claimant was entitled to require the Defendant
to repair or replace the goods.

6.
By a letter dated [date], a copy of which is attached to these Particulars of Claim, the Claimant required the Defendant
to replace the goods.
Or
6.
By a letter dated [date], a copy of which is attached to these Particulars of Claim, the Claimant required the Defendant
to carry out [the following] repairs to the goods:
 
(a)[specify any particular repair requested].
7.
Accordingly it was the Defendant’s obligation to [replace] [repair] within a reasonable time and without causing any
inconvenience to the Claimant and to bear any necessary costs incurred in doing so. The Claimant will say that a reasonable
time would have been [set out time claimed].
8.
In breach of that obligation, the Defendant has failed to [replace the goods] [carry out the said or any repairs] within a
reasonable time or at all.
Or
8.
In breach of that obligation, in a telephone conversation on [date] the Defendant orally [refused to replace the goods unless
the Claimant transported them to the Defendant’s warehouse in [address] at his own expense] [refused to repair the goods
unless the Claimant agreed to pay for parts and labour].
9.
In the circumstances, the Claimant is entitled to an order requiring the Defendant to [replace] [repair] the goods.
10.
In the alternative, the Claimant will seek rescission of the agreement and repayment of the purchase price.
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)An order that the Defendant does replace the goods with goods of an identical description.
[alternatively]
 
(1)An order that the Defendant does [repair the goods] [carry out such repairs to the goods as are set out in the schedule
to the order]
 
(2)Alternatively to (1) rescission of the agreement and repayment of the purchase price of £[..........].
 
(3)Interest under [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981] [s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984] from [date] to judgment.
