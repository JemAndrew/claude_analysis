Defence denying notice of dishonour
1.
10-E7
It is admitted that the Defendant drew the bill relied on by the Claimant.
2.
The Defendant is unable to admit or deny whether the bill was duly presented, or was paid, or dishonoured.
3.
It is denied that any notice of such dishonour was given to the Defendant, nor were there circumstances dispensing with
the need for such notice, nor was any such requirement waived by the Defendant.
4.
In the circumstances if the bill was dishonoured the Defendant has nonetheless been discharged, and is not liable as alleged
or otherwise.
