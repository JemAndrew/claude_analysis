Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
Article 25 Establishing the Facts of the Case
1.
The arbitral tribunal shall proceed within as short a time as possible to establish the facts of the case by all appropriate
means.
2.
The arbitral tribunal may decide to hear witnesses, experts appointed by the parties or any other person, in the presence of
the parties, or in their absence provided they have been duly summoned.
3.
The arbitral tribunal, after consulting the parties, may appoint one or more experts, define their terms of reference and
receive their reports. At the request of a party, the parties shall be given the opportunity to question at a hearing any such
expert.
4.
At any time during the proceedings, the arbitral tribunal may summon any party to provide additional evidence.
5.
The arbitral tribunal may decide the case solely on the documents submitted by the parties unless any of the parties requests
a hearing.
