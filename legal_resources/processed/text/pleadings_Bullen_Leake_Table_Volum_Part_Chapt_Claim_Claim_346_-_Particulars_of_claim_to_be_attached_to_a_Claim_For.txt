Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
25-F16
The Claimant carries on the business of [identify business] and the Defendant as a manufacturer of [identify the product].
Or
1.
The Claimant is a consumer for the purposes of the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (‘the CRA’) and the Defendant, who carries
on business as a manufacturer of [identify the product] is a trader.
2.
By an agreement made between the Claimant and the Defendant on [date], the Defendant agreed to sell [identify the goods
sold] (“the goods”) at a price of £[..........].
3.
The agreement was contained in the Claimant’s faxed order of [date] and the Defendant’s faxed letter of acceptance of
[date]. Copies of those documents are annexed to these Particulars of Claim.
4.
There were implied warranties of the agreement that:
 
(a)the goods were and should remain free from any undisclosed encumbrance;
 
(b)the Claimant should enjoy quiet possession of the goods.
These warranties are to be implied by reason of the Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.12(2), (4) and (5)] [CRA s.17].
5.
In pursuance of the agreement, the Claimant paid the Defendant the price of £[..........] and on [date] the Defendant delivered
the goods to the Claimant.

6.
In breach of the warranties, the goods were not free from undisclosed encumbrances and the Claimant did not enjoy quiet
possession of them.
Particulars
 
(a)The goods were subject to a fixed charge by way of debenture granted to ZZ Bank Plc (“the bank”) to secure the
Defendant’s indebtedness to the bank.
 
(b)On [date] the bank demanded payment of all sums due from the Defendant and sought to exercise its charge.
 
(c)On [date] the bank commenced proceedings against the Claimant in the [..........] Court, seeking delivery up of the
goods and on [date] was granted an injunction against the Claimant, restraining the Claimant from disposing of them.
 
(d)On [date] the Claimant negotiated a settlement of the proceedings with the bank whereby, in consideration of a
payment of £[..........] (to include the bank’s costs), the bank agreed to discontinue the action, to consent to the discharge
of the injunction and to release the goods from the charge. The Claimant reasonably incurred costs of £[..........] in
respect of the proceedings.
7.
By reason of the Defendant’s breaches of warranty, the Claimant has suffered loss and damage.
Particulars
 
(a)Payment to the bank £[..........].
 
(b)The Claimant’s costs of the bank proceedings £[..........].
 
(c)Loss of profit on the goods whose retail price had fallen between the date of the injunction and the date of the
settlement with the bank: £[..........].
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)Damages.
 
(2)Interest under [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981] [s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984] at the rate of x% until
judgment.
