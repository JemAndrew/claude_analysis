Section 7 - Legal Representatives—Costs and Litigation Funding
Section 7C - The Solicitors Act 1974
Solicitors’ (Non Contentious Business) Remuneration Order 1994
13.— Refunds by solicitor
7C-206+
(1) If a solicitor has received payment of all or part of his costs and a remuneration certificate is issued for less than the
sum already paid, the solicitor must immediately pay to the entitled person any refund which may be due (after taking
into account any other sums which may properly be payable to the solicitor whether for costs, paid disbursements,
value added tax or otherwise) unless the solicitor has applied for an order for taxation within one month of receipt
by him of the remuneration certificate.
(2) Where a solicitor applies for taxation, his liability to pay any refund under paragraph (1) shall be suspended for
so long as the taxation is still pending.
(3) The obligation of the solicitor to repay costs under paragraph (1) is without prejudice to any liability of the solicitor
to pay interest on the repayment by virtue of any enactment, rule of law or professional rule.
