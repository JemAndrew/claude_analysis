Defence denying dishonour by non-acceptance
1.
10-E6
It is denied that the bill was dishonoured by non-acceptance as alleged, or in any way, for these reasons:
 
(i)It is admitted that on 14 March 2000 the Claimant purport to present the bill for acceptance to AB Ltd upon whom
the bill was drawn.
 
(ii)The Claimant purported to do so by presenting it at 6.30 p.m. to AB Ltd’s reception, which was then staffed by a
temporary receptionist, all of the management of AB Ltd having left for the evening. She was not prepared to sign
the bill, which was not left with her but taken away by the Claimant.
 
(iii)That was not presentment at a reasonable hour on a business day, nor was it to a person authorised to accept or
refuse acceptance on behalf of AB Ltd and in each of those respects s.41(a) of the Bills of Exchange Act 1882 was
not complied with.
 
(iv)Further and in any event, upon proper presentment of the bill on 18 March 2000, AB Ltd did accept the bill, which
is not yet due for payment.
2.
The bill is not yet due for payment. It is therefore denied that the Defendant is liable upon the bill as alleged or at all.
