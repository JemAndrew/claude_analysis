Surety’s Defences
Defence of withdrawal from or cancellation of the principal agreement
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
27-F14
The Defendant admits that he entered into the contract of guarantee set out in the Particulars of Claim (“the guarantee”),
whereby he guaranteed the liabilities of XY to the Claimant in respect of an agreement made between the Claimant and
XY dated [date] (“the principal agreement”).
2.
The Defendant admits receipt of the letter of demand dated [date] referred to in the Particulars of Claim. For the reasons
set out below, he is not liable to the Claimant in the sum claimed or any sum.
3.
The principal agreement was a consumer credit agreement regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”).
Pursuant to CCA s.66A, the agreement was subject to a right of the debtor to withdraw from the agreement within 14 days
of the relevant day (such day being [date]).
4.
Within 14 days of the relevant day on [date] XY gave [oral] [written] notice of withdrawal from the agreement to [name]
being the person designated in the agreement for the purpose of such notice.
5.
Following the giving of this notice, under CCA s.66A(7) the principal agreement is to be treated as if it had never been
entered into.
Alternatively
3.
The agreement was concluded at the home of XY at [address] and was an agreement to which the [Cancellation of Contracts
made in a Consumer’s Home or Place of Work etc Regulations 2008 (“the 2008 Regulations”)] [now by the Consumer
Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (“the 2013 Regulations”) applied.
