Claim for repayment of money (or return of property) procured by undue Influence
1.
18-E1
The Claimant is aged 78 years and is the father of the Defendant.
2.
On or about [date], the Claimant paid to the Defendant the sum of £[..........] [or transferred to the Defendant the property
set out in Schedule 1 to this statement of case]. A copy of the bank transfer form/deed of transfer 62  is attached to the
Particulars of Claim and marked “A”.
3.
The payment [or transfer of property] by the Claimant was procured by the undue influence of the Defendant over the
Claimant.
Particulars of undue influence
[Set out in full detail, in short and simple sentences the influence exerted on the Claimant and why in law it can be said
to be undue and that the Claimant had no means to resist the influence.]
 
(i)The Defendant is the son of the Claimant.
 
(ii)On or about [date] the Defendant arrived at the Claimant’s home with a bundle of papers which he told the Claimant
to sign.
 
(iii)The Claimant, who was then 76 years old, partially sighted and suffering from chronic heart disease, was unwilling
to sign the papers (which included the payment transfer form [deed of transfer over the property] and asked his son,
the Defendant to explain the significance of the papers to him.
 
(iv)When it became clear to the Defendant that the Claimant was unwilling to sign the payment transfer form [deed
of transfer over the property] the Defendant became threatening and abusive and told the Claimant that he would not
leave the house until the Claimant signed the document making the payment [transfer of property].
 
(v)Further, the Defendant threatened that the Claimant would be denied access to his granddaughter (the Defendant’s
daughter) unless he signed the payment transfer form [deed of transfer over the property]).
 
(vi)The Claimant was alone in the house and had no opportunity to consult an independent legal adviser; further, the
Claimant feared for his own state of health.

4.
The undue influence of the Defendant caused the Claimant to sign the payment transfer form [deed of transfer over the
property].
5.
The Claimant is entitled to and does claim repayment by the Defendant of the sum of £[..........] together with interest
on that sum at the rate of [..........] per cent per annum [compounded monthly] by way of equitable relief amounting to
£[..........] at [date of claim] and continuing at a daily rate of £[..........].
AND the Claimant claims:
 
(1)A declaration that the payment [transfer of property] ought to be rescinded/set aside as having been procured by
undue influence of the Defendant over the Claimant.
 
(2)An order that the Defendant do deliver up the payment transfer order [deed of transfer] to be cancelled forthwith.
 
(3)Repayment of the sum of £[..........] together with interest on that sum at the rate of [..........] per cent per annum
[compounded monthly] by way of equitable relief amounting to £[..........] at [date of claim] and continuing at a daily
rate of £[..........] under paragraph [..........] above.
 
[(4)An order that the Defendant reconvey the property listed in Schedule 1 to the Claimant.
 
(5)An account of the dealings by the Defendant with the property listed in Schedule 1 and of the income received by
the Defendant from that property and payment to the Claimant of all sums found due to the Claimant as a result of
the taking of the account plus interest as claimed in paragraph (3) above.
 
(6)All further accounts directions and inquiries as may be necessary.]
