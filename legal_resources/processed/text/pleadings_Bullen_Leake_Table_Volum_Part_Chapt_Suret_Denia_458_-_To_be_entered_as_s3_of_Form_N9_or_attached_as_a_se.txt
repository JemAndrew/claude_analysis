Surety’s Defences
Denial of default by principal debtor
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
Defence
1.
27-F7
The Defendant admits that XY Limited entered into the loan contract and the Defendant entered into the contract of
guarantee which contracts are set out in the Particulars of Claim and annexed to the Claim Form.
2.
It was a term of the loan contract that the loan should be repayable by XY Limited to the Claimant on demand.
3.
The Defendant admits that by the letter dated [date] annexed to the Claim Form, the Claimant demanded payment from
the Defendant pursuant to the guarantee of the sum of £[..........] alleged to be due from XY Limited.
4.
At the date of the said demand upon the Defendant, XY Limited was not liable to pay any sum to the Claimant, no demand
for repayment having been made by the Claimant upon XY Limited.
5.
In the premises no sum was or is due from the Defendant under the guarantee and the Defendant is not liable to the Claimant
in the sum claimed or any sum.
