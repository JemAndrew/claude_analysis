Rule 44_3a
44.3A— Costs orders relating to funding arrangements 1
7B-11+
(1) The court will not assess any additional liability until the conclusion of the proceedings, or the part of the
proceedings, to which the funding arrangement relates.
(“Funding arrangement” and “additional liability” are defined in rule 43.2.)
(2) At the conclusion of the proceedings, or the part of the proceedings, to which the funding arrangement relates
the court may—
(a) make a summary assessment of all the costs, including any additional liability;
(b) make an order for detailed assessment of the additional liability but make a summary assessment of the other
costs; or
(c) make an order for detailed assessment of all the costs.
(Part 47 sets out the procedure for the detailed assessment of costs)
Effect of rule
7B-11.
1+
This rule and rr.44.3B, 44.15, 44.16 and 44.17, were inserted in Part 44 by the Civil Procedure (Amendment No.3) Rules
2000 (SI 2000/1317) when, after the bringing into force of the Access to Justice Act 1999, new funding arrangements for
legal proceedings became possible (see para.7B-6+) and, for the purposes of regulating proceedings where the new funding
arrangements applied, and providing for the assessment of costs in such proceedings, additional rules of court were required.
SI 2000/1317 came into effect on 3 July 2000, subject to transitional provisions.
For meaning of “summary assessment” and “detailed assessment”, see r.43.3 (at para.7B-10+) and r.44.4 (at para.7B-13+).
As explained above (para.7B-6+), r.44.3A is a provision “relating to funding arrangements” and applies in relation to “a pre-
commencement funding arrangement” as it was in force immediately before 1 April 2013. That rule is supplemented by Section
9 of the Costs Practice Direction (see para.7BPD-3+).

“any additional liability”
7B-11.
2+
The additional liability may consist of any “percentage increase” (for calculation of a “success fee”) and any “insurance
premium” (as defined, respectively in paras (l) and (m) in r.43.2(1)) to which a funding arrangement relates; see paras 7B-2+,
7B-10.3+ and 7B-55+.
Summary or detailed assessment
7B-11.
3+
Because it was felt that disclosure of full details of funding arrangements, particularly the percentage success fee in a conditional
fee agreement, was prejudicial, the rules provide for limited information to be given to opposing parties until the final assessment
(summary or detailed) is made (see r.44.15 and Section 19 of the Costs Practice Direction (see para.7BPD-5+; note also Practice
Direction (Pre-Action Conduct) para.9.3). As to disclosure on detailed assessment, see commentary following r.47.14 (Detailed
assessment hearing)). Rule 44.3A(1) states that the court will not assess any additional liability until the conclusion of the
relevant part of the proceedings. At that point the court may carry out a summary assessment of all the costs, make a summary
assessment of the base costs only and order a detailed assessment of the additional liability, or make an order for detailed
assessment of all the costs (r.44.3A(2)). In Burton v Kingsley [2005] EWHC 1034 (QB), [2005] P.I.Q.R. P2 (Richards J) the
judge summarily assessed the additional liability (a success fee under a CFA) and ordered detailed assessment of the claimant’s
costs in relation to liability.
When the court makes a summary assessment during the course of the proceedings the judge should state separately the amount
allowed in respect of solicitors’ charges, counsels’ fees and other disbursements. This is so even though any additional liability
is not at that stage assessed. The reason for this is that when the final assessment takes place it will be necessary to identify
the total figures allowed to solicitors and counsel in order that any percentage increase (which may be different for solicitors
and counsel) can be applied.
