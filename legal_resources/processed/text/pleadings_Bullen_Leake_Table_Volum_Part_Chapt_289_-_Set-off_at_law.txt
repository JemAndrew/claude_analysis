Set-off at law
24-02
Set-off was the product of the Statutes of Set-off (1729 and 1735) under the provisions of which mutual debts, due and payable,
but arising from different transactions could be set off one against the other in extinction or diminution of the amounts due. The
debt had to be capable of being ascertained with certainty thereby disentitling a person from raising a claim in damages as this
was unliquidated or too uncertain. This restriction no longer applies. 4
