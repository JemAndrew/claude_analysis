Regulated agreements: events after the making of the contract
Defence under s.93 of the CCA
To be inserted in s.4 of Form 9B or 9D or contained in a separate document
1.
20-F64
The Defendant admits he entered into the agreement referred to in the Particulars of Claim. The agreement was an
agreement regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”).
2.
The Defendant does not admit that he was ever in breach of the agreement or that there were ever sums due and unpaid
under the agreement whether as alleged in the Particulars of Claim or at all. Alternatively if there were any sums due and
unpaid under the agreement, the Defendant avers that all such sums have now been paid to the Claimant.
3.
Clause 5 of the agreement provided for the Defendant to pay interest on sums in arrears at the rate of 25% per annum
whereas the APR stated in the agreement was 11% per annum. The Defendant has paid to the Claimant interest at the
rate of 11% per annum on all sums claimed to be overdue and the Claimant’s present claim relates to the balance of the
interest claimed at 25%.
4.
By reason of s.93 of the CCA, the Claimant is not entitled to claim any interest upon default at a rate greater than that
charged in the agreement. In the premises the Claimant is not entitled to maintain an action for interest at a higher rate
than the stated APR of 11%.
