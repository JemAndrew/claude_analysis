When the “Consumer Credit Act Procedure” (“CCA Procedure”) applies
Category 8: Sureties, assignees and personal representatives
20-50
For sureties, the basic rule is contained in s.141(5) that all parties to a regulated agreement must be joined as parties to any
proceedings relating to the agreement unless the court orders otherwise. Under CPR PD 7B.9.1 the court may dispense with
this requirement if the claim form has not been served on the debtor or the surety, and the claimant makes an application before
or at the hearing (which can be made with or without notice) to make such an order.
For claims by assignees, the CPR PD 7B.9.2 reverses the rule in s.141(5) by providing that where the claimant was not one of
the original parties to the agreement but the former creditor’s rights have passed to him by assignment or operation of law, the
former creditor does not have to be joined as a party under s.141(5) unless the court otherwise orders.
For claims against personal representatives, where a creditor or owner makes a claim for an order under s.86(2) following the
death of a debtor or hirer, under CPR PD 7B.9.3 and 9.4 the personal representatives must be made a party unless no grant of
representation has been made in which case the claimant must make an application for directions under CPR Pt.23 as to who
is to be made a party to the Action.
