Defence alleging renunciation in writing
1.
10-E15
It is admitted that the Defendant signed and indorsed the bill sued upon, which was indorsed by him to AB. 81
2.
Thereafter, and while AB was the holder of the bill, AB by a letter dated [date], attached marked “A”, absolutely and
unconditionally renounced the liability of the Defendant upon the bill.
3.
The bill was subsequently indorsed to the Claimant after it was overdue, and in the circumstances the Claimant is not a
holder in due course.
4.
It is therefore denied that the Defendant is liable to the Claimant upon the bill as alleged or at all.
Footnotes
81
As to the renunciation of liability on a bill, see s.62 of the Act, and see also s.63 as to the cancellation of a bill.
