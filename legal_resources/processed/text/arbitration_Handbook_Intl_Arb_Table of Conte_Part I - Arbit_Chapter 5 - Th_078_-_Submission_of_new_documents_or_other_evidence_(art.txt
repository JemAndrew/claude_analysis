Part I - Arbitration Rules
Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
Submission of new documents or other evidence (art.25)
22-95
Normally, the procedural timetable provides that all documents shall be submitted with the respective filings or that there is
a cut-off date by which documents and other evidence shall be filed. Some criticise the tendency of Tribunal to accept such
documents due to concern about due process. 276
22-96
In the authors’ experience, there frequently are submissions of documents late in the proceedings and Tribunals tend to accept
them (while providing the other party with an opportunity to respond), provided that there is an adequate explanation as to
why the documents were not submitted earlier and the other party will have a reasonable opportunity to deal with the new
documents or other evidence. Nevertheless, Tribunals also frequently refuse to accept new documents or evidence where there
is no adequate explanation for their late submission or where the result would be procedural unfairness.
22-97
In ICC Arbitration No.19832 (unpublished), a party submitted, on the cut-off date for new documents shortly prior to the
hearing, a supplementary witness statement of one witness and a witness statement of a new witness with respect in particular
to the negotiation of the underlying contract. The Tribunal excluded both witness statements on the basis that they could and
should have been filed previously, noting in particular that, as regards the existing witness, he would be subject to brief direct
examination. 277
22-98
In ICC Arbitration No.19425 (unpublished), a party submitted witness evidence from witnesses who were unable to attend
the rescheduled evidentiary hearing, although one of the witnesses was at the place of arbitration for the hearing before its
sudden rescheduling and the witness was available for questioning. The Tribunal excluded the witness statements from both
witnesses. 278
22-99
In LCIA Arbitration No.132455 (unpublished), the respondent submitted a supplemental (third) expert report with additional
documents shortly before the hearing. The Tribunal excluded the expert report as being untimely and noting that the expert
would have an opportunity to make a brief presentation at the outset of the hearing in any event. 279
22-100
In LCIA Arbitration No.132498 (unpublished), the claimant submitted during the hearing, logs to show its failure to receive
the required dispute notice. The Tribunal excluded the documents on the basis that there was no adequate evidence as to the
reliability of the documents. 280
22-101
In an unpublished UNCITRAL Arbitration, it was the Tribunal that ordered production of a document during the hearing after
the witness of one of the parties repeatedly relied upon it in his testimony. 281
