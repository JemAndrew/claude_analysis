Chapter 15 - Mortgages and Charges
Counterclaims
15-14
Generally, the existence of a counterclaim for a sum of money where the counterclaim is for (1) unliquidated damages or (2)
liquidated damages which do not (even if proved) exceed the amount of the mortgage debt, does not defeat the mortgagee/
lender’s claim to possession. 23  It is as yet undecided whether or not a counterclaim to a liquidated sum which exceeds the
mortgage debt is sufficient to discharge the debt altogether and prevent the mortgagor from obtaining possession under the
mortgage or charge.
