Pre-action conduct
19-10
It was formerly obligatory for the claimant to state either in the claim form or in the Particulars of Claim whether he has complied
with any relevant protocol. This seems no longer to be the case.
Pleading rules
19-11
With the exception of the sections on Credit, Finance and Leasing (Section 20) and on Surety (Section 27), the CPR do not
contain any special requirements for pleading claims or defences. Those two sections (particularly the former) are governed by
multiple, strict and, it has to be said, not always consistent, rules of pleading arising from the CPR. The anomalies in that area
may be more apparent than real and have been improved since the original introduction of the CPR. The reader is referred to the
procedural material in the section on Credit, Finance and Leasing for a more detailed explanation. It should be noted, however,
that, in this area, the special procedural requirements are not confined to cases governed by the Consumer Credit Act 1974
nor, as was the case before the introduction of the CPR, confined to the County Court. If, therefore, an action involves credit,
finance, leasing or surety (at almost any level), the reader should check whether the CPR imposes requirements on the pleading.
