Section 52
Part I
Unqualified persons acting as solicitors
52. Power of Society to draw up order of court
7C-15
Where an order, whether nisi or absolute, is made by the High Court or the Court of Appeal on a motion to strike the name
of a solicitor off the roll, or to require a solicitor to answer allegations contained in an affidavit, and that order is not drawn
up by the applicant within one week of its being made, the Society may cause the order to be drawn up, and all future
proceedings on the order shall be taken as if the motion had been made by the Society.
Brexit note
7C-15. 1 The European Communities (Lawyer’s Practice) Regulations 2000 (SI 2000/1119) were to be revoked by the Services of
Lawyers and Lawyer’s Practice (Revocation etc.) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019 (SI 2019/375) reg.3(b). The 2019 Regulations
were revoked as from 24 November 2020 by the Services of Lawyers and Lawyer’s Practice (Revocation etc.) (EU Exit)
Regulations 2020 (SI 2020/1342), which also, as from 31 December 2020, revoked the 2000 Regulations.
By way of a saving provision in Sch.1 para.1 of the 2020 Regulations, s.52 continues to have effect as applied by reg.37(3) of,
and Sch.4 to, the 2000 Regulations as if the 2000 Regulations had not been revoked as above, in relation to an individual who—
 
“(a)was a registered European lawyer (within the meaning of regulation 2(1) of the 2000 Regulations as
it had effect immediately before IP completion day), registered with the Law Society, at a time before IP
completion day; but
 
(b)is not a Swiss lawyer to whom regulation 6 applies,
as those provisions had effect before IP completion day in relation to an individual who had ceased to be registered
with the Law Society under regulation 17 of the 2000 Regulations.”
