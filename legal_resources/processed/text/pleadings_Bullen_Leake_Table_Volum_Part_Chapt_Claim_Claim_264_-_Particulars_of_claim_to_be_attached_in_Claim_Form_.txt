Claims by Agents against Principals
Claim for the lost opportunity to earn commission
Particulars of claim to be attached in Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
21-F3
By a written agreement (“the agency agreement”) made on the [date] the Defendant appointed the Claimant as its sole
agent for the sale of [identify the goods] in [identify geographical area]. A copy of the agency agreement is annexed to
this Claim Form.
2.
It was an express term of the agency agreement that the Claimant was entitled to be paid commission at the rate of x% of
the sale price on sales made by the Defendant which were effected through the agency of the Claimant.
3.
On [date], the Defendant contracted with X Limited for the sale of [identify goods sold] at a total cost of £[..........] for
delivery on [date] with the option to cancel the contract should the goods not be delivered (“the sales contract”). The sales
contract was effected through the agency of the Claimant.
4.
It was an implied term of the agency agreement that the Defendant would do everything necessary to perform the sales
contract and would not do anything to prevent the Claimant earning its remuneration.
5.
The Defendant failed to deliver the goods on [date] or at all and X Limited cancelled the sales contract.
6.
In failing to deliver the goods in accordance with the sales contract and thereby permitting the purchaser to cancel it, the
Defendant acted in breach of the agency agreement and thereby prevented the Claimant from earning his remuneration.
7.
The Claimant has thereby suffered loss and damage, namely, lost commission at the rate of x% on the sale price of £[..........],
namely, £[..........].
