Chapter 25 - Supply of Goods and Digital Content
Seller’s Defences
Denial of right to reject under the Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.15A
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F46
The Defendant admits that it entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Defendant agreed to sell and
deliver the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant does not admit that the agreement was subject to the terms to be implied by the Sale of Goods Act 1979,
ss.13 to 15 and denies that it was in breach of the such terms of the agreement whether as alleged in the Particulars of
Claim or at all.
3.
The Claimant did not deal as consumer but in the course of his business as [identify business].
4.
If, contrary to the Defendant’s primary case, the agreement was subject to the terms to be implied by the Sale of Goods
Act 1979, ss.13 to 15 and the Defendant was in breach of those terms, the Defendant will say that such breach was so
slight that it would be unreasonable for the Claimant to reject the goods and the Defendant will rely upon the Sale of
Goods Act 1979, s.15A.
5.
In the premises the Claimant’s rejection of the goods was unjustified and the Defendant is not liable to repay the purchase
price to the Claimant or any part thereof.
