Defence or reply putting exemption clause in issue
If a defence, to be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document: if a reply to be served in a separate
document headed “Reply” with appropriate changes of wording
1.
25-F70
The Defendant admits that it entered into the written contract with the Claimant annexed to the Claim Form whereby the
Claimant agreed to sell and deliver to the Defendant the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant further admits that the contract contained the exclusion clause set out in paragraph X of the Particulars
of Claim.
3.
When entering into the contract the Defendant dealt as a consumer within the meaning of the Unfair Contract Terms Act
1977 and/or the [Unfair Terms in Consumer Contracts Regulations 1999] [the Consumer Rights Act 2015] and the Claimant
cannot exclude liability for breach of the obligations implied into the contract by the [Sale of Goods Act 1979, ss.12 to 15]
[the Consumer Rights Act 2015 ss.9 to 16], whether by the said clause or at all.
4.
Alternatively, if, which is denied, the Defendant did not deal as a consumer, the contract was in the Claimant’s standard
written terms of business and the Defendant will rely on the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977, ss.3 and 6.
5.
For the purposes of the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977, ss.3 and 6, the Defendant denies that it is reasonable for the
Claimant to exclude or restrict his liability to the Defendant whether by the said clause or at all.
[Statement of truth]
