Rule 45_27
45.27— Scope and interpretation 1
7B-42+
(1) This Section applies to claims that have been or should have been started under Part 8 in accordance with Practice
Direction 8B (“the Stage 3 Procedure”).
(2) Where a party has not complied with the RTA Protocol rule 45.36 will apply.
(3) “RTA Protocol” means the Pre-Action Protocol for Personal Injury Claims in Road Traffic Accidents.
(4) A reference to “Claim Notification Form” is a reference to the form used in the RTA Protocol.
Effect of rule
7B-42.
1+
This Section was added to Pt 45 by the Civil Procedure (Amendment) Rules 2010 (SI 2010/621) as a consequence of the making
of the Pre-Action Protocol for Low Value Personal Injury Claims in Road Traffic Accidents (“the RTA Protocol”) and came
into effect on 30 April 2010. There is no direction in the Costs Practice Direction supplementing this Section of Pt 45.
Application where pre-commencement funding arrangement
7B-42.
2+
Section VI applies to claims that have been or should have been started under Pt 8 in accordance with Practice Direction 8B
(“the Stage 3 Procedure”).
When first introduced, the Pre-Action Protocol for Low Value Personal Injury Claims in Road Traffic Accidents (“the RTA
Protocol”) applied to claims arising out of accidents occurring on or after 30 April 2010. By CPR Update 65 that Protocol was
revised and re-issued. As modified, the Protocol applies where a claim for damages arises from a road traffic accident where
the Claim Notification Form (CNF) is submitted on or after 31 July 2013, and the claimant values the claim at no more than

“the Protocol upper limit”. That limit is £25,000 where the accident occurred on or after 31 July 2013, or £10,000 where the
accident occurred on or after 30 April 2010, and before 31 July 2013. The RTA Protocol continues to apply in the form as it
stood immediately before 31 July 2013, to all claims where the CNF was submitted before that date. In certain circumstances,
proceedings may be commenced under a modified Pt 8 procedure for a claim subject to the pre-action process prescribed by
the RTA Protocol under what is termed (in the Protocol) “the Stage 3 procedure”. That modified procedure is set out in Practice
Direction 8B.
Before 1 April 2013, the rules as to fixed costs in Section VI of “old” Pt 45 applied to claims that had been or should have been
started under Pt 8 in accordance with Practice Direction 8B. After that date the rules as to fixed costs in Section III of “new”
Pt 45 apply. However, where the claimant in a claim that is or should have been started under Pt 8 in accordance with Practice
Direction 8B entered into a funding arrangement before 1 April 2013 (“a pre-commencement funding arrangement”), the rules
“relating to funding arrangements” in Section VII of “old” Pt 45 will apply. See also s.30 of the SCCO Guide 2013; please see
the 2018 edition of the White Book at Vol.2 para.1C-206.
A few rules in Section VII of “old” Pt 45 relate directly to funding arrangements, in particular, r.45.30 (Disbursements)
(insurance premium as type of disbursement) and r.45.31 (Success fee), and those rules are referred to in a number of other
rules in the Section (sometimes only for cross-referencing purposes, but at other times for more substantial reasons).
