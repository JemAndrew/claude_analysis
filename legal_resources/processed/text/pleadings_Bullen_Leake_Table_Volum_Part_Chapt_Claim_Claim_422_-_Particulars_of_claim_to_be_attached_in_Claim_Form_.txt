Claim for conversion by detention and when the defendant is treated as an insurer
Particulars of claim to be attached in Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
26-F9
The Claimant is the owner of a quantity of antiques that were deposited with the Defendant at his home at [address] whilst
the Claimant was working abroad. The goods are set out in the schedule annexed to the Claims Form.
2.
In a letter sent to the Defendant on [date] a copy of which is annexed to this Claim Form, the Claimant unconditionally
demanded the return of the goods and, by agreement with the Defendant, attended at the Defendant’s home on [date] to
remove them.
3.
The Defendant was absent from his home when the Claimant arrived to collect them.
4.
The Defendant’s failure to comply with the Claimant’s demand for the return of the goods on [date] rendered him liable
as an insurer from that date.
5.
On [date], the goods were destroyed by fire.
6.
In the premises, the Defendant is liable to the Claimant for the value of the goods. The approximate values that the Claimant
attributes to the goods are set out in the schedule.
7.
The Claimant is entitled to interest from [date] at such rate as the Court deems just pursuant to [s.69 of the County Courts
Act 1984] [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981].
The Claimant therefore claims:
