Solicitors Act 1974
Section 50
Part I
Unqualified persons acting as solicitors
50.— Jurisdiction of Senior Courts over solicitors
7C-10
(1) Any person duly admitted as a solicitor shall be an officer of the Senior Courts.
(2) Subject to the provisions of this Act, the High Court, the Crown Court and the Court of Appeal respectively, or
any division or judge of those courts, may exercise the same jurisdiction in respect of solicitors as any one of the
superior courts of law or equity from which the Senior Courts were constituted might have exercised immediately
before the passing of the Supreme Court of Judicature Act 1873 in respect of any solicitor, attorney or proctor admitted
to practise there.
(3) An appeal shall lie to the Court of Appeal from any order made against a solicitor by the High Court or the Crown
Court in the exercise of its jurisdiction in respect of solicitors under subsection (2).
Note
7C-11
Amended by the Constitutional Reform Act 2005 s.59 and Sch.11 para.21(5) with effect from 1 October 2009 (SI 2009/1604).
Subsection (2)
7C-12
The jurisdiction of the Court is frequently exercised, notwithstanding the existence of the Solicitors Regulation Authority. For
the circumstances in which it will be exercised see Gupta v Comer [1991] 2 W.L.R. 494; Holden & Co v Crown Prosecution
Service [1990] 1 All E.R. 368, CA.
