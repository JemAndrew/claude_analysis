Regulated agreements: pre-contract events
Defences under ss.40 and 149 of the CCA or, after 1 April 2014, the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA)
To be inserted in s.4 of Form 9B or 9D or contained in a separate document
1.
20-F48
The Defendant admits that he entered into the agreement with the Claimant set out in and annexed to the Claim Form.
2.
At the time of the agreement and at all material times thereafter the Claimant was not the holder of a licence under the
Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”).
Or
2.
At the time of the agreement and at all material times thereafter the Claimant was not authorised to carry on a consumer
credit business under Part 4A of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA) and was thus in breach of the
general prohibition under FSMA s.19.
3.
In the premises, the agreement is unenforceable by the Claimant pursuant to [s.40(1) of the CCA] [s.26 of FSMA].
Or
2.
Prior to entering into the purported agreement, the Defendant was introduced to the Claimant by one PQ, a credit-broker.
PQ was at no time the holder of a credit-broker’s licence under the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”).

[Statement of truth]
Note
References to FSMA s.26 should include references to s.26A which provides similar consequences where the lender is authorised
under FSMA but not to carry out the transaction in question. Note that, in these instances, the borrower can recover from the
lender any money already paid under the agreement.
Note also that under FSMA s.28(3) that the court can permit the lender to enforce the loan notwithstanding the apparent breach
of s.19 if it would be just and equitable to do so.
