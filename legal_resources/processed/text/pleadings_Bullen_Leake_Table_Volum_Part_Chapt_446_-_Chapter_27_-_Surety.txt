Chapter 27 - Surety
27-01
Surety is a contract of personal security, in contrast to real security—the securing of an obligation on land or chattels. Surety is a
form of contract whereby one person assumes responsibility for the obligations of another. The responsibility may be collateral
to the liability of the principal debtor, as with a guarantee, or may involve primary liability to the creditor, as with an indemnity.
This explanatory note will cover the general law of surety as it applies in all cases but it will not cover:
 
(1)procedural rules governing claims to enforce guarantees or indemnities given in respect of agreements regulated by the
Consumer Credit Act 1974;
 
(2)contracts of insurance.
Consumer credit forms will be found in Section 20: Credit, Finance and Leasing.
