Rule 45_19
45.19— Assessment of alternative percentage increase
7B-34+
(1) This rule applies where the percentage increase of fees is assessed under rule 45.18(4).
(2) If the percentage increase is assessed as greater than 20% or less than 7.5%, the percentage increase to be allowed
shall be that assessed by the court.
(3) If the percentage increase is assessed as no greater than 20% and no less than 7.5%—
(a) the percentage increase to be allowed shall be 12.5%; and
(b) the costs of the application and assessment shall be paid by the applicant.
Application for an alternative percentage increase
7B-34.
1+
Where the success fee which will be allowed either in respect of solicitors’ fees or counsels’ fees is 12.5%, a party may apply
for a greater or lesser percentage (r.45.18(1)). This may be done where any of the circumstances listed in para.(2) of r.45.18
applies. (In effect, this rule provides an exception to r.45.16 and r.45.17, wherein the success fee is fixed and may not be varied
by the court (Lamont v Burton [2007] 429, [2007]1 W.L.R. 2814, CA, at para.26).)
If the court is satisfied that the case falls within the relevant criteria it will assess the percentage increase or make an order for
it to be assessed (by a costs judge or district judge). If the percentage increase allowed is greater than 20% or less than 7.5%
the percentage allowed will be that assessed by the court. If the percentage increase is assessed at no greater than 20% and no
less than 7.5% the increase allowed will remain at 12.5% and the costs of the application and assessment will be paid by the
applicant (rr.45.18(4) and 45.19).
