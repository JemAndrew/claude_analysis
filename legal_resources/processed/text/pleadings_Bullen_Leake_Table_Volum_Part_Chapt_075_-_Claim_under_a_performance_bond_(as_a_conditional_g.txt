Claim under a performance bond (as a conditional guarantee)
1.
11-E4
The Defendant is a bank.
2.
By a letter dated [date] (“the Bond”) the Defendant undertook to indemnify the Claimant against all claims, costs and
damages which the Claimant necessarily suffered or incurred as a direct consequence of the failure by XY to fulfil in
accordance with its terms any of his obligations under the contract made between the Claimant and XY dated [date] (“the
Contract”) by paying to the Claimant such amount as may be claimed in writing by the Claimant in respect such claims,
costs and damages, provided that the total amount payable by the Defendant should not exceed £100,000. The Bond is
attached to these Particulars of Claim marked “A”.
3.
AB has failed to perform his obligations under the Contract and as a direct consequence the Claimant has incurred damages
and costs in excess of £100,000.
4.
By a letter of demand dated [date], the Claimant demanded the sum of £100,000 pursuant to the Bond.
5.
Notwithstanding that demand, the Defendant has failed to pay any sum to the Claimant, and accordingly the Claimant is
entitled to and claims £100,000.
6.
Further or alternatively, by reason of the matters set out above the Claimant has suffered loss and damage.
7.
Further the Claimant is entitled to and claims interest pursuant to s.35A of the Senior Court Acts 1981 on such sum at such
rate and for such period as the Court shall determine.
AND the Claimant claims:
 
(1)£100,000;
 
(2)Further or alternatively, damages;
