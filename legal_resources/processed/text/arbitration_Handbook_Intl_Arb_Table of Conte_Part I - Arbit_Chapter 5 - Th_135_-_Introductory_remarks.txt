Introductory remarks
28-1
Article 28 deals with conservatory and interim measures. 452  Effective dispute resolution depends on ensuring that the parties
have access to evidence and obtain the benefit of the proceedings by being able to realise on any award. It is important that
production of evidence and enforcement of the award is not frustrated by actions of the other party. National courts have
developed various methods to ensure that this will be the case, using the enforcement mechanisms that are available to them.
Arbitral tribunals are by their nature different in that they lack the “imperium” to enforce certain measures. Therefore, the issue
of conservatory and interim relief depends on the interaction between national courts and Tribunals. 453
28-2
Article 28(1) deals with interim and conservatory measures from the Tribunal, once it has received the file. Article 28(2)
deals with interim and conservatory measures from courts prior to, and exceptionally, after the file has been transmitted to the
Tribunal. Both art.28(1) and (2) must be read together with the Emergency Arbitrator provisions in art.29 which have been used
in over 154 cases since they were introduced in 2012. 454  Therefore, although the provisions of art.28 govern conservatory and
interim measures decided upon by Tribunals, there is an alternative under art.29 that the parties should consider at the outset.
As discussed under art.29, the decisions of the Emergency Arbitrator may be revisited by the Tribunal.
28-3
Article 28 refers to “conservatory or interim measures”, without providing a definition of these terms. 455  It has rightly been
suggested that these terms may be construed “as broadly as may be appropriate in each case”. 456  In an ICC arbitration, final
measures are set out in an award (whether partial or final) and that award is subject to the scrutiny of the ICC Court under art.34.
28-4
A fundamental characteristic common to provisional and conservatory measures relates to the fact that they are intended to
protect the rights of a party solely pending the final resolution of the dispute, and are therefore temporary in nature. 457  Such
measures will normally imply an assessment of the prima facie chances of the requesting party to win the case on the merits,
the measure should not lead to a prejudgment of the case, although the Rules have blurred the issue with reference to partial
awards with respect to interim and conservatory measures.
28-5
Article 28 deals with interim and conservatory measures that may require intervention of the Tribunal to preserve evidence.
However, for general issues relating to evidence, such as requests for production, it is art.25 that is applicable and not art.28.
Applications for disclosure of documents are sometimes phrased in terms of applications for interim relief. However, for
the authors a distinction should be drawn between applications for document production under art.25 and applications under
art.28(1). For the former, the main issue is relevance to the matter in dispute. For the latter, in addition to the issue of relevancy,
there is an added element of urgency or the need to preserve evidence. Therefore, the burden for obtaining an order for production
under art.25 is lower than that under art.28.
28-6
Generally, arbitration laws provide little guidance as to the granting of interim and conservatory measures by Tribunals.
Nevertheless, the need to ensure that international arbitration is effective was the main impetus for the first revision to the
UNCITRAL Model Law since it was enacted over 30 years ago. The UNCITRAL Model Law as so modified in 2006 has not

28-7
As discussed under art.18, in general the link between the Tribunal and the national courts is through the courts of the place
of arbitration. 459  However, with respect to enforcement of conservatory or interim measures, this is not necessarily the case.
The relevant national court for conservatory and interim measures will usually be the court where the conservatory or interim
measure is to be carried out. This is frequently not the country of the place of arbitration, although it may well be the country
of enforcement. Therefore, one of the key aspects of the revision to the UNCITRAL Model Law was to seek to provide a legal
mechanism for the enforcement of interim measures. 460
28-8
For example, to order disclosure of documents that might otherwise not be available, the parties will frequently seek the approval
of the courts of the respondent’s or a third party’s place of business. Therefore, where the respondent in one case was based
in the US, the party seeking an order for inspection addressed itself to the US courts. 461  In the case of inspection of a ship
located in the US, the court proceedings were brought in the US, although the place of arbitration was London. 462  To order
the attendance of a witness, usually the competent court will be the court of the place of residence of the witness. In some
instances, the place of arbitration and the place of inspection or of residence of a witness will coincide. However, that is not
always or even usually the case. Indeed, to obtain the assistance of the local courts, Tribunals may well hold hearings in places
other than the place of arbitration. 463
