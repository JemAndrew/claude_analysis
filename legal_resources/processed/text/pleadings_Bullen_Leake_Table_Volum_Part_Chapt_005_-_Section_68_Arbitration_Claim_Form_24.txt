Service out of the jurisdiction
11.
The Claimant will seek the consent of the Defendant to serve the Claim Form and all ancillary documents by way of service
on the London law firm that represented the Defendant in the Arbitration, namely [add details].
12.
If the Defendant refuses to provide his consent, the Claimant will seek an order that it has permission pursuant to CPR
Part 6.15(1) and paragraph 3.1 of PD 62 to serve the Claim Form and all ancillary documents on [the name of the law
firm] in London and/or will seek permission pursuant to CPR Part 62.5(1)(a)(i) to serve the Claim Form and all ancillary
documents out of the jurisdiction on the Defendant. The Court is not required to consider any such application at this stage.
Future directions for the Claim (if appropriate)
13.
In order to ensure the efficient case management of this claim, the Claimant will seek to agree with the Defendant the
terms of a Consent Order which departs from the automatic directions which would ordinarily apply to a challenge under
section 68 of the 1996 Act. The Claimant will rely inter alia on the comments of Colman J in Azov Shipping Co v Baltic
Shipping Co [1999] 2 Lloyd’s Rep. 159 regarding the case management of section 67 challenges which raise complex
issues of fact and foreign law. If the Defendant refuses to provide his consent, the Claimant will seek an order for non-
standard directions. The Court is not required to consider any such application at this stage.
