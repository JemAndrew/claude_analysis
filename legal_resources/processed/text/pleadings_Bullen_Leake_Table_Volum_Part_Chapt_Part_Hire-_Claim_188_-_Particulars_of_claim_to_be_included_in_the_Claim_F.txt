Hire-purchase and conditional sale agreements
Claim by the owner for delivery of the goods based on non-payment of hire and termination (regulated agreement)
(Consumer Credit Act Procedure)
Particulars of claim to be included in the Claim Form N1 or served with it
1.
20-F3
By a hire-purchase agreement in writing dated [date], the Claimant let to the Defendant on hire-purchase a [identify the
goods] (“the goods”). A copy of the agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
2.
The agreement was regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the Act”) and this is a Consumer Credit Act claim subject
to the Consumer Credit Act procedure.
3.
The cash price of the goods was £[..........], the deposit was £[..........] and the balance of £[..........] with charges of £[..........]
was payable by x equal consecutive monthly instalments of £[..........]. The total purchase price was £[..........].
4.
By clause 3 of the agreement, the Defendant agreed to pay interest of x% over PQ Bank Plc base rate on all overdue sums.
5.
By clause 10 of the agreement, if the Defendant defaulted upon the payment of any instalment, the Claimant might, upon
written notice, determine the agreement and the hiring whereupon the Defendant would be liable to deliver up the goods
to the Claimant.
6.
The following are the particulars required by the Civil Procedure Rules, PD7B.7.2.:
 
(a)the date of the agreement: [date]
 
(b)the parties to the agreement: the Claimant and the Defendant

(c)the number or other identification of the agreement (with enough information to allow the borrower to identify
the agreement): [number]
 
(d)where the Claimant was not one of the original parties to the agreement, the means by which the rights and duties
of the lendor passed to him: not applicable
 
(e)the place where the agreement was signed by the Defendant (if known): [address]
 
(f)the goods claimed: [identify the goods]
 
(g)the total price of the goods: £[..........].
 
(h)the paid-up sum: £[..........]
 
(i)the unpaid balance of the total price: £[..........]
 
(j)whether a default notice or a notice under s.76(1) or s.98(1) of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 has been served on
the Defendant, and if it has, the date and method of service: a default and termination notice was served on [date]
(copy annexed to these Particulars of Claim)
 
(k)the date when the right to demand delivery of the goods accrued: [date]
 
(l)the amount (if any) claimed as an alternative to the delivery of goods: £[..........]
 
(m)the amount (if any) claimed in addition to—
 
(i)the delivery of the goods, or
 
(ii)any claim under sub-paragraph (l) above,
with the grounds of each claim: see following paragraphs.
7.
In pursuance of the agreement the Defendant paid the deposit and x months instalments of rent, totalling £[..........].
8.
In breach of the agreement the Defendant failed to pay the instalment due [date] or any subsequent instalment. At [date]
the instalments were £[..........] in arrear. The further sum of £[..........] was due by way of contractual interest.
9.
By the said notice dated [date] the Claimant lawfully determined the agreement and the hiring. On [date], therefore, the
Claimant became entitled to possession of the goods. The Defendant has failed or refused to deliver up the goods to the
Claimant.
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)Delivery up of [identify the goods] or payment of £[..........] its value.
 
(2)£[..........] arrears of instalments £[..........].
 
(3)£[..........] contractual interest accrued from [date when each sum became due] to the date of the issue of the claim
and continuing at the daily rate of £[..........] until judgment.
 
(4)Further or alternatively: damages for breach of contract.
 
(5)Contractual interest alternatively interest pursuant to s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984 at the rate of x% on all
sums due from [date] to judgment.
