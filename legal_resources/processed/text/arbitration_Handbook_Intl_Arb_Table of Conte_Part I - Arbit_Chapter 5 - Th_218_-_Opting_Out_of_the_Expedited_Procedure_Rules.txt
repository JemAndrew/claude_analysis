Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
“Opting Out” of the Expedited Procedure Rules
30-18
Arbitration agreements signed after 1 January 2021 and containing a general reference to the ICC Rules and providing that
disputes shall be resolved by three arbitrators will be interpreted as containing an implicit agreement that disputes will be
resolved by a Sole Arbitrator, where the amount in dispute does not exceed the US$3 million threshold. 653  Should parties wish
to avoid such an outcome, they will need to either provide that the Expedited Procedure Provisions shall not apply, i.e., to opt
out, or, alternatively, that they agree to have any dispute settled by three arbitrators including in cases where the Expedited
Procedure Provisions apply.
30-19
If the parties wish to exclude the application of the Expedited Procedure Provisions, the ICC recommends an express statement
to that effect in the ICC arbitration clause as follows:
“The Expedited Procedure Provisions shall not apply.”
30-20
It undoubtedly requires a minimum of understanding of the ICC Rules to appreciate the impact and meaning of such exclusion
in the standard ICC arbitration clause. In 2019, parties decided to opt out of the Expedited Procedure Rules in only 5 cases. 654
