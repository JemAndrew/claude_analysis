Defence that bill was delivered conditionally, and that claimant on notice of the condition
1.
10-E13
It is admitted that the Defendant signed as drawer the Bill payable to AB Ltd upon which the Claimant sues.
2.
The Bill was delivered to AB Ltd conditionally upon terms agreed orally between the Defendant and Mr EF (the managing
director of AB Ltd) that [set out the details of the condition].
3.
That condition has not been complied with in that [set out details].
4.
Despite those facts, Mr EF has wrongfully purported to indorse the bill to the Claimant CD Ltd, which is another company
of which he is a director.
5.
The Claimant (by virtue of the knowledge of Mr EF) had notice of those matters and is in the premises not a holder in due
course of the Bill, which has never been validly delivered.
6.
In the premises, the Defendant is not liable upon the bill as alleged or at all.
