Interaction between Article 29 and Appendix V
29-17
With its seven paragraphs, art.29 is one of the longest articles in the Rules. Appendix V, which contains the “Emergency
Arbitrator Rules,” consists of eight articles. These provisions, together with art.29(1)–(4) referred to herein as the Emergency
Arbitrator Provisions, provide an autonomous set of procedural rules for an emergency arbitrator. However, with regard to the
substantive issue of whether interim measures should be granted the provisions of art.28 are highly relevant.
29-18
The procedural autonomy of the Emergency Arbitrator Provisions is confirmed by the General Rule implemented in art.8 of
App.V. Pursuant to art.8(1). The President has the power to discretionarily decide all administrative questions that were not
dealt with in art.29 and App.V. Article 8(3), which echoes art.42 of the ICC Rules, provides that in all matters not addressed in
the Appendix, the President and the Emergency Arbitrator should act “in the spirit of the Rules and this Appendix”. This general
provision aims at allowing the President and the Emergency Arbitrator to fill any lacunae in art.29 and App.V, to carry out the
purposes of art.29. For instance, the Emergency Arbitrator could request on the basis of art.8(3) of the Emergency Arbitrator
Rules the parties’ counsel to submit a power of attorney with reference to art.17 of the Rules.
