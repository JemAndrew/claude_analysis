In light of the far-reaching impact of the Expedited Procedure Provisions, the ICC felt rightly that these new provisions could not
be imposed upon parties that at the time of entering into an ICC arbitration agreement were not, and could not have been, aware
of the existence of the Expedited Procedure Provisions. The same considerations prevailed when the new Emergency Arbitrator
measures were introduced into the Rules in art.29. 663  As of 1 January 2021, for arbitration agreement entered into after 1 March
2017, this holds true only as far as the threshold amount for US$3 million (instead of previously US$2 million) is concerned.
30-33
As mentioned above, even where the parties’ original agreement predates 1 March 2017, the parties remain free to agree on
the application of the Expedited Procedure Provisions.
