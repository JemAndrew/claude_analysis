Part 3 Costs Orders Against a Legally Aided Party and the Lord Chancellor
11. Enforcement of costs order against a legally aided party
7D-114+
Where, for the purpose of enforcing a costs order against a legally aided party who is not a legal person, a charging order
is made under section 1 of the Charging Orders Act 1979 (alone or together with any other judgment or order) in respect
of that party’s interest in the main or only dwelling in which that party resides—
(a) that charging order must operate to secure the amount payable under the costs order (including, without limitation,
any interest) only to the extent of the amount (if any) by which the proceeds of sale of the legally aided party’s interest
in the dwelling (having deducted any mortgage debts) exceed £100,000; and
(b) an order for the sale of the dwelling must not be made in favour of the person in whose favour the charging order
is made.
