Order
29-65
Although the word “order” is not defined in the Rules, it is clear that the decision of the Emergency Arbitrator is not to be
an award, but an Order, not different from any other procedural order a Tribunal will take when conducting an arbitration.
By not giving the Emergency Arbitrator the possibility to grant provisional relief in the form of an (interim) award, 580  a
marked difference exists with the provisional relief a Tribunal can grant pursuant to art.28(2) of the Rules and the Order that
the Emergency Arbitrator may issue under art.29. 581  Unlike the ICC, other arbitral institutions have given the Emergency
Arbitrator the power to issue either an award or an order. 582
29-66
Since the decision is rendered only in the form of an Order, it follows logically that it is not open, or subject, to the scrutiny
process under art.34 of the Rules, which is exclusively reserved to awards. In that respect, the Order of the Emergency Arbitrator
is not treated any differently from the procedural order of a Tribunal. Neither the ICC Court, nor its Secretariat formally review
procedural orders of a Tribunal. Accordingly, the Emergency Arbitrator Provisions do not provide for any kind of scrutiny or
review of the Order issued by the Emergency Arbitrator.
29-67
To facilitate the task of the Emergency Arbitrator, the Secretariat has prepared a checklist to assist in drafting the Order, and
will, upon the request of the Emergency Arbitrator, review the draft Order. 583
29-68
Article 6(1) of the Emergency Arbitrator Rules reiterates the statement of art.29(2) (first sentence) of the Rules that the decision
of the Emergency Arbitrator is to be in the form of an Order.
29-69
It is implied by the first sentence of art.29(2) of the Rules that the Order must be in writing. Article 6(3) of the Emergency
Arbitrator Rules expressly states this, and further states that the Order must contain reasons. It shall also be dated and signed
by the Emergency Arbitrator.
