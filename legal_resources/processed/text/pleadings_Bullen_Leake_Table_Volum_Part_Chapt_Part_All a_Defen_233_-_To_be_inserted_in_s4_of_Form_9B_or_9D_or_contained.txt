Part I. - Defences against Actions by the Lender or Owner
All agreements
Defence to a claim under a minimum payments clause in a hire agreement
To be inserted in s.4 of Form 9B or 9D or contained in a separate document
Defence
1.
20-F47
The Defendant admits that he entered into the hire agreement with the Claimant set out in and annexed to the Claim Form.
2.
The Defendant denies that he was in breach of the agreement either as alleged in the Particulars of Claim or at all and
denies that the Claimant was entitled to serve the said or any notice terminating the hiring.
3.
In so far as the Claimant relies on clause X of the agreement purporting to provide for a minimum payment to be made by
the hirer in the event of termination, the Defendant will say that this clause is void or unenforceable as being an unlawful
penalty.
4.
In the premises, the Defendant is not liable to the Claimant either as set out in the Particulars of Claim or at all.
[Statement of truth]
