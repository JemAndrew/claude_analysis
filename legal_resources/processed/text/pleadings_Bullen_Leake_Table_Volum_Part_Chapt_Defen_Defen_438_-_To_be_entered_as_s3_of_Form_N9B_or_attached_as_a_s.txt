Defences Based on Loss of Title
Defence of purchase from mercantile agent
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9B or attached as a separate document
1.
26-F25
The Claimant is a dealer in machinery and appointed Jones his agent having in the customary course of his business the
power to sell the Claimant’s machines. In the premises, Jones was a mercantile agent within the meaning of s.1(1) of the
Factors Act 1889.
2.
In the course of his agency, Jones stored a quantity of the Claimant’s machines in his warehouse in [address] and was
thereby in possession of those machines with the consent of the Claimant.
3.
It is admitted that by a contract made on [date], Jones sold x machines to the Defendant at £[..........] a piece. The Defendant
is unable to admit or deny whether in doing so, Jones had the authority of the Claimant and the Claimant is required to
prove an absence of authority. At the time of the sale, the Defendant as purchaser was acting in good faith and without
notice of such lack of authority as may be proved and thereby acquired good title to the x machines.
