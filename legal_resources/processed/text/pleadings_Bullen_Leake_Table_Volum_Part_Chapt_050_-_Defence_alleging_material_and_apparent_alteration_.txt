Defence alleging material and apparent alteration, or denying status of claimant as holder in due course
1.
10-E8
The Defendant accepted the bill relied on by the Claimant except that the Bill as it was drawn and accepted by the Claimant
was payable on 30 June 2015.
2.
At a date which is unknown to the Defendant but which was subsequent to his acceptance the bill has been altered by the
amendment of the date of “30th June 2015” so as to cause it to read “30th January 2015”.
3.
That was a material alteration within the meaning of s.64 of the Bills of Exchange Act 1882 and was made without the
Defendant’s knowledge authority or assent.
4.
Furthermore, the alteration was apparent upon an inspection of the face of the bill, where the overwritten letters can be
clearly distinguished.
5.
Because of that, the bill was not complete and regular upon its face, and the Claimant is not a holder in due course of it.
6.
In the circumstances the bill is avoided as against the Defendant and he is not liable upon it, nor is the Claimant entitled
to enforce the bill to any extent.
