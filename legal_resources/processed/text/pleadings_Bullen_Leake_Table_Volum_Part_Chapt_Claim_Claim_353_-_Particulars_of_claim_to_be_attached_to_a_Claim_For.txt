On or about [date] the Claimant entered into an oral agreement with the Defendant at the Defendant’s premises at [address]
whereby the Claimant bought [set out the goods bought] for a price of £[..........].
2.
The Claimant bought the goods as a consumer.
3.
Under s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979 (“the 1979 Act”), it was an implied term of the agreement that the goods should
be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Claimant required it and should be of satisfactory quality.
Or
3.
Under s.9 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (“the 2015 Act”), it was a term of the agreement that the quality of the goods
should be satisfactory and under s 10, that they should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Claimant required
them.
4.
In breach of the agreement, the goods were not reasonably fit for the purpose for which they were required nor were they
of satisfactory quality.
