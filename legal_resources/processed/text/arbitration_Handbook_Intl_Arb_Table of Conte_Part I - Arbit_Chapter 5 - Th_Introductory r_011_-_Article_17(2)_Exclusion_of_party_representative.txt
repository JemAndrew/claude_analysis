Article 17(2): Exclusion of party representative
“The arbitral tribunal may, once constituted and after it has afforded an opportunity to the parties to comment in writing
within a suitable period of time, take any measure necessary to avoid a conflict of interest of an arbitrator arising from
a change in party representation, including the exclusion of new party representatives from participating in whole or in
part in the arbitral proceedings.”
17-7
The second paragraph of art.17 was also newly introduced with the 2021 Rules and provides now for a far-reaching power:
the possibility to exclude counsel from the proceedings, if that is “necessary to avoid a conflict of interest of an arbitrator.”
The (2013) IBA Guidelines on Party representation in International Arbitration contemplate in Guideline 6 a similar power “to
safeguard the integrity of the proceedings.” 16
17-8
In exercising its power to exclude a party representative, the Tribunal must balance its duties under the Rules, including its
duties to seek to render an enforceable award and the parties’ rights to due process. The 2021 ICC Note states with respect to
art.17(2) that the Tribunal “shall carefully consider all relevant circumstance with the aim of safeguarding the integrity of the
arbitration, such as (a) the ability of the party that has introduced new representative to properly submit its case in the absence
of that representative, (b) the timing of the addition of such newly introduced party representative, and (c) the disruption to
the arbitration that may result from its continuing participation in case of a successful challenge against one or more of the
arbitrators.” 17  The Tribunal will have to give the parties the opportunity to comment in writing within a suitable period of
time. In practice, there may even be dual exchanges of written submissions. Such incidents in themselves may prove to be very
disruptive for the arbitration and may require the Tribunal to suspend and thereafter adjust the procedural timetable. 18
