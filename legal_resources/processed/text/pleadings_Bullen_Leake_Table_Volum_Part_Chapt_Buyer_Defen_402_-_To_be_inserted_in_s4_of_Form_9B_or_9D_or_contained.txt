Buyer’s Defences
Defence in relation to a “doorstep selling” transaction — contract unenforceable
To be inserted in s.4 of Form 9B or 9D or contained in a separate document
1.
25-F72
The Defendant admits that he entered into the agreement referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The agreement was concluded at the Defendant’s home at [address] and was an agreement to which [the Cancellation of
Contracts made in a Consumer’s Home or Place of Work etc Regulations 2008 (“the 2008 Regulations”)] [the Consumer
Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (“the 2013 Regulations”)] applied.
3.
Under reg.7 of the 2008 Regulations, the Claimant was obliged to give the Defendant at the time of the contract written
notice containing the information set out in reg.7(3). Under reg.7(4) where, as here, the agreement is concluded in writing,
the notice must be incorporated in the same document as the agreement.
Or
3.
Under reg.10 of the 2013 Regulations the Claimant was obliged to give the Defendant the information set out in Schedule
2 to the Regulations and a cancellation form as set out in Sch.3 Part B.
4.
Under reg.7(3)(d), the Claimant was obliged to serve with the notice of cancellation rights a cancellation form in the form
prescribed by Part II of the Schedule to the Regulations.
5.
In breach of reg.7 of the 2008 Regulations:
 
(a)the Claimant failed to supply the Defendant with a notice of cancellation rights whether as part of the agreement
or at all

(b)the notice of cancellation rights contained in the agreement did not contain the following prescribed information

6.
In the circumstances the agreement is unenforceable by the Claimant under reg.7(6) of the 2008 Regulations.
Or
4.
Under reg.18 of the 2013 Regulations it was a term of the contract that the Claimant had complied with regs.9 to 14 and 16.
5.
In breach of reg.10 of the 2013 Regulations the Claimant failed to provide the information required by Sch.2 [give
particulars] and did not serve a cancellation form whether in the form prescribed by Sch.3 or at all.
6.
In the circumstances the Defendant was entitled to reject the goods and to treat the contract as at an end and has done so.
The Claimant is therefore precluded from enforcing the agreement whether as claimed or at all.
