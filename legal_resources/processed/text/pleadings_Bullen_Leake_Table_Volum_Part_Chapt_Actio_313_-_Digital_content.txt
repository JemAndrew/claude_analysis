Chapter 25 - Supply of Goods and Digital Content
Action for breach of condition or warranty
Digital content
25-32
The SGA makes no provision for digital content but it is given an entire chapter (Pt 1 Ch.3) of the CRA. The Act covers both
contracts to supply digital content for a price and contracts where the supply of the content is free, but it can only be obtained
by purchasing goods or services—CRA s.33(2). The contents of Ch.3 are in line with those of Ch.2 (goods). The digital content
must be of satisfactory quality—CRA s.34—and reasonably fit for purpose—s.35. It must match its description—CRA s.36—
and pre-contract information given under the 2013 Regulations (see para.20-15) is incorporated into the contract—s.37. There
is an obligatory term that the supplier has the right to supply the content to the consumer—s.41. The terms imported by CRA
ss.34 to 37 and 41 cannot be excluded—CRA s.47. Note also in relation to goods that goods which include digital content do not
conform to the contract if the digital content does not conform even if, in all other respects, the goods do conform—CRA s.16
(not excludable). Thus if a computer is provided with an application that does not work properly, the entire goods (computer
plus software) fail to conform with the contract.
