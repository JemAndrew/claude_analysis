9.
On [date], the Defendant commenced proceedings against the Claimant in High Court of [X], [Country], claiming a negative
declaration of liability in respect of the alleged breach of warranty made by the Claimant (“the [Country] Proceedings”).
The claim in the [Country] Proceedings were served on the Claimant on [date]. A copy of the claim is at pages [--] to
[--] of KM1.
10.
I believe that the [Country] Proceedings concern disputes between the parties which prima facie fall within the scope of
the Arbitration Agreement and therefore fall to be resolved and/or determined according to the arbitral procedure set out
therein. I believe that the commencement and continuation of the [Country] Proceedings constitute a clear breach of the
Arbitration Agreement.
11.
I wrote to the Defendant on [date] informing it of this, and demanding that it discontinue those proceedings forthwith. A
copy of this letter is at pages [--] to [--] of KM1. On [date], the Defendant responded, denying that the [Country] Proceedings
constituted a breach of the Arbitration Agreement on the basis that the Arbitration Agreement is invalid and, in any event,
that the dispute was outside of the scope of the Arbitration Agreement.
12.
The Claimant’s [Country] lawyers, [name], advises, and I believe, that the Claimant has taken no steps which might be
construed as a submission to the jurisdiction of the High Court of [X] on the merits.
13.
On [date], the Defendant wrote to the Claimant stating that it intended to commence separate proceedings in the High
Court of [X], [Country], for an allegedly defamatory statement made by the Claimant’s current Chief Executive Officer,
Mr [name], to the local newspaper in relation to various conduct of the Defendant’s employees during the negotiation and
execution of the SSPA (“the Threatened [Country] Proceedings”). A copy of the published newspaper article is at pages
[--] to [--] of KM1. I believe that the Threatened [Country] Proceedings concern disputes between the parties which prima
facie fall within the scope of the Arbitration Agreement and therefore fall to be resolved and/or determined according to
the arbitral procedure set out therein. I believe that the commencement of the Threatened [Country] Proceedings would
constitute a further clear breach of the Arbitration Agreement.
14.
I believe that the Defendant has no intention of commencing arbitration against the Claimant under the Arbitration
Agreement and unless restrained will continue to commence and/or pursue its claims in continued breach of the Arbitration
Agreement.
15.
In all the circumstances, I believe that it is just and convenient to grant a final anti-suit injunction against the Defendant
restraining it from pursuing the [Country] Proceedings and from commencing any further proceedings there or anywhere
else, in breach of the Arbitration Agreement.

Service of the Claim Form out of the jurisdiction
16.
The Claimant has applied for permission to serve the Claim Form, the response pack (consisting of Forms N8B and N15),
the Claimant’s evidence in support of its application, any Order of this Court, and any other documents requiring service
on the Defendant under the Civil Procedure Rules in regard to this claim on the Defendant out of the jurisdiction.
17.
For the purposes of that application, I state the following:
 
(1)The grounds on which the Claimant relies for permission to serve out of the jurisdiction are those contained in: (i)
CPR r.62.5(1)(c), because the Claimant requires a question to be decided and/or seeks a remedy affecting an arbitration
and/or the seat of the said arbitration is London England; and/or (ii) CPR r.6.36 (founded on paragraph 3.1(6)(c) of
Practice Direction 6B to the CPR), because the claim is made in respect of a contract (the Arbitration Agreement)
which is governed by English law
 
(2)The Defendant’s address for service is: [address].
 
(3)For the purposes of CPR r.6.37(1)(b), I confirm that the Claimant believes that the claim has a reasonable prospect
of success. For the purposes of CPR r.6.37(3), I confirm that England and Wales is the proper place in which to bring
the claim because the seat of the arbitration is within the jurisdiction.
