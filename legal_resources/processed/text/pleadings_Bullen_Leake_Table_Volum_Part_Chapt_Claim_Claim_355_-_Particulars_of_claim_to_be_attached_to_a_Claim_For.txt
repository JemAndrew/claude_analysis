25-F25
On or about [date] the Defendant was offering for sale the following digital content [identify the software] (‘the software’).
2.
In advertising material [identify the material] the Defendant represented [set out representations]. The representations
concerned matters which the Defendant was required to disclose under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation
and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/3134).
3.
In reliance on the representations, the Claimant bought the software as a consumer and installed it on his computer. The
sale was a contract to which Part 3 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (‘the 2015 Act’) applied.
4.
Under the 2015 Act the following were terms of the agreement:
 
(a)the quality of the software should be satisfactory (s.34);
 
(b)the software should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Claimant required it (s.35);
 
(c)the software should match the description given to it by the Defendant (s.36);
 
(d)the information given pursuant to the 2013 Regulations became terms of the agreement (s.37)
5.
In breach of the agreement, the quality of the software was not satisfactory, it was not reasonably fit for the purpose
for which it was required, it did not conform with the description nor with the pre-contract information under the 2013
Regulations.
