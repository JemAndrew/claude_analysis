Financial promotion
12-06
FSMA prohibits the communication of an invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity or in claims management
activity in the course of a business unless:
 
(i)the person making the communication is an authorised person; or
 
(ii)the content of the communication has been approved by an authorised person. 106
Engaging in investment activity means entering or offering to enter into an agreement the making or performance of which
by either party constitutes a controlled activity, or exercising any rights conferred by a controlled investment to dispose of,
underwrite or convert a controlled investment. 107  Controlled activities and controlled investments are specified by the Treasury
in delegated legislation 108 ; this concept is not co-terminous with the concept of “regulated activity”. 109
Engaging in claims management activity means entering into or offering to enter into an agreement the making or performance
of which by either party constitutes a controlled claims management activity. 110  Controlled claims management activities are
specified by the Treasury in delegated legislation. 111
Where a communication is made or approved by an authorised person, the act of communication must comply with the
regulations made under FSMA. Communications are divided into “real time” (i.e. communications in the course of a personal
visit, telephone conversation or other interactive dialogue) and all other communications, which are termed “non-real time”.
Certain communications are exempted. 112  This includes:
 
(i)A communication received by a person outside the UK or directed (whether from inside or outside the UK) only
at persons outside the UK. An unsolicited real time communication which emanates from the UK or is made for the
purpose of a business carried on inside the UK is not exempt. This exemption does not apply to an electronic commerce
communication 113  made from an establishment in the UK to a person in an EEA State other than the UK. It also does not
apply to any communication in respect of a controlled claims management activity. 114
 
(iii)SI 2005/1529 art.12(4) set out conditions which, if met, will result in a communication being regarded as directed only
at persons outside the UK. However, a communication may be determined to be directed only at persons outside the UK
even if none of the conditions set out are met. 115
 
(iv)Communications may be made to persons whom the communicator believes on reasonable grounds to be “certified high
net worth individuals”. 116  These communications must be solicited, if they are real time communications and are subject
to requirements as to the content of the communication. Principally, the communication must contain or be followed by a
warning: “The content of this promotion has not been approved by an authorised person within the meaning of the Financial
Services and Markets Act 2000. Reliance on this promotion for the purpose of engaging in any investment activity may
expose an individual to a significant risk of losing all of the property or other assets invested.”
 
(v)Communications may also be made to high net worth companies, unincorporated associations, partnerships and high
value trusts. Generally, a company or unincorporated association or partnership must have called-up share capital or net

(vi)Communications may also be made to “certified sophisticated investors” i.e. a person who has a current certificate in

(vii)A communication concerning controlled activities or controlled claims management activities may be made to a person
who carries on a business of publishing material relating to controlled activities or controlled claims management activities.
This potentially exempts communications with the investment press and other news media. 119
