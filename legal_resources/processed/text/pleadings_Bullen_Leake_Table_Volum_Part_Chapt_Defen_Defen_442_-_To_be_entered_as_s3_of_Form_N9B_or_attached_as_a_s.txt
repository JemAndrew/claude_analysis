Chapter 26 - Title to Goods—Torts Against Goods
Defences Asserting a Right Against the Owner
Defence of stoppage in transit
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9B or attached as a separate document
1.
26-F29
The Defendant admits that he sold the goods to the Claimant as alleged in paragraph 1 of the Particulars of Claim and that
property in the goods thereupon passed to the Claimant.
2.
The Claimant neglected to pay the price when the same fell due.
3.
On [date], the Defendant delivered the goods to XY Limited, a carrier, with instructions to deliver them to the Claimant.
4.
On [date], if not before, the Claimant became insolvent in that it ceased to pay its debts in the ordinary course of business
or was unable to pay its debts as they fell due. The Defendant will rely upon a petition to wind up the Claimant that was
presented to the High Court on [date] in which it was alleged that the Claimant was insolvent.
5.
Pursuant to s.44 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979, the Defendant was entitled to exercise a right of stoppage in transit and to
prevent the delivery of the goods to the Claimant. The Defendant gave notice to the carrier of his right to do so on [date],
a copy of which is annexed to this Defence. The Defendant thereby resumed his right to possession of the goods.
6.
In the premises, the Defendant is entitled to a lien on the goods until payment or tender of the price.
