UPON THE APPLICATION of the claimant by summons dated [date]
UPON HEARING etc
AND UPON READING the documents recorded on the Court File as having been read
THE COURT declares that the contract mentioned in the Statement of Claim herein ought to be specifically performed
and carried into execution
AND IT APPEARING that the defendant will be entitled to an equitable lien on such shares until payment of the balance
of the purchase price (if any) in accordance with clause [number] in the said contract
AND UPON the claimant by his Counsel undertaking that he will deposit (a) the certificate relating to the shares hereinafter
mentioned with his solicitors and (b) a transfer of the said shares duly executed by him in favour of the defendant
IT IS ORDERED:
 
(1)that the defendant do deliver to the claimant the certificate relating to 4,000 ordinary shares of 1 each in C.D.
Limited in the name of the defendant and a transfer duly executed of the said shares to the claimant;
 
(2)that the solicitors for the claimant do hold the certificate for the said shares in the name of the claimant and the
transfer referred to in the claimant’s said undertaking as stakeholders until payment of the balance of the consideration
(if any) due under the said contract in accordance with clause [number] thereof;
 
(3)that the defendant do pay to the claimant his costs of this action down to and including this order to be taxed if
not agreed;
 
(4)the parties are to be at liberty to apply.
Conditional relief
The above minute of order is based on the order in Langen & Wind v Bell [1972] Ch. 685, and is intended to preserve the
equitable lien of the vendor where part of the consideration for the purchase price is payable after the date for completion
of the sale. A well-drafted contract ought to provide for security for the purchaser’s obligations in such circumstances.
