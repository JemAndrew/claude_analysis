Claims against Auctioneers
Claim by purchaser for non-delivery
Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
25-F31
The Claimant is a dealer in old master paintings. The Defendant carries on the practice of a fine art auctioneer.
2.
At an auction sale held at the Defendant’s premises at [address] on [date], the Claimant purchased from the Defendant a
painting [name of painting] (“the painting”) for the sum of £[..........].
3.
At the conclusion of the sale, the Claimant tendered to the Defendant a bank draft for the said sum plus auction commission
and tax but the Defendant refused to deliver up the painting to the Claimant on the pretext that the sale had been subject
to a reserve which the Defendant had forgotten to apply during the auction.
4.
In the premises the Claimant is entitled to immediate delivery up of the painting.
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)An order that the Defendant do forthwith deliver up to the Claimant the painting [identify the painting].
 
(2)Interest on damages under [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981] [s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984] at the rate
of x% until judgment.
