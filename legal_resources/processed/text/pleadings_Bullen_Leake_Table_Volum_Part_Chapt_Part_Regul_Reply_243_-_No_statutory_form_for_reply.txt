Reply by lender to a defence in 20–F51
No statutory form for reply
1.
20-F55
By way of reply, the Claimant denies the validity of the defence.
2.
The Claimant accepts that the agreement was regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”) and that the
Consumer Credit (Agreements) Regulations 1983 (“the Regulations”) applied to it.
3.
The Claimant denies that the agreement was not properly executed within the meaning of ss.61 and 65 of the CCA either
as alleged in the Defence or at all [state reasons if possible].
4.
The Claimant denies the agreement failed to comply with the provisions of s.61 of the CCA or with the provisions of
the Regulations.
5.
If, however, which is denied, the agreement failed to comply with the provisions of s.61 of the CCA or of the Regulations
then the Claimant will seek an enforcement order under s.65 of the CCA and the Claimant will contend that it would be
just to make an enforcement order in the circumstances.
Particulars
 
(a)The Defendant was not prejudiced by the alleged contraventions of the CCA and the Regulations in that the
document set out all the material terms of the agreement and the Defendant continued to perform obligations under
the agreement with knowledge that the agreement did not conform to the CCA and the Regulations.
 
(b)Any responsibility for the contravention of the CCA and the Regulations is that of the Defendant who altered the
form of the agreement before signing it.
