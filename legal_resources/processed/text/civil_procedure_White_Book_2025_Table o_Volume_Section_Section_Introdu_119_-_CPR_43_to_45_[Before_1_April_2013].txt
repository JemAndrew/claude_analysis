Introduction
7B-0+
With effect from 1 April 2013, by r.15 of the Civil Procedure (Amendment) Rules 2013 (SI 2013/262), Pt 43 was revoked and,
by r.16, Pts 44 to 48 as they stood before that date were replaced by Pts 44 to 48 as set out in the Schedule to that statutory
instrument. By CPR Update 60, the Costs Practice Direction was revoked and replaced by several new practice directions.
However, by operation of transitional and savings provisions, various provisions in the Parts revoked and related practice
direction continue to have effect for certain purposes.
In particular, success fees under conditional fee agreements and after the event insurance premiums continue to be recoverable
as costs where they arise under “a pre-commencement funding arrangement”. In Pt 48 as substituted by the Civil Procedure
(Amendment) Rules 2013 (SI 2013/262), brought into effect on 1 April 2013, r.48.1(1) states that the provisions “relating to
funding arrangements” in Pts 43 to 48, and the attendant provisions of the Costs Practice Direction, will apply in relation to “a

pre-commencement funding arrangement” (as defined in r.48.2) as they were in force immediately before 1 April 2013, with
such modifications (if any) as may be made by a practice direction on or after that date.
In relation to proceedings other than insolvency-related proceedings, publication and privacy proceedings, or a mesothelioma
claim, “pre-commencement” means a funding arrangement made before 1 April 2013. In relation to insolvency-related
proceedings, publication and privacy proceedings, or a mesothelioma claim, “pre-commencement” means a funding
arrangement made at any time before ss.44 and 46 of the Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012 come
into force. In respect of insolvency-related proceedings, ss.44 and 46 came into force on 6 April 2016. In respect of publication
and privacy proceedings, s.44 (success fees) came into force on 6 April 2019. However s.46 (after the event insurance premiums)
has not as yet been brought into force in respect of publication and privacy proceedings. Neither section has yet been brought
into force in relation to mesothelioma claims.
For a detailed explanation, see paras 48.0.1 to 48.0.4 “Continuing Application of CPR Parts 43 to 48 and Costs Practice Direction
as in force immediately before April 1, 2013”.
In Pt 44 (as substituted with effect from 1 April 2013), r.44.3(2) and (5) introduced a new test of proportionality. By r.44.3(7)
those paragraphs do not apply to cases commenced before 1 April 2013 or to costs incurred in respect of work done before 1
April 2013. In such cases, r.44.4(2)(a) as it was in force immediately before 1 April 2013 continues to apply.
Parts 43 to 48, as they were in force before 1 April 2013, therefore continue to apply to cases currently being litigated and, until
ss.44 and 46 of the 2012 Act are brought into force in respect of mesothelioma claims and s.46 is brought into force in respect
of publication and privacy proceedings, these parts will continue to apply. Accordingly the rules in Pts 43 to 45 and the relevant
parts of the Costs Practice Direction, as they stood before 1 April 2013, which continue to apply where additional liabilities
are recoverable or where the old test of proportionality is relevant, are published below. Parts 46 to 48, as they stood before 1
April 2013, were largely re-enacted and are not reproduced here. For the current destinations of the rules in those parts, please
see the 2019 edition of the White Book, paras 46x.0.1 to 48x.0.1.
