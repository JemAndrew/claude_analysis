Article 25(5): Documents only arbitration
“The arbitral tribunal may decide the case solely on the documents submitted by the parties unless any of the parties
requests a hearing.”
25-85
Unlike some other types of arbitration, ICC arbitration is a “documents only” arbitration only if the parties so agree. Any party
to an ICC arbitration may request a hearing, except in expedited arbitration for so-called small claims. Indeed, pursuant to
art.3(5) of the Expedited Procedure Rules, the Tribunal may decide the dispute on the basis of the documents submitted by the
parties, even if one party so requests, provided the Tribunal has at least consulted the parties on the issue. 410  Most parties—
and most arbitrators—assume that there will be a hearing.
25-86
It follows from the revised art.26(1) that if a party requests a hearing, the Tribunal is required to hold it, i.e. cannot render its
award based on the documents only. It also follows from that provision that the Tribunal can decide on its motion to hold a
hearing. It is unusual that the Tribunal will not hold a hearing before rendering a final award. However, it is much more frequent
that a Tribunal will render an interim or partial award without a hearing. In many instances, the parties may feel that it is not
strictly necessary to have an issue such as jurisdiction or the applicable law or applicable language decided by the Tribunal
with a hearing and that it is more cost effective to skip the oral hearing step. As regards how any hearing is to be conducted,
see the discussion of art.26(1) below.
