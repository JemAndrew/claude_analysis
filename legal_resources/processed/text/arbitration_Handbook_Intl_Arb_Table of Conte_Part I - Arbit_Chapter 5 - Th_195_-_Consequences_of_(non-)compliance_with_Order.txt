Consequences of (non-)compliance with Order
29-100
The enforceability of the Emergency Arbitrator’s Order is one of the most critical issues facing Emergency Measures under
the Rules. The Rules are silent in that respect, and in no way exclude such enforceability. Rather, this is a matter of the law
applicable of the country where a party would wish to seek judicial assistance for enforcing such an order.
29-101
Pursuant to art.17H(1) of the UNCITRAL Model Law, as amended in 2006, “an interim measure by an arbitral Tribunal shall be
recognized as binding and, unless otherwise provided by the arbitral Tribunal, enforced upon application to the competent court
…”. While this provision refers to a Tribunal, not an emergency arbitrator, courts in countries following art.17H of the Model
Law 610  will have to consider whether to treat the Emergency Arbitrator’s Order like an interim measure of a Tribunal. 611
In at least one instance, a court has enforced an Emergency Arbitrator Award. However, those measures were rendered as an
award and not an Order. 612
29-102
Whether or not judicial aid is available to enforce the Order, the parties will most probably comply, as there is a high degree of
compliance with provisional measures in ICC arbitration, 613  and with orders issued by ICC Pre-Arbitral referees. 614
