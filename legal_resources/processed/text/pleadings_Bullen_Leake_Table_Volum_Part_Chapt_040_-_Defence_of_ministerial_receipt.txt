Defence of ministerial receipt
1.
9-E13
It is admitted that on [date] the Defendant received the sum of £[..........] from AB (“the Payment”).
2.
The Payment was received innocently by the Defendant as agent of XY.
3.
Prior to the Claimant giving notice to the Defendant that the Payment was or might have been paid in breach of AB’s
fiduciary duties towards the Claimant, the Defendant applied the payment to the account of XY which was in credit at
all material times.
4.
For these reasons, it is denied [that the Claimant is entitled to restitution of the Payment] and/or [that the Defendant is
liable to the Claimant for having knowingly received property subject to fiduciary duties and/or as a constructive trustee].
