Note
It is important to note that, following implementation of the EU Consumer Credit Directive on 1 February 2011, the category of
agreements cancellable under CCA s.67 was reduced to hire agreements and (some) credit agreements for credit over £60,260.
Instead, almost all consumer credit agreements are subject to a right of withdrawal within 14 days under a new CCA s.66A.
By a series of blunders, the regulations introducing s.66A did not import into it the sections of the CCA providing for the
consequences of withdrawal under the old law. Consequently, withdrawal under s.66A does not involve rescission of a part-
exchange transaction. This precedent cannot therefore be used for a s.66A withdrawal.
