12-14
FSMA provides that the contraventions which give rise to the remedy for breach of statutory duty in ss.20 and 138D do not
make any transaction void or voidable or to create any other right of action for breach of statutory duty other than that specified
in the relevant section. 149  Accordingly, where transactions and agreements are to be attacked, it is necessary to have recourse
to the provisions of the Act which specifically render agreements unenforceable and provide for the recovery of property. 150
No action can be brought for damages against the FCA or PRA, as FSMA Sch.1ZA para.25 and FSMA Sch.1ZB confer immunity
from liability in damages on the FCA and PRA and their members, officers and employees for anything done or omitted in the
discharge or purported discharge of their functions. This is subject to two exceptions:
 
(i)Actions or omissions in bad faith. This probably requires either actual malice towards the claimant or knowledge on the
part of the relevant person that the FCA or PRA lacked power to do the act in question, or the commission of the tort of
misfeasance in public office, which necessarily involves bad faith in that sense 151 ;
 
(ii)Claims against the FCA or PRA as a public authority under s.7 of the Human Rights Act 1998, for acts on its part which
are unlawful by virtue of s.6(1) of that Act.
