1. How much of the claim do you dispute?
I dispute the full amount claimed as shown on the claim form.
2. Do you dispute this claim because you have already paid it?
No.
Defence
1.
The Defendant admits that it entered into the written contract with the Claimant annexed to the Claim Form whereby the
Defendant agreed to sell and deliver the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant further admits that it has not delivered the goods to the Claimant. For the reasons hereafter set out, the
Defendant was and is entitled to refuse delivery.
3.
It was expressly agreed in clause 4 of the contract that the Claimant should pay the purchase price within seven days of
invoice.

4.
On [date] the Defendant rendered an invoice for the goods but the Claimant has failed to pay the invoice or any part thereof
either within seven days or at all.
Or
4.
On [date] the Defendant rendered an invoice for the goods and the Claimant sent the Defendant a cheque drawn in favour

presented the cheque but it was dishonoured.
Or
4.
On [date] the Defendant rendered an invoice for the goods but on [date] a petition was presented for the winding-up of
the Claimant on the ground of its insolvency.
5.
In the premises, the Defendant, as an unpaid seller is entitled to a lien over the goods pending payment of the price and is
entitled to refuse delivery until payment of the price in full. The Defendant will rely upon the Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.41.
