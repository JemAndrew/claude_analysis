When the “Consumer Credit Act Procedure” (“CCA Procedure”) applies
Category 7: Reopening extortionate credit bargains and unfair relationships
20-49
If the debtor or surety wishes to start his own proceedings to reopen an extortionate credit bargain under s.139 or to challenge an
agreement under ss.140A-140C (“unfair relationships”), he should do so by a claim form (using the basic Form N1). The CCA
Procedure applies so that the particulars of claim must be served with the claim form which must include or be accompanied
by a notice of the hearing date.
If, however, proceedings have already been started against the debtor or surety and he wishes to challenge the agreement, CPR
PD 7B.10. applies and he must within 14 days of service of them on him serve a written notice of his intention to apply to reopen
the bargain on the court and all other parties to the proceedings. If he does this, he will be treated as having filed a defence for the
purposes of CCA Procedure. On the basis that a written notice under CPR PD 7B10 is now sufficient; no precedent for a defence
seeking the reopening of a credit bargain under ss.137–140 or challenging an agreement under ss.140A–140C has been included.
