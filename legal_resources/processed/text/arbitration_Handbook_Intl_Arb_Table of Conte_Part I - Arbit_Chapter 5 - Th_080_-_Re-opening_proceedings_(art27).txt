Re-opening proceedings (art.27)
22-105
After the hearings have been closed, parties may apply to bring new evidence to the attention of the Tribunal. To be admitted, in
the authors’ view, it must be central to the case and not have been available prior to the closing of the proceedings. In addition,
the other party must have an opportunity to respond. In an unpublished AAA arbitration relating to a licensing arrangement,
after the hearings and submissions had been completed, the parties received notice that the underlying patent was presumptively
invalid. The Tribunal re-opened the hearings to deal with the issue.
22-106
In ICC Case No.17251, dealing with claims under a joint venture agreement, the Tribunal wrote on 6 June 2012 to close
proceedings as of 15 May 2012. On 14 June 2012, a party informed the Tribunal that a judgment had been issued on 15 May 2012
in a court case. After seeking comments from the other party, the Tribunal refused to re-open the proceedings, as it determined
that the court proceedings were not related to the claims under the joint venture agreement. The award was upheld but the court
reviewed the court judgment that the Tribunal had refused to admit and concluded that it would not have changed the result. 287
In the authors’ view, the Tribunal’s decision was unfortunate. If the court judgment was unrelated, it would have been preferable
for the Tribunal to deal with it on the basis of the judgment itself, as the court eventually did.
