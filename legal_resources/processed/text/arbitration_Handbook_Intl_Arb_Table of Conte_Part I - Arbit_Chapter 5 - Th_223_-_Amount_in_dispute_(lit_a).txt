Part I - Arbitration Rules
Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
Amount in dispute (lit. a)
30-25
The benchmark for the Expedited Procedure Rules to apply is currently an amount in dispute of US$2 million if the arbitration
agreement under the 2021 Rules was concluded on or after 1 March 2017 and before 1 January 2021, and US$3 million if the
arbitration agreement under the 2021 Rules was concluded on or after 1 January 2021. When claims are submitted in a different
currency than the US$, the exchange rate at the date of submission of the claims will determine whether a claim falls within
the cap or not. 657
30-26
Just like the previous versions of the ICC Rules, the 2021 ICC Rules do not define how to calculate the amount in dispute.
In standard ICC arbitrations, the amount in dispute influences the ICC Court’s decision as to the number of arbitrators and
gives the basis for calculating the costs of arbitration. 658  Since 1 January 2021, the determination of the amount in dispute is
the key factor in determining whether certain provisions of the ICC Rules apply or not. In the 2021 ICC Note, it is stated that
the Expedited Procedure Provisions will in principle not apply to declaratory or non-monetary claims, which value cannot be
estimated, “unless it appears that such non-monetary claims merely support a monetary claim or that they do not add significantly
to the complexity of the dispute”. 659
30-27
Pursuant to art.1(3) of App.VI, the Secretariat will inform the parties as to whether the Expedited Procedure Provisions apply
“[u]pon receipt of the Answer to the Request pursuant to Article 5 of the Rules, or upon expiry of the time limit for the Answer
or at any relevant time thereafter”. Consistent with ICC practice, in order to calculate the amount in dispute, the Secretariat will
take into account claims, counterclaims and set-off claims subject to the availability of this information at the time when the
Answer to the Request for Arbitration is received. 660
30-28
The ICC is not the only arbitration institution providing for automatic application of Expedited Procedure Provisions when
the amount in dispute is below a certain amount. Yet compared to these other institutions, the ICC’s US$3 million benchmark
amount can be considered a rather high threshold. 661
