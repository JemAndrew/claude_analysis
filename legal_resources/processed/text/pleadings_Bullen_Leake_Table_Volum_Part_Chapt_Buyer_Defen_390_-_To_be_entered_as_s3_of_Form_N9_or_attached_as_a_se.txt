Chapter 25 - Supply of Goods and Digital Content
Buyer’s Defences
Defence of rejection for breach of terms implied by the Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.12 or the Consumer Rights Act 2015
s.17
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F60
The Defendant admits that he entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Claimant agreed to sell and
deliver to the Defendant the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
It was a term of the agreement that the Claimant had the right to sell the goods. The term was implied into the agreement
by the [Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.12(1)] [the Consumer Rights Act 2015 s.17].
3.
The Defendant admits that, in purported pursuance of the agreement, on [date] the Claimant delivered the goods to the
Defendant.
4.
In breach of the said condition, the Claimant did not have the right to sell the goods which were at all times the lawful
property of XY Limited. The Claimant’s purported sale to the Defendant was without the knowledge or consent of XY
Limited who had deposited the goods with the Defendant for repair.
5.
On discovering the true ownership of the goods, on [date], the Defendant, as he was entitled to do, rejected the goods and
returned them to the Claimant.
6.
In the premises the Claimant is not entitled to payment of the purchase price or any part thereof.
[Statement of truth]
