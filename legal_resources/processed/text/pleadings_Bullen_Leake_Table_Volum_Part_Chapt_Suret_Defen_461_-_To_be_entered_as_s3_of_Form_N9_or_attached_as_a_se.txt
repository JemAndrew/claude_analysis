Chapter 27 - Surety
Surety’s Defences
Defence of discharge by release of security
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
27-F10
The Defendant admits that he entered into the contract of guarantee set out in the Particulars of Claim (“the guarantee”),
whereby he guaranteed the liabilities of XY Limited to the Claimant under the lease of [address] dated [date] (“the lease”).
2.
It was an express condition of the guarantee that the Claimant should procure the execution by XY Limited of a charge
over its freehold property at [address] to secure all sums due to the Claimant under the lease.
3.
In pursuance of the guarantee, the Claimant procured the execution by XY Limited of a first legal charge over the said
freehold property dated [date].
4.
On [date] in breach of the guarantee, the Claimant agreed to release XY Limited from the legal charge which was discharged
on [date] and on [date] XY Limited sold the property [address].
5.
At all material times the value of the said property has been greater than any sums due from XY Limited under the lease.
6.
XY Limited is currently in an insolvent liquidation.
7.
The Defendant admits the letter of demand dated [date] referred to in the Particulars of Claim but will say that by reason
of the wrongful cancellation of the legal charge, he has been seriously and irremediably prejudiced and is discharged from
all liability under the guarantee.
[Statement of truth]
