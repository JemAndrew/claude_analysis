Section 7A - Litigation Funding after 1 April 2013
Recovery of Costs Insurance Premiums in Clinical Negligence Proceedings (No.2) Regulations 2013
Regulation 3
3.— Costs order may require payment of an amount of the relevant part of the premium
7A-25
(1) A costs order made in favour of a party to clinical negligence proceedings who has taken out a costs insurance
policy may include provision requiring the payment of an amount in respect of all or part of the premium of that
policy if—
(a) the financial value of the claim for damages in respect of clinical negligence is more than £1,000; and
(b) the costs insurance policy insures against the risk of incurring a liability to pay for an expert report or reports
relating to liability or causation in respect of clinical negligence (or against that risk and other risks).
(2) The amount of the premium that may be required to be paid under the costs order shall not exceed that part of the
premium which relates to the risk of incurring liability to pay for an expert report or reports relating to liability or
causation in respect of clinical negligence in connection with the proceedings.
Editorial note
7A-25. 1 The Recovery of Costs Insurance Premiums in Clinical Negligence Proceedings (No.2) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/739) revoke
and replace the Recovery of Costs Insurance Premiums in Clinical Negligence Proceedings Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/92) with
effect from 1 April 2013. The earlier regulations, which would have come into effect on 1 April 2013, therefore did not come
into force. They were the subject of criticism by the Parliamentary Joint Committee on Statutory Instruments on the ground
that they failed to comply with the requirements of the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990 s.58C(2), in that they applied to
all clinical negligence proceedings and not to proceedings only of a prescribed description and applied to all policies and not
to policies only of a prescribed description. The later regulations apply therefore only to clinical negligence proceedings the
financial value of which exceed £1,000 and only to policies which insure against the risk of liability to pay for experts’ reports
relating to liability or causation in respect of clinical negligence.
