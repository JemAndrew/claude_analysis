Seller’s Defences
Defence of supervening event excusing delivery (non-consumer sale)
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F34
The Defendant admits that it entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Defendant agreed to sell and
deliver the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The agreement was an unconditional contract for the sale of specific goods in a deliverable condition and accordingly
property in the goods passed to the Claimant on the making of the agreement and thereafter remained with the Claimant.
The Defendant will rely upon the Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.18, Rule 1.
3.
On [date] when the goods were at the Claimant’s risk, without fault on the Defendant’s part, the goods were stolen and
subsequently destroyed by the thieves.
4.
In the premises, the Defendant is not liable to the Claimant for the nondelivery of the goods.
