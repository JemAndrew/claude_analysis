Particulars of claim to be attached in Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
26-F4
The Claimant is the owner of [identify the goods] (“the goods”) which was part of a quantity of property stored by him
at the warehouse of Storage Limited in [address].
2.
At a date unknown to the Claimant, the goods came into the possession of the Defendant.
3.
By a letter dated [date], a copy of which is annexed to this Claim Form, the Claimant’s solicitors wrote to the Defendant
requesting him to deliver up the goods to the Claimant but the Defendant has failed or refused to do so.
4.
In the premises the Defendant converted the goods to his own use.
5.
By reason of the conversion, the Claimant has suffered loss and damage.
Particulars
The estimated value of the goods is £[..........].
6.
The Claimant is entitled to interest on the value of the converted goods from the date of conversion until delivery up at
such rate as the Court deems just pursuant to [s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984] [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981].
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)An order for the delivery up of the goods;
 
(2)Alternatively, delivery up of the goods or the payment of £[..........] their value.
 
(3)Alternatively, damages for conversion.
