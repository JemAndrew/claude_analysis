Credit-Sale
20-08
Credit-sale is now relatively rare. Credit-sale is a sale where the purchase price is payable by instalments but the property passes
to the buyer when the agreement is made. It is much closer to the concept of outright sale than to conditional sale and gives the
creditor no security against the goods in the event of the buyer’s default.
An agreement can be a credit-sale agreement even if only part of the purchase price is payable by instalments, a definition
repeated in s.189(1) of the CCA. As with hire-purchase and conditional sale, credit-sale can involve the tripartite relationship
between the tor, the hirer and the supplier. It is noticeable that credit-sale, as such, is not mentioned in the RAO though it
remains regulated.
