Defence alleging stockbroker’s general lien and counterclaim for unpaid commission
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9B or attached as a separate document
Defence and counterclaim
1.
23-F1
It is admitted that the Defendants are stockbrokers and that on [date], they purchased [identify securities purchased] on
behalf of the Claimant. The Defendants thereby became entitled to payment of £[..........] as commission.
2.
The Claimant failed or refused to pay the commission.
3.
On or about [date], in the course of transacting business as the Claimant’s stockbroker, the Defendant lawfully came into
possession of the share certificate for [identify the securities claimed by the Claimant] which shares were the property of
the Claimant as set out in paragraph 3 of the Particulars of Claim.
4.
In the premises, the Defendants were entitled to exercise a general lien over all the Claimant’s property lawfully coming
into their hands in the course of business, including the share certificate, to secure the payment of all sums due to them.
5.
By reason of their lien, the Defendants are entitled to retain possession of the said share certificate until the Claimant has
paid the commission due of £[..........].
