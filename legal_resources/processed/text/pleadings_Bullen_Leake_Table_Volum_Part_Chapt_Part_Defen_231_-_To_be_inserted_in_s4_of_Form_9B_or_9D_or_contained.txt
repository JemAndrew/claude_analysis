Defences of the lender based on the non-application of the CCA (claim based on a regulated agreement)

Defence
1.
20-F45
The Defendant admits that it entered into the agreement set out in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant denies that the agreement was a regulated consumer credit agreement within the Consumer Credit Act 1974
(“the CCA”) [or (if made after 1 April 2014) the Financial Services Act 2000 (Regulated Activities) Order 2001 (RAO)] .
Particulars
 
(a)[Agreements made prior to 6 April 2008] The amount of the credit exceeded the statutory limit of £25,000.
 
(b)The agreement was a non-commercial agreement [details].
 
(c)The agreement is exempted from the provisions of the CCA by [s.16(6A)] [s.16(6B)] of the CCA [RAO Art 60C].
 
(d)The agreement is exempted from the provisions of the CCA by the Consumer Credit (Exempt Agreements) Order
1989 [RAO Art 60E/60F/60G].
 
(e)The Claimant is a “high net worth” individual within the meaning of s.16A of the CCA [RAO Art 60H] and entered
into the agreement in that capacity: the agreement is therefore exempt from regulation under that section [Article].
 
(f)The agreement was an agreement for credit exceeding £25,000 and was entered into by the Claimant wholly or
predominantly for the purposes of a business carried on by him and is thus exempt from regulation under s.16B of
the CCA [RAO Art 60C].
 
(g)The agreement was an investment agreement within the meaning of s.16C of the CCA [RAO Art 60D] and is
therefore exempt from regulation under that section [Article]. This defence will not apply to investment (buy-to-let)
