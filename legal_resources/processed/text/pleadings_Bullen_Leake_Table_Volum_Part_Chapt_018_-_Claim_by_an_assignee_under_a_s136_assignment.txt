Claim by an assignee under a s.136 assignment
1.
6-C2
By a contract made in writing and set out in an agreement dated [date], the Defendant agreed to buy from A 41  a boat, The
Aiolos, in consideration of the sum of £[amount].
2.
On or about [date], A delivered The Aiolos to the Defendant.
3.
By an assignment made in writing dated [date], signed by A, 42  A assigned to the Claimant the debt of £[amount] owing
by the Defendant to the First Claimant. 43
4.
By a notice made in writing, the Claimant gave notice to the Defendant of the assignment. 44  A assigned to the Claimant
the debt of £[amount] owing by the Defendant to the First Claimant.
5.
The Defendant has not paid the sum of £[amount] or any sum.
6.
By reason of the facts and matters as aforesaid, the Claimant claims the sum of £[amount] from the Defendant.
7.
Further, the Claimant is entitled to and claims interest on the said sum pursuant to s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981 at
such rate and for such period as the Court in its discretion considers fit. 45
AND the Claimant claims against the Defendant:
 
(1)The sum of £[amount].
 
(2)Interest as aforesaid.
[Statement of truth]

Footnotes
41
i.e. the assignor. In the case of s.136 assignments, the assignor need not be joined as a party.
42
The assignment must “by writing under the hand of the assignor”. Otherwise no particular form is stipulated. See further
Smith & Leslie, The Law of Assignment (2018), paras 16.30 to 16.31.
43
Consideration is not a requirement for a s.136 assignment any more than it is for an equitable assignment. See, further,
Smith & Leslie, The Law of Assignment (2018), para.16.39.
44
In contrast to an equitable assignment, a s.136 assignment requires express notice in writing to be given to the debtor.
An assignment under s.136 will be effective only as from the date that the notice is received by or on behalf of the
debtor. Generally speaking, either the assignor or the assignee will give the notice, but this is not a requirement. As to
the requirements of the notice, see Smith & Leslie, The Law of Assignment (2018), paras 16.32 to 16.38.
45
Consideration is not a requirement for a s.136 assignment any more than it is for an equitable assignment. See, further,
Smith & Leslie, The Law of Assignment (2018), para.16.39.
