Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
20-F28
The Claimant is a finance company and the Defendant is a [set out the supplier’s business]. PQ carried on business as a
[state the hirer’s business if relevant].
2.
By a written agreement dated [date] (“the sale agreement”), the Defendant sold [identify the goods] (“the goods”) to the
Claimant for the sum of £[..........]. A copy of the sale agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
3.
At the time of the sale agreement, the Defendant knew that the Claimant was purchasing the goods for the purpose of letting
it on hire-purchase to PQ pursuant to a hire-purchase agreement which had been completed in draft on the Defendant’s
premises at [address].
4.
By s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979, there were terms of the sale agreement that the goods:
 
(a)should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Claimant required it, namely for letting on hire-purchase
to PQ;
 
(b)should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which PQ required it, namely [identify PQ’s particular purposes];
 
(c)should be of satisfactory quality.
[Or, when in force]
4.
Under s.9 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015, it was a term of the sale agreement that the quality of the goods should be
satisfactory [and under s.10 that the goods should be reasonably fit for purpose].

5.
By a written agreement dated [date], (“the hire-purchase agreement”) the Claimant let the goods to PQ on hire-purchase
to PQ. A copy of the hire-purchase agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
6.
Under the hire-purchase agreement, the cash price of the goods was £[..........], the deposit was £[..........], the total charge
for credit was £[..........] and the balance of the total purchase price of £[..........] was payable by x equal consecutive monthly
instalments of £[..........].
7.
By the Supply of Goods (Implied Terms) Act 1973, s.10, there were terms of the hire-purchase agreement that the goods:
 
(a)should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which PQ required it, namely for use in his said business;
 
(b)should be of satisfactory quality.
[Or, when in force]
7.
Under s.9 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015, it was a term of the hire-purchase agreement that the quality of the goods
should be satisfactory [and under s.10 that the goods should be reasonably fit for purpose].
8.
In breach of the sale agreement, the goods were [not reasonably fit for the said purposes nor were they] of satisfactory
quality.
Particulars
 
(a)[set out particulars of breaches]
9.
Consequently, the Claimant was in breach of the hire-purchase agreement.
10.
By reason of the breach of the hire-purchase agreement, PQ sought damages from the Claimant in the sum of £[..........].
On [date], the Claimant as it was bound to do, paid the said sum to PQ in satisfaction of his claims.
11.
By reason of the Defendant’s breaches of the sale agreement, therefore, the Claimant has suffered loss and damage in the
amount of £[..........] paid to PQ.
Or
10.
By reason of the breach of the hire-purchase agreement, PQ was entitled to treat the Claimant as having repudiated the
agreement and by a letter dated [date], (copy annexed to these Particulars of Claim) accepted that repudiation. On [date]
PQ delivered up the goods to the Claimant.
