Claim by holder in due course against acceptor, holder and prior indorsee
10-E3
On 31 January 2015 the First Defendant drew upon the Second Defendant a bill (the Bill) payable to the Third Defendant
in the sum of Euro 100,000, payable 30 days after sight.
2.
The Second Defendant accepted the Bill on 10 February 2015.
3.
On 14 February 2015 the Third Defendant as payee indorsed the Bill specially to the Claimant, in consideration of the sum
of £60,000 paid by the Claimant to the Third Defendant, and the Claimant became a holder in due course of it.
4.
On 10 March 2015 the Claimant presented the Bill to the Second Defendant for payment, but it was dishonoured and
remains unpaid.
5.
On 11 March 2015 the Claimant gave notice of dishonour in writing to the First Defendant. On the same date the Third
Defendant himself contacted the Claimant and orally expressly waived the requirement of notice of dishonour.
6.
The Claimant, as holder in due course, claims against the Second Defendant as acceptor, against the First Defendant as
drawer, and against the Third Defendant as indorser:
 
(1)The sum of Euro 100,000 [and continue as before or as appropriate].
