Defence alleging that cheque was procured by misrepresentation
1.
10-E21
It is admitted that the Defendant drew and delivered to the Claimant the cheque sued upon, and that the Defendant
countermanded payment of the cheque.
2.
The Defendant drew the cheque in payment of a non-refundable deposit for the purchase of a horse known as Dobbins
and described by the Claimant as a “brood mare”. During the course of negotiations for the purchase by the Defendant of
the horse the Claimant stated and represented to the Defendant that the horse was medically sound and would be fit for
breeding, and the cheque was drawn by the Defendant pursuant to and in reliance upon those representations.
3.
In fact those representations were false, in that the horse was infertile. Furthermore, the representations were known by
the Claimant to be false since the Claimant had himself obtained veterinary reports to that effect. In the circumstances the
representations were made fraudulently.
4.
The Defendant is entitled to and does rescind the cheque, and it is therefore denied that he is liable on it as alleged or
otherwise.
