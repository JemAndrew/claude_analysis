Mandatory application
30-22
Whenever the conditions for the application of the Expedited Procedure Provisions are met, these provisions will apply even if
the parties’ arbitration agreement provides any contrary terms. Thus, even where the ICC arbitration clause refers for instance
to a three-member tribunal, a sole arbitrator may be appointed by the ICC Court (see also art.2(1) of App.VI).
30-23
Should the parties, however, wish to have a three-member tribunal even when the Expedited Procedure Provisions apply, they
may so indicate in their agreement by providing, for example, that “all disputes arising out of or in connection with the present
contract shall be finally settled under the Rules of Arbitration of the International Chamber of Commerce by three arbitrators
appointed in accordance with the said Rules, including in cases where the Expedited Procedure Provisions of Appendix VI
apply”. Party autonomy will then prevail. Likewise, the parties will also be free to agree upon three arbitrators after the Request
for arbitration is filed. 656

The mere reference in the arbitration agreement to a three-member Tribunal will not be viewed sufficient by the ICC
Court as an “opt out” of the Expedited Procedure Provisions.
