2.
The Defendant admits that the agreement was subject to the conditions implied by [s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979].
[The Defendant denies that the Consumer Rights Act 2015 applied to the agreement for the reasons set out below.]
3.
The Defendant denies that the Claimant dealt as a consumer for the purposes of the [1979] [2015] Act. The Claimant
bought the goods in the course of his business as a [give the relevant particulars]. In the circumstances, the Claimant
cannot rely on [s.14(2D) of the 1979 Act] ] [the 2015 Act].
4.
If the Claimant did deal as consumer, the Defendant denies that he is entitled to rely on the public statements alleged to
have been made by PQ Limited as manufacturer or ZX Limited as importer of the goods set out in the Particulars of Claim.
5.
At the time of the agreement the Defendant was unaware of the said public statements and could not reasonably have been
aware of them [set out the relevant circumstances]. Consequently, under [s.14(2E)(a) of the 1979 Act] [s.9(7) of the 2015
Act], the statements are not to be taken into account.

Act], the statements are not to be taken into account.
