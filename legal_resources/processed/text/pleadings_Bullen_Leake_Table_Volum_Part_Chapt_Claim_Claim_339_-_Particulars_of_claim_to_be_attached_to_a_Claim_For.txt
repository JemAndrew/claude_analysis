Claim for price due on a contract for sale by instalments and damages for repudiation
Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
25-F9
The Claimant carries on business as a manufacturer of [identify products].
2.
By an agreement made between the Claimant and the Defendant on [date], the Claimant agreed to manufacture and sell to
the Defendant [identify relevant goods] (“the goods”) at a total price of £[..........].
3.
The agreement was concluded partly orally and partly in writing. In so far as it was oral, it was concluded at a meeting at
the Claimant’s factory at [address] between DF of the Defendant and ST of the Claimant, and in so far as it was in writing,
it was contained in or evidenced by a document in the Claimant’s standard order form completed with details of the sale
and initialled at the meeting by DF and ST (copy annexed to these Particulars of Claim).
4.
It was a term of the agreement that the goods should be supplied in x instalments of y units, each instalment to be delivered
on the first working day of the month and the first delivery to take place on [date]. Payment for each instalment was to
be made within seven days of delivery.
5.
In pursuance of the agreement, the Claimant manufactured and delivered the instalments due on [date] and [date] and the
Defendant paid the price due in respect thereof.
6.
In further pursuance of the agreement, the Claimant manufactured and delivered the instalments due on [date] and [date].
Notwithstanding the Defendant’s acceptance of those instalments, the Defendant failed to pay the price due in respect of
them either within seven days or at all and the total price of £[..........] remains due and owing.

7.
By reason of the Defendant’s failure to make the said payments, the Defendant evinced an intention no longer to be bound
by the agreement and repudiated it.
8.
Alternatively, in further breach of the agreement, in a telephone call made by DF to ST on [date], the Defendant informed
the Claimant that it would accept no further deliveries of jeans and would make no further payment. The Defendant thereby
repudiated the agreement.
9.
In the course of the said telephone call and subsequently in a letter dated [date] (copy annexed to these Particulars of
Claim), the Claimant accepted that repudiation.
10.
By reason of the Defendant’s repudiation of the agreement, the Claimant has suffered loss and damage.
Particulars
 
(a)Loss on resale of goods already manufactured at the date of repudiation, £[..........].
 
(b)Cost of wasted materials and labour on work in progress at the date of repudiation £[..........].
 
(c)Loss of profit on goods not started at the date of repudiation, £[..........].
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)Under paragraph 6: £[..........].
 
(2)£[..........] contractual interest alternatively interest under [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981] [s.69 of the County
Courts Act 1984] accrued from [date when each sum became due] to the date of the issue of the claim and continuing
at the daily rate of £[..........] until judgment.
[or, where appropriate]
 
(2)Interest under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 in the sum of £[..........], calculated at
an annual rate of x% from [date when each sum became due] to the date of the issue of the claim, and continuing at
the daily rate of £[..........] until judgment.
[and, for contracts made after 6 August 2002]
 
(3)Sum payable under s.5A of the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998: £[..........].
 
(3)[(4)]Under paragraph 10: damages.
 
(4)[(5)]Interest on damages at the rate of x% pursuant to [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981] [s.69 of the County
Courts Act 1984].
