Paying bank’s defence alleging customer’s failure carefully to draw cheque
1.
9-E7
It is admitted that the cheque dated [date] was drawn by the Claimant payable to AB in the sum of £100, and that AB
fraudulently raised the amount of the cheque to £1,100.
2.
The Claimant owed a duty to the Defendant to take reasonable and ordinary precautions against forgery in the drawing
of the cheque.
3.
In breach of that duty, the Claimant drew the cheque in such a manner to as to enable AB to write the words “one thousand”
before the words “one hundred pounds” and to insert the figure “1” before the figure “100” on the face of the cheque.
4.
Accordingly, the Defendant was entitled to debit the Claimant’s account with the full amount of the cheque. Further or
alternatively, the Claimant’s loss was caused by his breach of duty as pleaded above.
