Chapter 16 - Sale of Shares
Rights under the contract
16-02
The express terms of the contract and the general law of contract govern the rights and liabilities of the vendor and purchaser
of shares. In the absence of express provision to the contrary, the following are the consequences of the contract:
 
(1)Where the particular shares sold are not specified in the contract, the court cannot order specific performance of the
contract in favour of the purchaser until shares have been appropriated to the contract. 6  Appropriation may possibly not
occur in many cases of sales on the Stock Exchange until the transfer form has been filled in. After the shares have been
identified, specific performance can be ordered 7  even where the purchase price is dependent on future events, such as
future profits or losses. In such a case, the court may make a modified order for specific performance, designed, for example,
to preserve the vendor’s lien for payment of the price. 8
 
(2)The vendor remains the legal owner of the shares and is to be treated by the company as the owner of them: Companies
Act 2006 s.126.
 
(3)Beneficial ownership of the shares passes to the purchaser on the making of the contract. 9  This is so even if the sale is
in breach of a right of pre-emption conferred by the articles of association of the company. 10
 
(4)The passing of the beneficial ownership means that the purchaser becomes entitled to dividends declared after the date
of the contract, even if the dividends relates to a period before the contract. 11  The vendor is entitled to dividends declared
before the contract even if paid thereafter. 12  On a sale on the Stock Exchange at a time when a dividend is about to be
declared, the contract will normally provide that the sale is to be cum div. or ex div. as the case may be.
 
(5)The vendor is liable to the company for calls made on him in respect of the shares after the date of the contract; the
purchaser (or a sub-purchaser to whom the shares have passed) is liable to indemnify him against the calls. 13  There is an
implied promise to indemnify the vendor against such calls unless the purchaser “positively negative” it. 14
 
(6)The company will only recognise the right to vote of the registered holder of shares. The vendor is entitled to cast the
votes, and the purchaser acquires a right to direct how the votes are to be cast when he has paid the purchase price. 15
 
(7)If a winding up intervenes before completion of the sale, the contract will not be specifically enforced, except in special
circumstances, because a transfer of the shares would be prima facie void under the Insolvency Act 1986 s.127. 16  However,
an agreement to sell shares may be sanctioned by the court as a scheme of arrangement, as such an agreement may amount
to an “arrangement” between the company and its members for the purposes of s.895 of the Companies Act 2006, even if
the company has purely administrative functions in relation to that arrangement. 17
 
(8)The vendor’s obligation is to deliver to the purchaser a properly executed transfer of the shares and the relevant share
certificate. There is no warranty that the company will accept the purchaser as a member, and the vendor may keep
