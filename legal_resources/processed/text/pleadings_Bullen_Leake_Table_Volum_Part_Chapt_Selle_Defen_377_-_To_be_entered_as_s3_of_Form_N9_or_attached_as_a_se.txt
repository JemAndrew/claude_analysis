Seller’s Defences
Defence to a claim for an order for repair or replacement of the goods (consumer sales only)
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F47
The Defendant admits that he entered into the agreement with the Claimant set out in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant admits that the agreement was subject to the conditions implied by [s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979]
[ss.9 and 10 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015].
3.
The Defendant denies that the goods failed to conform with the agreement either as alleged in the Particulars of Claim
or at all.
4.
It is admitted that the Claimant asserted that the goods were subject to the defects alleged in the Particulars of Claim and
that he requested the Defendant to [replace][repair] them.
5.
It is admitted that the Defendant has refused to [replace][repair] the goods. He is entitled to refuse for the following reasons
[set out the reasons relied on].
6.
It is impossible to [replace][repair] the goods for the following reasons [set out reasons] and the Defendant is not obliged
to do so under [s.48B(3)(a) of the Sale of Goods Act 1979] [s.23(3)(a) of the Consumer Rights Act 2015].
Or
6.
It is disproportionate to [replace][repair] the goods rather than to [replace][repair] them for the following reasons [set out
reasons] and the Defendant is not obliged to do so under [s.48B(3)(b) of the Sale of Goods Act 1979] [s.23(3)(b) of the
Consumer Rights Act 2015].

7.
On [date] the Defendant offered to [replace][repair] the goods but the Claimant wrongfully refused the offer. In the
circumstances, the Claimant is not entitled to the relief claimed.
Or
6.
It is disproportionate to [replace][repair] the goods in comparison with an appropriate reduction in the purchase price for
the following reasons [set out reasons] and the Defendant is not obliged to do so under [s.48B(3)(c) of the Sale of Goods
Act 1979] [s.23(4) of the Consumer Rights Act 2015].
7.
An appropriate reduction in the purchase price would be £[..........]. In a letter dated [date], a copy of which is attached to
this Defence, the Defendant tendered the said sum to the Claimant but the Claimant wrongfully refused the offer. In the
circumstances, the Claimant is not entitled to the relief claimed.
Or
6.
It is disproportionate to [replace][repair] the goods in comparison with rescission of the agreement for the following reasons
[set out reasons] and the Defendant is not obliged to do so under [s.48B(3)(c) of the Sale of Goods Act 1979] [s.23(4)
of the Consumer Rights Act 2015].
7.
In a letter dated [date], a copy of which is attached to this Defence, the Defendant offered to rescind the agreement
and tendered the purchase price to the Claimant but the Claimant wrongfully refused the offer. In the circumstances, the
Claimant is not entitled to the relief claimed.
