Need to File Request for Arbitration
29-59
It follows from art.1(6) of the Emergency Arbitrator Rules that an applicant must generally file a Request for Arbitration within
10 days after having submitted its Application to the ICC Court’s Secretariat unless the Emergency Arbitrator determines
that a longer period is necessary. This requirement was added to test from the outset the seriousness of the Application,
and thus to protect the responding party. The applicant must follow suit with its claim on the merits, if it wishes to obtain
pre-arbitral provisional relief from an Emergency Arbitrator. The drafters of the 2012 Rules wanted to avoid the risk that
a party would initiate Emergency Arbitrator Proceedings for purely tactical and possibly abusive reasons. 578  In the first
80 Emergency Arbitrator Proceedings, the Request for Arbitration was almost invariably filed either before the Emergency
Arbitration Application or within the 10-day period thereafter. On only five occasions was an extension sought and in those
cases the Request for Arbitration was filed within the extension period or the proceedings were abandoned. The President
did not dismiss any Emergency Arbitration Proceedings for the failure to meet this requirement of art.1(6) of the Emergency
Arbitrator Rules.
