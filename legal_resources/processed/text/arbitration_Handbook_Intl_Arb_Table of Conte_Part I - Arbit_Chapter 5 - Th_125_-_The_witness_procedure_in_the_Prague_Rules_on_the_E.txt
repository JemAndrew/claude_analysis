The witness procedure in the Prague Rules on the Efficient Conduct of Proceedings
26-43
The Prague Rules on the Efficient Conduct of Proceedings in International Arbitration were released on December 2018. 428
They establish evidentiary rules based primarily on civil law traditions and the inquisitorial model of procedure. With regard to
fact witnesses, art.5 of the Prague Rules emphasises tribunal-controlled process but which in reality is not any different from
the one foreseen in the IBA Rules of Evidence, as noted at para.26-31. Under arts 5(2) and 5(3), the Tribunal has control over
which witnesses are to be examined, during the hearing, for instance if it considers a testimony to be “irrelevant, immaterial,
unreasonably burdensome, duplicative or for any other reasons not necessary for the resolution of the dispute”. Similarly, under
art.5(6) of the Rules the Tribunal may decide that a witness “should not be called for examination at the hearing”.
