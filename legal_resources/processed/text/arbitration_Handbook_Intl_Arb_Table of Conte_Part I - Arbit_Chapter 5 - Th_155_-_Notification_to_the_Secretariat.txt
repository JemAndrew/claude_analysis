Part I - Arbitration Rules
Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
Notification to the Secretariat
28-80
Pursuant to art.28(2), penultimate sentence, a party seeking interim or conservatory measures from a national court must notify
the Secretariat of both the application and the measures taken without delay. The reason for this provision, which existed already
in the 1998 version of the Rules, remains somewhat unclear, as the Secretariat will normally not act in any way based on such
information, other than to inform the Tribunal thereof, which in turn, it can only do once the latter has been constituted. Typically,
the Secretariat will only inform the Tribunal of such action at the time of transmitting the file to it. The party that obtained the
interim measure from a state court may then on its own inform the Tribunal thereof. However, as long as the Tribunal is not
confronted with any request from any of the parties in relation to the state court’s interim measure, it is unlikely to take any
action of its own based on such information.
28-81
In the case of ex parte measures, it is questionable whether the Secretariat should be informed promptly after they have been
granted. The applicant can only be required to do so, once the other party has been notified of such measure. If the ex parte
measure is refused, there could possibly exist an obligation of the applicant to inform the Secretariat. In any event, one would
imagine that most parties would inform the Tribunal of such measures as well as the Secretariat, as it is the Tribunal that is
responsible for the arbitral proceedings.
