Claim against Stockbroker for Wrongfully Closing Account
1.
17-E4
The defendant is a member of the Stock Exchange.
2.
By a letter dated [date] the claimant employed the defendant as such stockbroker to purchase for him certain shares, namely
[identify] for the mid-June account day and the defendant by a letter dated [date] accepted such appointment. On [date]
the defendant purchased the said shares at, £[amount] per share.
3.
On [date] in consideration of the deposit by the claimant with the defendant of the securities [identify them] which the
claimant did deposit with the defendant accordingly, the defendant agreed with the claimant orally that the defendant would
carry over the said shares to the account day on [date].
4.
In breach of that agreement the defendant did not carry over the said shares to [date] and on [date] they wrongfully and in
breach of contract without the claimant’s instructions or authority closed the claimant’s account and sold the said shares.
5.
By reason of the foregoing the claimant has suffered loss and damage.

The claimant claims interest on the amount of the damages at such rate and for such period as the Court thinks fit pursuant
to s.31A of the Senior Courts Act 1981.
And the claimant claims damages and interest.
