“Opting Out” agreement of the Parties (lit. b)
30-34
Consistent with the principle of party autonomy and the need of the ICC to provide some flexibility in the application of its Rules,
the parties remain free to exclude the applications of the Expedited Procedure Provisions from their ICC arbitrator agreement.
To that effect, the ICC has provided express language that is to be added to the standard arbitration clause: “The Expedited
Procedure Provisions shall not apply.” Such or similar language, from which one can imply the parties’ wish not to be subject
to the Expedited Procedure Provisions should therefore be used whenever the parties wanted to exclude the application of the
Expedited Procedure Provisions.
30-35
The agreement to opt out of the Expedited Procedure Provisions can be reached when making the original ICC arbitration
agreement, as well as at any time thereafter until the start of an ICC arbitration.
