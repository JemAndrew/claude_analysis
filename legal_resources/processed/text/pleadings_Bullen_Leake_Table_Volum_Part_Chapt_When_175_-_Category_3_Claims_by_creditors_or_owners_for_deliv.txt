When the “Consumer Credit Act Procedure” (“CCA Procedure”) applies
Category 3: Claims by creditors or owners for delivery of goods supplied under regulated hire-purchase and
conditional sale agreements
20-45
These claims must be brought in the county court and the claim form must contain the information in the format prescribed by
CPR PD 7B.7.2.(2), and PD 16.6.1 makes it obligatory to use the procedure in CPR PD 7B.7.2.(2) in place of PD 16.6. With
regulated agreements, all defendants will, of course, be non-corporate.
The CCA Procedure applies so that the particulars of claim must be served with the claim form which must include or be
accompanied by a notice of the hearing date. No default judgment is possible.
Where the goods are protected and s.90(1) applies, PD 7B.8 provides a special procedure for the defendant to admit the claim
and offer terms for a suspended possession order using a request in Form N9C. The admission is served on the claimant who
can accept the offer and obtain judgment by an application in Form N228 or can refuse it, in which case the admission can be
used as evidence at any hearing where the defendant fails to attend.
