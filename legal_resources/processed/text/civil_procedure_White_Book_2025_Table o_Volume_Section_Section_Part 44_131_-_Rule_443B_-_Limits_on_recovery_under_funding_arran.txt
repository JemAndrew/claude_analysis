Rule 44_3b
44.3B— Limits on recovery under funding arrangements 1
7B-12+
(1) Unless the court orders otherwise, a party may not recover as an additional liability—
(a) any proportion of the percentage increase relating to the cost to the legal representative of the postponement
of the payment of his fees and expenses;
(b) any provision made by a membership organisation which exceeds the likely cost to that party of the premium
of an insurance policy against the risk of incurring a liability to pay the costs of other parties to the proceedings;
(c) any additional liability for any period during which that party failed to provide information about a funding
arrangement in accordance with a rule, practice direction or court order;
(d) any percentage increase where that party has failed to comply with—
(i) a requirement in the Costs Practice Direction; or
(ii) a court order,
to disclose in any assessment proceedings the reasons for setting the percentage increase at the level stated in the
conditional fee agreement;
(e) any insurance premium where that party has failed to provide information about the insurance policy in
question by the time required by a rule, practice direction or court order.
(Paragraph 9.3 of the Practice Direction (Pre-Action Conduct) provides that a party must inform any other party as
soon as possible about a funding arrangement entered into before the start of proceedings.)
(2) This rule does not apply in an assessment under rule 48.9 (assessment of a solicitor’s bill to his client).
(Rule 3.9 sets out the circumstances the court will consider on an application for relief from a sanction for failure to
comply with any rule, practice direction or court order.)

The rule sets out the limits on recovery under the funding arrangements. Any proportion of the percentage increase in a
conditional fee agreement which relates to the fact that payment of fees and expenses is delayed is not recoverable. In relation
to provision made by a membership organisation, no more than the cost of a premium of an insurance policy covering the risk
of having to pay the costs of other parties to the proceedings is recoverable. Recovery of additional liability is not possible for
any period where the party has failed to provide information about a funding arrangement.
As explained above (para.7B-6+), r.44.3B is a provision “relating to funding arrangements” and applies in relation to “a pre-
commencement funding arrangement” as it was in force immediately before 1 April 2013. The rule is supplemented by Section
10 of the Costs Practice Direction, para.7BPD-4+.
For claims arising out of road traffic accidents occurring after 5 October 2003, the percentage increase to be allowed is to be
determined in accordance with Pt 45 Section II (Road Traffic Accidents—Fixed Recoverable Costs).
Failure to provide information or to comply with direction etc
7B-12.
2+
Provisions as to the providing of information about funding arrangements are contained in r.44.15 and Section 19 of the Costs
Practice Direction (supplementing r.44.15) (see para.7BPD-5+). Section 32 of the Costs Practice Direction (supplementing
r.47.6) contains provisions stipulating the information that be provided to the court where a detailed assessment is commenced
in respect of additional liability. See further para.7B-16.1+.
Paragraph (1)(c) of r.44.3B(1) states that where there is a failure to provide information about a funding arrangement in
accordance with a rule, practice direction or court order a party may not recover any additional liability (unless the court orders
otherwise). Paragraph (1)(e) of the rule limits recovery where there is a failure to provide information about any insurance
premium in the time required by a rule etc. (No costs may be recovered in respect of an insurance premium in the circumstances
provided for by r.44.12B. (Costs-only proceedings—costs in respect of insurance premium in publication cases).)
Paragraph (1)(d) of r.44.3B is restricted to additional liability in the form of “percentage increase” and to failures to make the
particular disclosure referred to therein in any assessment proceedings (whether summary or detailed) as required (not by any
rule etc. but) by the Costs Practice Direction or a court order.
The claimant failed to serve either a statement of the reasons for the percentage increase or a copy of the risk assessment, then
required by para.32.5 of the Costs Practice Direction, with the notice of commencement, but did serve this further information
shortly after the defendants had served their points of dispute. On appeal from the costs judge’s decision to refuse relief
from sanctions, it was held that the further information should have been served with the notice of commencement and that
accordingly there had been a failure to comply with the practice direction. The sanction for that failure was that set out in
r.44.3B(1)(c), rather than 44.3B(1)(d). The breach was insignificant and trivial: Long v Value Properties Limited [2014] EWHC
2981 (Ch), Barling J.
A party affected may apply for relief from the sanctions imposed by paras (1)(c), (1)(d) or (1)(e), in which event, as the
parenthesis at the end of r.44.3B indicates, CPR r.3.9 applies. That rule was amended by the Civil Procedure (Amendment)
Rules 2013 (SI 2013/262); the amendments apply to any applications for relief from any sanction imposed for a failure to
comply with any rule, practice direction or court order, made on or after 1 April 2013, but not before. Where the application
for relief from sanction relates to para.(1)(c) or para.(1)(d), the provisions of Section 10 of the Costs Practice Direction apply
(see para.7BPD-4+).
Paragraph 9.3 of Practice Direction (Pre-Action Conduct) stated that, where a party enters into a funding arrangement within
the meaning of r.43.2(1)(k), that party must inform the other parties about this arrangement as soon as possible and in any event
either within 7 days of entering into the funding arrangement concerned or, where a claimant enters into a funding arrangement
before sending a letter before claim, in the letter before claim. By CPR Update 66 that provision was removed with effect from
1 October 2013, as an amendment made necessary as a consequence of the costs reforms that came into effect on 1 April 2013.
In Caliendo v Mishcon de Reya [2014] EWHC 3414 (Ch); [2014] 6 Costs L.O. 935 (Hildyard J.), the claimant should have
complied with para.9.3 by notifying the defendants of one funding arrangement by 27 February 2013, and of another by 18

March 2013, but failed to do so, and eventually gave such notice of both in their letter before action sent on 11 June 2013.
The court granted the claimant’s application under r.3.9 for relief from the sanction imposed by the effect of r.44.3B(1)(c) and
(e). The decision was upheld by the Court of Appeal: [2015] EWCA Civ 1029. However, in Springer v University Hospitals of
Leicester NHS Trust [2018] EWCA Civ 436; [2018] 4 W.L.R. 61, CA, the Court of Appeal concluded that “as soon as possible”
in para.9.3 (see para.C1-010.1) meant just that, with the consequence that the claimant would be unable to recover additional
liabilities in the period before notice of funding was given.
In Montlake v Lambert Smith Hampton Group Ltd [2004] EWHC 1503 (Comm), [2004] 4 Costs L.R. 650 (Langley J), where
the paying party had from the outset the information to which it was entitled, in relation to funding arrangements, the court
found that there was no prejudice to that party from breaches of para.10.1 of the Costs Practice Direction (no formal notice
had been given) and the receiving party was entitled to relief from the sanction provided for by r.44.3B(1)(c) and was not to be
deprived of the opportunity, in principle, to recover the agreed success fee.
The nature and extent of the relevant details of additional liability required by Section 32 of the Costs Practice Direction to be
served in detailed assessment proceedings were examined in Light on Line Ltd v Zumtobel Lighting Ltd [2012] EWHC 3376
(QB); [2013] 1 Costs L.R. 129 (Slade J and assessors), where a costs judge found that proper information in support of an
insurance premium had not been served in time by the claimants and held that, under the terms of r.44.3B(1)(e), the premium
was not recoverable, and where, on appeal, the High Court held that the claimants should be granted relief from sanction. For
text of Section 32 see para.47PD.34 above.
