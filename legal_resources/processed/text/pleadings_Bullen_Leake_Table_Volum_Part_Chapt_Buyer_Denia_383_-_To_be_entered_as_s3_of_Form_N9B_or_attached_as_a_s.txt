Chapter 25 - Supply of Goods and Digital Content
Buyer’s Defences
Denial of any delivery and denial of delivery on time
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9B or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F53
The Defendant admits that he entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Claimant agreed to sell and
deliver to the Defendant the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant denies that the Claimant has delivered the goods or any of them to the Defendant.
Or
2.
It was an express condition of the agreement that the Claimant should deliver the goods on or before [date 1]. In breach
of the condition, the Claimant failed to deliver the goods or any of them by [date 1] and did not tender delivery until [date
2]. The Defendant was therefore entitled to reject the goods and did so orally on tender [and by a letter dated [date] (copy
annexed to this Defence)].
3.
In the premises, the Claimant is not entitled to the purchase price or any part thereof.
