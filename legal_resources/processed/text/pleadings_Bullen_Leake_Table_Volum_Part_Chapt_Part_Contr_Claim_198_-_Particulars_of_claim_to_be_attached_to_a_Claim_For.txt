Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
20-F13
By an agreement in writing dated [date], the Claimant advanced to the Defendant the sum of £[..........]. A copy of the
agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
2.
The agreement was regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 and this is a Consumer Credit Act claim.
3.
The said loan, together with a total charge for credit of £[..........] was payable by equal consecutive monthly instalments
of £[..........].
4.
By clause 6, the agreement provided that, if the Defendant failed to pay any instalment on the due date, the Claimant might
serve upon him a notice of default and termination requiring the Defendant to remedy the breach within 14 days and giving
notice that, if he failed to do so, upon the expiry of the period, in addition to any instalments accrued due, the Defendant
should become liable to pay the balance of the principal sum lent and the balance of the said charge for credit subject to
a rebate of charges, if appropriate to be calculated at the day of payment.
5.
The Defendant failed to pay the instalments due from [date] to [date] in the total sum of £[..........].
6.
By a notice of default and termination dated [date] (copy annexed to these Particulars of Claim), the Claimant required the
Defendant to remedy his breach within 14 days and gave notice that, in default of so doing, the Defendant should be liable
to pay the said arrears of £[..........] and the balance of the loan and charges of £[..........] (subject to a rebate, if appropriate,
to be calculated at the date of payment).

7.
The Defendant failed to remedy the breach within the said period and on the expiry of the period became liable to pay
the Claimant the sums of £[..........] and £[..........].
8.
The Defendant has failed to pay the said sums or any part thereof.
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)£[..........] arrears of instalments.
 
(2)£[..........] balance of the loan and charges.
 
(3)£[..........] contractual interest accrued from [date when each sum became due] to the date of the issue of the claim
and continuing at the daily rate of £[..........] until judgment.
[Statement of truth]
Note
This form incorporates the decision of the Court of Appeal in Forward Trust Ltd v Whymark [1990] 2 Q.B. 670. Where the
agreement is regulated, it is no longer appropriate to make an allowance for accelerated repayment in the claim form. The
Claimant should seek the full amount due on termination and the judgment will be given for that amount, any rebate being
given on settlement or enforcement of the judgment.
