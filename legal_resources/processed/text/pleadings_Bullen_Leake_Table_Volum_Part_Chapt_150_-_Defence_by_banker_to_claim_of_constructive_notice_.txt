Defence by banker to claim of constructive notice of undue influence by third party; bank relying on “reasonable
steps” taken
1.
18-E8
The Bank is unable to admit or deny the allegations of undue influence by Mr A over the Claimant at the time that the
Claimant executed the Guarantee.
2.
The Bank denies that it had constructive notice of the alleged undue influence. The Bank avers that it took reasonable
steps to satisfy itself that the Claimant had had brought home to her the nature and effect of the proposed transaction, as
set out below.
3.
The Bank wrote to the Claimant on [date] enquiring whether she had a solicitor acting for her in the proposed transaction.
In that letter, the Bank stated that, for its own protection, it would require confirmation from a solicitor, acting for the
Claimant, that that solicitor had explained the nature and practical effect of the documents to the Claimant. That letter
further asked the Claimant to nominate a solicitor willing to act for her. A copy of that letter is attached hereto marked “A”.
4.
The Claimant wrote to the Bank on [date] stating that Messrs [name] acted for her. A copy of that letter is attached hereto
marked “B”.
5.
On [date], the Bank wrote to the principal debtor asking for his consent to release the following information to the solicitors
acting for the Claimant: the purpose of the facility which it was proposed that the Claimant would secure, the current
amount of the principal debtor’s indebtedness, the amount of the principal debtor’s current overdraft facility, the amount
and terms of the new facility and a copy of the application for the new facility. That letter is attached hereto marked “C”.
6.
On [date], the principal debtor wrote to the Bank giving his consent to such release. That letter is attached hereto marked
“D”.
