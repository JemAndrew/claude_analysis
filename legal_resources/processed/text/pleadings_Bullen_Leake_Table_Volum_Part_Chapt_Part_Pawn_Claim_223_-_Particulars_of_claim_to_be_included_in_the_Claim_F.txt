Particulars of claim to be included in the Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
20-F37
The Defendant carries on the business of a pawnbroker.
2.
On [date] the Claimant entered into two pawn transactions with the Defendant namely:
 
(a)the Claimant deposited [identify the goods] (“Goods A”) to secure a loan of £[..........]
 
(b)the Claimant deposited [identify the goods] (“Goods B”) to secure a loan of £[..........].
3.
In respect of each pawn transaction, the Defendant issued to the Claimant a form of receipt (copies annexed to these
Particulars of Claim).
4.
On [date] the Claimant tendered to the Defendant the sums necessary to redeem Goods A together with the form of receipt.
In breach of its duty under s.117 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”), the Defendant refused to deliver up Goods
A and unlawfully continued to detain them. In the premises, the Defendant has unlawfully converted Goods A to its own
use and benefit alternatively has wrongfully interfered with them.
5.
At the date of the Claimant’s demands to redeem Goods A, their value was £[..........].
6.
The Claimant failed to redeem Goods B by the end of the redemption period, namely [date]. In breach of s.121 of the CCA,
the Defendant failed to serve on the Claimant any notice of its intention to sell Goods B and on [date] sold it for £[..........]
purporting to charge expenses of sale of £[..........].

7.
At [date] the amount due in respect of Goods B was £[..........]. Its true market value was £[..........] and the expenses of
sale claimed by the Defendant were unreasonable.
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)An order that, upon the Claimant tendering to the Defendant the sum necessary to redeem Goods A as at [date] and
the pawn receipt the Defendant do forthwith deliver up Goods A to the Claimant alternatively pay to the Claimant
£[..........] their value.
 
(2)Further or alternatively to (1): damages for conversion and/or wrongful interference with Goods A.
 
(3)A declaration that, at [date] the true market value of Goods B was £[..........].
 
(4)An inquiry as to what would have been the proper expenses of the sale thereof.
 
(5)A declaration that the Claimant’s liability to the Defendant in respect of Goods B and the loan secured by its pawn
have been discharged and extinguished.
 
(6)An order for the payment by the Defendant to the Claimant of the sum by which the said value of £[..........] less
the proper expenses of sale, exceeds the amount due on [date] to redeem Goods B.
 
(7)Damages.
 
(8)Interest at the rate of x% pursuant to s.69 of the County Courts Act.
