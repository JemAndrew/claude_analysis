Surety’s Defences
Defence of revocation of security
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
27-F13
The Defendant admits that he entered into the contract of guarantee set out in the Particulars of Claim (“the guarantee”),
whereby he guaranteed the liabilities of XY Limited to the Claimant in respect of its overdraft with the Claimant.
2.
The Defendant admits receipt of the letter of demand dated [date] referred to in the Particulars of Claim. For the reasons
set out below, he is not liable to the Claimant in the sum claimed or any sum.
3.
By clause X of the guarantee, the guarantee was revocable on immediate notice.
4.
On [date] the Defendant gave immediate notice revoking the guarantee. On the said date the account of XY Limited was
in credit.
5.
The Defendant is not therefore liable to the Claimant in respect of any sums advanced by the Claimant to XY Limited
following such revocation.
Or
3.
By clause X of the guarantee, the guarantee was revocable on 90 days’ notice.
4.
On [date] the Defendant gave 90 days’ notice to revoke the guarantee, such notice expiring on [date]. Prior to the expiry
of such notice, the Claimant made no demand for payment either on XY Limited or on the Defendant.
