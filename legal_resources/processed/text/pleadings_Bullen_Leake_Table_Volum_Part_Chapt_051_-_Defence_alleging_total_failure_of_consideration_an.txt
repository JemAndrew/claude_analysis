Defence alleging total failure of consideration, and denying status of claimant as holder in due course or holder for
value
1.
10-E9
It is admitted that the Defendant accepted the bill relied on by the Claimant, which was drawn by the AB and made payable
to himself.
2.
By an exchange of faxes on 14 February 2000 (attached and marked A) AB agreed to sell and the Defendant agreed to
buy a consignment of machine parts (the goods) conforming to the specifications there set out and in particular that they
should be [set out particulars]. The Defendant accepted the bill drawn upon him in payment of the price for the goods.
3.
Upon delivery of the goods to the Defendant he rejected them, as he was entitled to do, as they did not conform to the
description agreed in that [set out particulars]. In the circumstances the consideration for the Defendant’s acceptance has
wholly failed.
4.
The Claimant gave no consideration for the negotiation to him of the bill. It is therefore denied that the Claimant is a holder
in due course or for value of the bill.
5.
In the circumstances it is denied that the Defendant is liable as alleged or at all.
