Claim for breach of the implied conditions of reasonable fitness and satisfactory quality (buyer not a consumer)
Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
25-F18
The Claimant carries on business as a [identify business] and the Defendant carries on business as a [identify business].
2.
By a contract in writing made [date] between the Claimant and the Defendant, the Defendant agreed to sell to the Claimant
[identify the goods sold] (“the goods”) for the sum of £[..........].
3.
The contract was contained in the Defendant’s standard contract form completed with details of the transaction and signed
by both parties. A copy of the contract is annexed to this Claim Form.
4.
At the time of the contract, the Defendant well knew that the Claimant was buying the goods for use in his business.
5.
There were implied conditions of the contract that
 
(a)the goods should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Claimant required them, namely for use in his
business;
 
(b)the goods should be of satisfactory quality.
These conditions were implied into the contract by reason of the Sale of Goods Act 1979, s.14.
6.
In pursuance of the contract, the Defendant delivered the goods to the Claimant and the Claimant paid the sum of £[..........].
7.
In breach of the conditions of the contract, the goods were not reasonably fit for the purpose for which they were required
nor were they of satisfactory quality.
