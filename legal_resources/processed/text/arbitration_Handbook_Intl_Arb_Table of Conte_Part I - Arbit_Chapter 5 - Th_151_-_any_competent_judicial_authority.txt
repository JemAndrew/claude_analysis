Part I - Arbitration Rules
Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
“any competent judicial authority”
28-68
Article 28(2) permits parties to seek interim or conservatory measures from a judicial authority. The reference to a judicial
authority is aimed in particular at state courts. However, it could extend to any entity with adjudicating authority, including for
example, administrative bodies. The judicial authority may be at the place of arbitration or in any other relevant jurisdiction,
such as the other party’s place of business or the place where the relevant contract is being performed.
