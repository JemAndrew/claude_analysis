Defence alleging that bill was drawn pursuant to a misrepresentation, and denying status of claimant
1.
10-E10
It is admitted that the Defendant drew the Bill sued upon and payable to AB.
2.
The Defendant drew the bill in reliance upon AB’s representation made orally that [set out particulars].
3.
AB’s representation was false in that [complete as appropriate] and was made fraudulently in that [complete as appropriate].
[Generally, state case in the form appropriate to a general contractual defence.]
4.
The Bill was negotiated to the Claimant after it was overdue. In the circumstances the Claimant is not a holder in due
course, but took the Bill subject to the Defendant’s right to rescind it.
5.
It is therefore denied that the Defendant is liable upon the bill or to the Claimant as alleged or at all.
