Regulated agreements: events after the making of the contract
Defence of failure to give notice of change of rate of interest under CCA s.78A
To be inserted in s.4 of Form 9B or 9D or contained in a separate document
1.
20-F61
The Defendant admits that he entered into the agreement with the Claimant set out in the Particulars of Claim. The
agreement was an agreement regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”).
2.
The Defendant denies that he was in breach of the agreement as alleged in the Particulars of Claim or at all.
3.
The claim relates to sums alleged to be due in respect of interest following a purported increase in the rate of interest
said to have occurred on [date]. It is admitted [denied] that the agreement entitled the Claimant to vary the rate of interest
charged on the credit.
4.
Immediately prior to [date] the rate of interest payable by the Defendant was x%. Contrary to CCA s.78A, the Claimant
failed to notify the Defendant in writing of the variation of the rate of interest and/or the variation of the amount of the
periodical payments to be made under the agreement and/or the variation of the number and frequency of the periodical
payments to be made under the agreement.
5.
Under CCA s.78A, therefore, none of the said variations could take effect and the Defendant was entitled to continue
making payments at the rate and frequency existing on [date]. Those payments having been made, the Claimant is entitled
to no further payment either as claimed or at all.
