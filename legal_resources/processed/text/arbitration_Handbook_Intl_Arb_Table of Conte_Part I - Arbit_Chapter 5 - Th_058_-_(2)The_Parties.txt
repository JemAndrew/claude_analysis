22-10
Article 22(1) expressly requires the parties to make every effort to conduct the arbitration in an expeditious and cost-effective
manner having regard to the complexity and the value of the dispute. However, in accordance with art.21(4) and applicable law,
a party is entitled to have a reasonable opportunity to present its case. As discussed under art.21(4), what is reasonable should
be viewed in the light of the Rules, ICC practice and applicable law.
22-11
In an ICC arbitration parties almost invariably act through their legal representatives. Therefore, the obligation under art.21(1)
requires the parties to ensure that their legal representatives meet its requirements. The IBA’s Representation Guidelines
(2013) 192  seek to provide guidance as to basic obligations. The 2021 ICC Note recommends that the parties and the Tribunal
be inspired by and adopt those guidelines. 193
22-12
At the outset of the proceedings, the parties’ obligation under art.22(1) is to cooperate under art.23 to seek to finalise the Terms
of Reference within 30 days after transmittal of the file to the Tribunal; to conduct the initial case management conference and to
establish the procedural timetable as provided for in art.24. During the course of the arbitration, the parties should comply with
the periods set out in the Procedural Timetable subject to new developments that may require an adjustment to the Procedural
Timetable to ensure that each party has a reasonable opportunity to present its case. After the award has been rendered, the
parties are required to comply with the provisions of art.36 regarding correction, interpretation and remission of an award in
particular by not raising issues that do not fall within the scope of that article.
22-13
The other specific issues that involve the cooperation of the parties relate in particular to the taking of evidence discussed in
art.25 and the conduct of hearings discussed in art.26.

In case the parties employ such dilatory techniques, the sanctions may be reflected in the allocation of costs depending on “the
extent to which each party has conducted the arbitration in an expeditious and cost-effective manner” pursuant to art.38(5). 194
More importantly, art.22(1) constitutes a clear basis upon which a Tribunal may rely in justifying its procedural decisions
regarding requests made by the parties. If such requests are not conducive to an expeditious and cost-efficient conduct of the
proceedings, and absent any over-riding reasons, due, for example, to special circumstances, a Tribunal may refuse such requests
on that basis.
