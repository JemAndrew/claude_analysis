Claim for money lent plus contractual interest
1.
14-E2
By an oral agreement made on or around [date] between the Claimant and the Defendant during a meeting at the Defendant’s
offices at [address], the Claimant agreed to lend the Defendant the sum of £20,000, and the Defendant agreed to repay
this sum by [repayment date] (“the Agreement”). The Agreement is evidenced in writing by the following documents: [set
out details of relevant documents].
2.
It was an express term of the Agreement that the Defendant would pay interest on the sum of £20,000 at the rate of [..........]
per cent per annum [as from the date of the loan].
3.
In performance of the Agreement, the Claimant drew a cheque in favour of the Defendant and paid the cheque over to the
Defendant by way of loan on [date of loan]. The cheque was duly met on presentation.
4.
On [date], the Defendant repaid the Claimant the sum of £1,750. However, no further repayments have been made by
[repayment date] or at all, and accordingly the Claimant is entitled to and claims the sum of £18,250.
5.
Further the Claimant is entitled to and claims interest at the agreed rate from [date] to [date], amounting to £[..........] at
[date] and continuing at the daily rate of £[..........] until judgment or sooner payment.
AND the Claimant claims:
 
(1)£18,250;
 
(2)Interest at the contractual rate, under paragraph 2 above.
