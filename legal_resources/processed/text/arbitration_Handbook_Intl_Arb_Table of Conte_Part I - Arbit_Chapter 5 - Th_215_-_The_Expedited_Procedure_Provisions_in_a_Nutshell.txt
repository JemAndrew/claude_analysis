The Expedited Procedure Provisions in a Nutshell
30-9
Articles 30(2) and 30(3), together with art.1 of App.VI, define the conditions for the expedited procedure regime to apply.
Pursuant to art.30(2), the Expedited Procedure Rules shall apply either: (a) where the amount in dispute does not exceed the
limit set out in art.1(2) of Appendix VI at the time of the submission of the Answer to the Request for Arbitration (i.e., currently
defined as US$2 million if the arbitration agreement was concluded on or after 1 March 2017 and before 1 January 2021, and
US$3 million if the arbitration agreement was concluded on or after 1 January 2021); or (b) where the parties so agree.
30-10
Article 30(3) in turn provides that the Expedited Procedure Provisions shall not apply: (a) if the arbitration agreement was
concluded before the date on which they came into force, i.e., before 1 March 2017; (b) if the parties have agreed to opt out of
them; or (c) if the ICC Court, upon the request of a party before the constitution of the Tribunal or on its own motion, determines
that it is inappropriate in the circumstances to apply them.
