Editorial introduction
7B-5+
As the title to this Part and the text of r.44.1 indicate, this Part contains general rules about costs, in particular rules as to
entitlement to costs and the assessment of costs. In the period since this Part was first brought into effect on 26 April 1999,
some of the original rules have undergone significant amendments. In that period, rules added to this Part are: rr.44.3A, 44.3B,
44.3C, 44.12A, 44.12B and 44.12C, and rr.44.15 to 45.20 (the purposes of those additional rules are briefly explained in the
commentaries relating to them).
Post-April 1, 2013 application
7B-6+
With effect from 1 April 2013, by r.16 of the Civil Procedure (Amendment) Rules 2013 (SI 2013/262), CPR Pts 43 to 48 were
substituted by Pts 44 to 48 as set out in the Schedule to that statutory instrument. By CPR Update 60, practice directions were
made supplementing Pts 44 to 48 as re-enacted, and substituting the Costs Practice Direction. However, by operation of statutory
transitional and savings provisions, various rules in Pts 43 to 48 as they stood before 1 April 2013, and the directions in the
Costs Practice Direction that supplemented them, for an indefinite period continue to have effect for certain purposes.
For detailed explanation of the transitional and savings provisions underlying Part 48, see paras 48.0.1 to 48.0.3 (“Continuing
application of CPR Parts 43 to 38 and Costs Practice Direction as in force immediately before 1 April 2013”).
The relevant savings provisions are dealt with in Pt 48 as substituted by the Civil Procedure (Amendment) Rules 2013 (SI
2013/262), and brought into effect on 1 April 2013, where r.48.1(1) states that the provisions “relating to funding arrangements”
in Pt 44 (and in Pt 43 and Pts 45 to 48), and the attendant provisions of the Costs Practice Direction, as they were in force
immediately before 1 April 2013, will continue to apply on and after that date in relation to “a pre-commencement funding
arrangement” (as defined in r.48.2) as they were in force immediately before that date. (This is subject to such modifications (if
any) as may be made subsequently to those rules and directions. It is expressly provided in r.48.1(1) such modifications may
be made by practice direction.)
Rules as in force before 1 April 2013, in Part 44 “relating to funding arrangements” and which apply in relation to “a pre-
commencement funding arrangement” include the following:
 
44.3ACosts orders relating to funding arrangements (supplemented by Section 9 of the CPD)
 
44.3BLimits on recovery under funding arrangements (supplemented by Section 10 of the CPD)
 
44.12BCosts-only proceedings—costs in respect of insurance premium in publication cases
 
44.15Providing information about funding arrangements (supplemented by Section 19 of the CPD)

Post-April 1 2013 destinations
7B-7+
Key provisions in Pt 44, as it stood before 1 April 2013, are r.44.3 (Court’s discretion and circumstances to be taken into account
when exercising its discretion as to costs) and r.44.4 (Basis of assessment). In Pt 44, as re-enacted by SI 2013/262 with effect
from 1 April 2013, the destinations of those rules are, respectively, rr.44.2 and 44.3.
Upon the re-enactment of the CPR Costs Rules by SI 2013/262, with effect from 1 April 2013, rr.44.18, 44.19 and 44.20 were
moved to Pt 3 (The Court’s Case and Costs Management Powers) where they constitute Section III (Costs Capping) of that
Part; see r.3.19 and following, and related commentary. Rule 44.17 is no longer of practical effect.
