Claim by payee against maker of promissory note payable by instalments with clause for default
1.
10-E25
On 31 January 2015 the Defendant made and delivered to the Claimant a promissory note (“the Note”, a copy of which
is attached marked “A”) by which the Defendant promised:
 
(i)To pay the sum of £1,000 by five instalments of £200 each payable on March 31, and the last day of the four
succeeding months thereafter, and
 
(ii)That in default of the payment of any instalment the whole of the balance of the sum of £1,000 should become
immediately due and payable.
2.
The Claimant paid the sum of £200 on 31 March 2000, but thereafter failed to pay the instalment due on 30 April 2000,
or any subsequent instalments, or the balance, which remains due.
3.
The Claimant is therefore entitled to and claims:
 
(1)The sum of £800 due under the Note,
 
(2)Interest thereon pursuant to s.57(1) of the Bills of Exchange Act 1882 at a rate of 1 per cent over base rate, presently
amounting to [..........] per cent, and amounting to the date hereof to £[..........] and continuing at a rate of £[..........]
per day; alternatively interest pursuant to s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981 in the same amount or such amount
as to the Court shall seem fit.
