Incidents of the cause of action
12-15
Where FSMA confers a right of action for breach of statutory duty, 152  it is expressed to be subject to the “defences and other
incidents applying to actions for breach of statutory duty”. Since the right of action is conferred by FSMA itself, there is no
need to go through the often-complex process of determining whether the statute confers a private law cause of action on a
claimant, or whether such cause of action has been conferred by delegated legislation. 153  An important incident of a claim
for breach of statutory duty is that the action is not “for negligence in the strict or ordinary sense”, but is more like strict or
absolute liability. 154  This observation does not, of course, apply to the statutory causes of action which depend on the duty of
an authorised person to exercise reasonable care (ss.56(6) and 59(1) and (2)). Equally, a claim under s.138D which relates to a
breach of an FCA rule importing a requirement of reasonable care cannot be categorised as a strict liability claim. To establish
a breach, the nature of the obligation under the relevant rule must be determined. 155
