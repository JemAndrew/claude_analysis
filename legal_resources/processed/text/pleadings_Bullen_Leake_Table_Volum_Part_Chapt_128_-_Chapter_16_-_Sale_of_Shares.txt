Chapter 16 - Sale of Shares
16-01
This title concerns the enforcement of contracts for the sale of shares in companies registered under the Companies Act 2006
(and earlier Companies Acts) in which the liability of the members is limited by the amount, if any, unpaid on the shares held
by them (“companies limited by shares”: see s.3(2) of the Companies Act 2006). Companies are required to number the shares
in issue sequentially unless all the shares, or all the shares in a particular class, are fully paid up and rank pari passu. 1  Such
shares are personal estate and not real estate; they are transferable in the manner and subject to the conditions laid down in the
articles of association of the company, in the Stock Transfer Act 1963 (simplified forms of transfer), or in regulations under
Ch.2 of Pt 21 of the Companies Act 2006 (regulations relating to dematerialised transfers). 2  The company may not register a
transfer unless a proper instrument of transfer has been delivered to it, or the transfer is an exempt transfer within the Stock
Transfer Act 1982 or is in accordance with the regulations concerning dematerialised transfers. 3
A contract for the sale of shares may be made orally or in writing, 4  and there is no requirement for any formality in the
formation of the contract. 5  In sales on the London Stock Exchange and in other markets, the rules of the exchange are generally
incorporated in the contract, so that the principals of each broker (to the extent that they are acting as agents) “drop out”, leaving
vendor and purchaser as the principal parties to the contract.
