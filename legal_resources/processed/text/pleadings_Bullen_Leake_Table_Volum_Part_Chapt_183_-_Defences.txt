1.contractual defences;
 
2.defences arising from the CCA.
The contractual defences are straightforward and require little explanation. In most cases the contractual defences will relate to
misrepresentations made before the contract was entered into or breaches of express or implied conditions or warranties as to the
condition, fitness or description of the goods or services which are the subject-matter of the credit agreement. See the paragraphs
“Claims relating to faulty goods and services” above. The provisions relating to “distance selling” (see para.20-14) afford new
defences based on cancellation or deemed cancellation as do the rules relating to “off-premises” contracts (see para.20-15).
