25-F24
The Defendant is the manufacturer of [identify the class or type of goods concerned]. All such goods were sold with a
“manufacturer’s guarantee” (“the guarantee”), a copy of which is attached to this Claim.
2.
By the terms of the guarantee, the Defendant undertook that, if any goods to which the guarantee applied did not conform
to the specifications set out in the guarantee, it would replace such goods or reimburse the purchase price paid in respect
of them.
3.
On or about [date] the Claimant entered into an oral agreement with the PQ Limited at its premises at [address] whereby
the Claimant bought [set out the goods bought] for a price of £[..........]. The goods were sold by PQ Limited with the
benefit of the guarantee and the Claimant was furnished with a copy of the guarantee.
4.
The Claimant bought the goods as a consumer and the guarantee was a consumer guarantee for the purposes of [the Sale
and Supply of Goods to Consumers Regulations 2002 (“the Regulations”)] [s.30 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015].
5.
In breach of the guarantee, the goods did not conform to the specifications set out in the guarantee.
