Defence

25-F67
The Defendant admits that he entered into the agreement with the Claimant set out in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
In entering into the agreement the Defendant dealt as a consumer.
3.
Under [s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979 (“the 1979 Act”), the following were implied terms of the agreement] [s.9 of
the 2015 Act (“the 2015 Act”) the following were terms of the agreement]:
 
(a)that the goods should be reasonably fit for the purpose for which the Defendant required them;
 
(b)that the goods should be of satisfactory quality.
4.
In breach of the said terms the goods were not reasonably fit for the said purpose or of satisfactory quality.

7.
Accordingly it was the Claimant’s obligation to [replace] [repair] the goods within a reasonable time and without causing

8.

Or
8.
In breach of that obligation, in a telephone conversation on [date] the Claimant orally [refused to replace the goods unless
the Defendant transported them to the Claimant’s warehouse in [address] at his own expense] [refused to repair the goods
unless the Defendant agreed to pay for parts and labour].
9.
In the circumstances, the Defendant is entitled to an order requiring the Claimant to [replace] [repair] the goods.
10.
In the alternative, the Defendant will seek rescission of the agreement and remission of the purchase price.
11.
The Defendant admits that the purchase price remains unpaid but will say by reason of the matters set out above that he
is entitled to withhold the price until the Claimant has performed his obligations under [Part 5A of the 1979 Act] [s.23
