FSMA enacts expressly that the illegality of various forms of contravention do not render a resulting transaction void or voidable
or unenforceable except to the extent expressly provided in the Act. Where the claim is for breach of statutory duty, the following
defences may arise:
 
(i)Defences created by the statute itself;
 
(ii)The causation of loss by the claimant’s own act (this requirement is effectively embodied in the relevant sections that
the loss claimed must have been suffered in consequence of the relevant breach of the Act or the rules 156 );
 
(iii)Contributory negligence (this is likely to be rarely established in a claim under the Act);
 
(iv)The loss was caused by the intervening act of a third person, at least if it was unforeseeable. 157

156
As to causation and remoteness, see Rubenstein v HSBC Bank Ltd [2012] EWCA Civ 1184; [2013] 1 All E.R. (Comm)
915 where the Court of Appeal held that the first instance judge had erred in concluding that the loss suffered by the
investor on his capital was caused by unprecedented market turmoil and was therefore unforeseeable and too remote.
The Court of Appeal held that the market turmoil and its impact on the investor were foreseeable.
