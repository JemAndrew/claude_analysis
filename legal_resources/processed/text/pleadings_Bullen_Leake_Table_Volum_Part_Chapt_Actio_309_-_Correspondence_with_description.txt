Chapter 25 - Supply of Goods and Digital Content
Action for breach of condition or warranty
Correspondence with description
25-25
SGA s.13 implies into a contract for the sale of goods by description a condition that the goods will correspond with the
description. In practice, most sales are sales by description; the problem in pleading a sale by description is to formulate with
precision the description applied to the goods in question. There can also be difficulties where the seller has made statements
about the goods prior to purchase, in determining what statements form part of the contractual description and what are pre-
contractual representations not subsequently incorporated into the contract. 14  It is worth noting that selling a work of art as by
a named artist is not necessarily a sale by description. 15
A sale is not precluded, however, from being a sale by description by the fact that the goods are exposed for sale or hire and
are selected by the buyer (SGA s.13(3)). There will often be an overlap between breach of the condition of correspondence
with description and breach of the conditions of reasonable fitness, satisfactory quality and correspondence with sample: where
appropriate, each condition should be pleaded. It should be noted that, unlike SGA s.14 this condition is not confined to sales
in the course of a business.
The addition of SGA s.14(2D) for consumer sales (see para.25-27) had the effect of widening the definition of what constitutes
a “description” in such sales.
For consumer sales after 1 October 2015 the CRA substitutes much lengthier provisions in s.11. The section reiterates the rule
that any sale by description includes a term that the goods will match the description. The section also sweeps up sale by sample
(see para.25-30). The sale is not precluded from being a sale by description just because the goods are exposed for supply
and selected by the buyer—s.11(3). Section 11(4) incorporates the information that must be supplied about the goods under
the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/3134) and makes that
information into a term of the contract. These terms also cannot be excluded—CRA s.31.
