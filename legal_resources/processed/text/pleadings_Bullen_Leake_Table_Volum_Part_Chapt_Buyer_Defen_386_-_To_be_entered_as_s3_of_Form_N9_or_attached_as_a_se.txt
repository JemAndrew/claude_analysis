Buyer’s Defences
Defence of tender and refusal by the seller to deliver
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F56
The Defendant admits that he entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Claimant agreed to sell and
deliver to the Defendant the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant denies that he has wrongfully refused to pay the purchase price either as alleged in the Particulars of Claim
of at all.
3.
On [date] the Defendant tendered to the Claimant a bank draft for the full purchase price and requested the Claimant to
deliver the goods. In breach of the agreement, the Claimant refused to accept the draft or to deliver the goods.
4.
On [date] and prior to the date of this defence, the Defendant paid the said sum into Court pursuant to the CPR 37.2(1).
5.
In the premises the Claimant is not entitled to the purchase price or any part thereof.
