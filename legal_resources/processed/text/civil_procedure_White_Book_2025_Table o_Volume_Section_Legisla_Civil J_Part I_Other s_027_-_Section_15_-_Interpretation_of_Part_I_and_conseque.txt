Civil Jurisdiction and Judgments Act 1982
Section 15
Part I Implementation of the Conventions
Other supplementary provisions
15.— Interpretation of Part I and consequential amendments
5-21
(1) In this Part, unless the context otherwise requires—
 
“judgment” has the meaning given by Article 4(1) of the 2005 Hague Convention;
 
“prescribed” means prescribed by rules of court.
or, as the case may be, Article 3(1) of the 2019 Hague Convention
(2) References in this Part to a judgment registered under section 4B, 4C, 6B or 6C include, to the extent of its
registration, references to a judgment so registered to a limited extent only.
(3) Anything authorised or required by this Part to be done by, to or before a particular magistrates’ court in Northern
Ireland may be done by, to or before any magistrates’ court acting for the same petty sessions district as that court.
Note
5-21. 1
Subsection (1) amended by the Civil Jurisdiction and Judgments Act 1991 Sch.2 para.10, with effect from 1 May 1992.
Subsections (1) and (2) amended by the Civil Jurisdiction and Judgments (Hague Convention on Choice of Court Agreements
2005) Regulations 2015 (SI 2015/1644) reg.10, with effect from 1 October 2015. Subsections (1) to (3) amended by the Civil
Jurisdiction and Judgments (Amendment) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019 (SI 2019/479) reg.25, with effect from 31 December
2020. Subsections (1), (2) further amended by the Recognition and Enforcement of Judgments (2019 Hague Convention etc.)
Regulations 2024 (SI 2024/713) reg.14, with effect from 1 July 2025.
