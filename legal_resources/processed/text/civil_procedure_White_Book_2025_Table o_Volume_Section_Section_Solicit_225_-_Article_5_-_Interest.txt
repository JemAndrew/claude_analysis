Section 7C - The Solicitors Act 1974
Solicitors’ (Non-Contentious Business) Remuneration Order 2009
Article 5
5.— Interest
7C-215
(1) A solicitor may charge interest on the unpaid amount of his costs plus any paid disbursements and value added
tax, subject to the remainder of this article.
(2) Where an entitlement to interest arises under paragraph (1), and subject to any agreement made between a solicitor
and client, the period for which interest may be charged runs from one month after the date of delivery of a bill.
(3) Subject to any agreement made between a solicitor and client, the rate of interest must not exceed the rate for the
time being payable on judgment debts.
(4) Interest charged under this article must be calculated, where applicable, by reference to—
(a) the amount specified in a determination of costs by the Law Society under Schedule 1A to the Solicitors
Act 1974;
(aa) the fees specified in a direction made by the ombudsman under section 137(2)(b)(i) of the Legal Services
Act 2007; or
(b) the amount ascertained on assessment if an application has been made for the bill to be assessed.

Paragraph 54 of Sch.16 to the Legal Services Act 2007 amends s.56 of the Solicitors Act 1974, and alters the purposes for
which orders may be made. As a result of the amendment the Committee (which has the power to prescribe the remuneration
of solicitors) “may make general orders prescribing the general principles to be applied when determining the remuneration of
solicitors in respect of non-contentious business” (Solicitors Act 1974 s.56(2) as amended). Previously the Act had given the
Committee power to regulate as well as prescribe. The effect of this is that the new 2009 Order omits any reference to Common
Form Probate Business, or the Remuneration Certificate Scheme operated by the Law Society, which is effectively abolished.
The interpretation section of the 2009 Order is extended to include employees of a solicitor, sole practitioner, and managers or
employees of a recognised body where a solicitor has charged the estate for his professional costs for acting in the administration
of the estate, and the only personal representatives are solicitors, whether or not acting in a professional capacity, solicitors acting
jointly with partners, managers or employees in a professional capacity, and those employees or managers. The interpretation
section is also expanded to define “Registered European Lawyer” (Solicitors’ (Non-Contentious Business) Remuneration Order
2009 art.2).
Solicitors’ costs must be fair and reasonable, having regard to all the circumstances of the case, and in particular to:
 
(a)the complexity of the matter, or the difficulty or novelty of the questions raised;
 
(b)the skill, labour, specialised knowledge and responsibility involved;
 
(c)the time spent on the business;
 
(d)the number and importance of the documents prepared or considered without regard to length;
 
(e)the place where and the circumstances in which the business or any part of the business is transacted;
 
(f)the amount or value of any money or property involved;
 
(g)whether any land involved is registered land within the meaning of the Land Registration Act 2002;
 
(h)the importance of the matter to the client; and
 
(i)the approval (express or implied) of the entitled person, or the express approval of the testator to:
 
(i)the solicitor undertaking all or any part of the work giving rise to the costs; or
 
(ii)the amount of those costs (ibid.art.4).
The solicitor may take security for the payment of any costs from his client, including the amount of any interest to which
the solicitor may become entitled (ibid art.4). A solicitor may charge interest on the unpaid amount of his costs, plus any paid
disbursements and VAT (subject to any agreement made between the solicitor and client) from one month after the date of
delivery of the bill. The rate of interest must not exceed the rate for the time being payable on judgment debts. If applicable
interest must be calculated by reference to the amount specified in a determination of costs by the Law Society under Sch.1A
of the Solicitors Act 1974, or the amount ascertained on assessment of costs, if an application has been made for detailed
assessment (ibid. art.5).
