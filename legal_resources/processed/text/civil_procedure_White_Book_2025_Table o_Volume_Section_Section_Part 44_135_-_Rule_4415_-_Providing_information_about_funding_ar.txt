Rule 44_15
44.15— Providing information about funding arrangements 1
7B-16+
(1) A party who seeks to recover an additional liability must provide information about the funding arrangement to
the court and to other parties as required by a rule, practice direction or court order.
(2) Where the funding arrangement has changed, and the information a party has previously provided in accordance
with paragraph (1) is no longer accurate, that party must file notice of the change and serve it on all other parties
within 7 days.
(3) Where paragraph (2) applies, and a party has already filed—
(a) an allocation questionnaire; or
(b) a pre-trial check list (listing questionnaire),
he must file and serve a new estimate of costs with the notice.
(The Costs Practice Direction sets out—
 
• the information to be provided when a party issues or responds to a claim form, files an allocation questionnaire,
a pre-trial check list, and a claim for costs;
 
• the meaning of estimate of costs and the information required in it.)
(Rule 44.3B sets out situations where a party will not recover a sum representing any additional liability.)
Effect of rule
7B-16.
1+
Rules 44.15, 44.16 and 44.17 (and also rr.44.3A and 44.3B), were inserted in Pt 44 by the Civil Procedure (Amendment No.3)
Rules 2000 (SI 2000/1317) when, after the bringing into force of the Access to Justice Act 1999, new funding arrangements
for legal proceedings became possible (see paras 7B-2.2+ and 7B-10.3+) and, for the purposes of regulating proceedings where
the new funding arrangements applied, and providing for the assessment of costs in such proceedings, additional rules of court
were required.

As explained above (para.7B-6+), r.44.15 is a provision “relating to funding arrangements” and applies in relation to “a pre-
commencement funding arrangement” as it was in force immediately before 1 April 2013. That rule is supplemented by Section
19 of the Costs Practice Direction (see para.7BPD-5+).
Rule 44.15 provides that any party who seeks to recover an additional liability must provide certain information about it and
must also provide information where the funding arrangement changes (note also r.44.3B). The information to be provided in
the notice of funding, and in the estimate of costs, is limited. Much fuller disclosure of information is required when the final
assessment of costs takes place. See Section 19 of the Costs Practice Direction (see para.7BPD-5+).
Rule 44.15 and Section 19 of the Costs Practice Direction came into effect on 3 July 2000. These provisions did not operate
retrospectively (Inline Logistics Ltd v UCI Logistics Ltd [2002] EWHC 519 (Ch), [2002] 2 Costs L.R. 304 (Ferris J).
Information about funding arrangement
7B-16.
2+
Provisions requiring a party to provide information about a funding arrangement are explained and amplified in Section 19 of
the Costs Practice Direction. A claimant who has entered into a CFA is required to file and serve with the claim form “a notice
containing the information set out in Form N251” (para.19.2(1)).
Section 32 of the Costs Practice Direction (supplementing r.47.6) contains provisions stipulating the information that be
provided to the court where a detailed assessment is commenced in respect of additional liability (see para.47PD.34 above). On
consequences for failure to comply with these directions as to funding arrangements and other relevant directions, see “Failure
to provide information or to comply with direction etc” para.7B-12.2+.
This rule, and r.44.3B (Limits on recovery under funding arrangements), and their supplementing practice directions were
examined in Supperstone v Hurst [2008] EWHC 735 (Ch); [2008] 4 Costs L.R. 572 (Floyd J and assessors), where the decision
of a Master in detailed assessment proceedings to grant relief from sanction to an applicant who had failed to serve a notice of
funding within the relevant time limit, was upheld on appeal.
In Scott v Duncan [2012] EWHC 1792 (QB); [2012] 4 Costs L.R. 787 (Spencer J and assessors), the High Court upheld a
Master’s decision, made in detailed assessment proceedings, to grant relief from sanction where the claimant’s solicitors had
failed to serve notice of a change in funding arrangements, necessitated by their practice becoming incorporated.
In Light on Line Ltd v Zumtobel Lighting Ltd [2012] EWHC 3376 (QB); [2013] 1 Costs L.R. 129. (Slade J & assessors),
questions arose as to whether the claimants, when commencing detailed assessment proceedings and claiming additional
liability in the form of an insurance premium, had provided adequate and timely information about an insurance policy as
required by provisions in Section 32 of the Costs Practice Direction. The court found that, although the receiving party had not
breached para.32.5(2)(c) by providing a redacted rather than an unredacted insurance certificate, there was a clear breach of the
requirement under CPR r.47.6, read with para.32.4 of Practice Direction 47 and para.32.5(2), to serve the insurance certificate
with the bill of costs, but in the event held that it was right that the claimants should be granted relief from sanction for failing
to serve the certificate on the due date.
Where a conditional fee agreement had been back-dated and the notice of funding specified the date from which the agreement
started rather than the date on which the agreement was made, there had been a significant breach of para.19.4 of the Costs
Practice Direction. The court allowed partial relief from sanctions by reducing the amount of the success fee that would otherwise
have been recoverable: O’Brien v Shorrock [2015] EWHC 1630 (QB) (Edis J).
