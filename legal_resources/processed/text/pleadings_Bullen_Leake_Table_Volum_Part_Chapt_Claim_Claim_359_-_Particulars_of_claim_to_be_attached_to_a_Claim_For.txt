The Claimant carries on business as a livestock auctioneer.
2.
On [date] at [address] the Claimant conducted an auction sale of horses upon terms contained in printed conditions of sale
(copy annexed to these Particulars of Claim), a copy of which was handed to all persons attending the sale.
3.
In the premises, the conditions were conditions of each sale effected at the auction.
4.
By clause 4 of the conditions all goods sold were to be paid for and removed from the Claimant’s premises on the day of sale.
5.
At the auction the Claimant sold two horses to the Defendant:
 
(a)“Dobbin” for the sum of £[..........];
 
(b)“Becky” for the sum of £[..........].
6.
In breach of the conditions of sale, the Defendant failed to pay for the horses or either of them and remains liable to the
Claimant in the aggregate sum of £[..........].
7.
In further breach of the conditions, the Defendant has failed or refused to remove either horse from the Claimant’s premises.
By reason of this breach the Claimant has suffered loss and damage.
