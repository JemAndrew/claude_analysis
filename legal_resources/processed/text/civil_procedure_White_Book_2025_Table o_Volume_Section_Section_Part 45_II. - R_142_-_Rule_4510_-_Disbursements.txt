Rule 45_10
45.10— Disbursements 1
7B-25+
(1) The court—
(a) may allow a claim for a disbursement of a type mentioned in paragraph (2); but
(b) must not allow a claim for any other type of disbursement.
(2) The disbursements referred to in paragraph (1) are—
(a) the cost of obtaining—
(i) medical records;
(ii) a medical report;
(iii) a police report;
(iv) an engineer’s report; or
(v) a search of the records of the Driver Vehicle Licensing Authority;
(b) the amount of an insurance premium; or, where a membership organisation undertakes to meet liabilities
incurred to pay the costs of other parties to proceedings, a sum not exceeding such additional amount of costs as
would be allowed under section 30 in respect of provision made against the risk of having to meet such liabilities;
(“membership organisation” is defined in rule 43.2(1)(n).)
(c) where they are necessarily incurred by reason of one or more of the claimants being a child or protected
party as defined in Part 21—
(i) fees payable for instructing counsel; or
(ii) court fees payable on an application to the court;
(d) any other disbursement that has arisen due to a particular feature of the dispute.
(“insurance premium” is defined in rule 43.2.)

Allowance for disbursements
7B-25.
1+
This rule applies in relation to a “pre-commencement funding arrangement”; see para.7B-19+, and paras 48.0.1 to 48.0.4
(“Continuing application of CPR Pts 43 to 48 and Costs Practice Direction as in force immediately before1 April 2013”). See
also s.30 of the SCCO Guide 2013.
In addition to fixed costs, a party may claim disbursements, and they may include the amount of an “insurance premium” (as
defined in r.43.2(1)(m)) or, where the party is supported by a membership organisation, an “additional amount” (r.45.10(2)(b)).
Where parties agree the amount of fixed costs, and the only dispute is as to the payment of, or amount of, a disbursement, the
proceedings should be issued under r.44.12A in the normal way, and not by reference to Section II of Pt 45 (Costs Practice
Direction para.25A.10).
The court has a discretion to allow disbursements; they are not fixed and are subject to assessment (Kilby v Gawith [2009]
EWCA Civ 812, [2009] 1 W.L.R. 853, at para.19).
With effect from 1 February 2004, r.45.10(2) was amended by the Civil Procedure (Amendment No.5) Rules 2003 (SI
2003/3361) to clarify that, in costs-only proceedings brought under Section II of Pt 45 by a party funded by a body which
indemnifies its members or other persons against liabilities for costs which they may incur in proceedings, the court may allow
that party as a disbursement a sum not exceeding such amount as would be allowed under s.30 of the Access to Justice Act
1999. Before then r.45.10(2) referred solely to “an insurance premium”.
