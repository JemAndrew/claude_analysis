Bids by the seller
25-43
Patently, if the seller himself can bid, he can push the price up and, if he (or his nominee) does it in secret, it is, in effect, a
fraud on genuine bidders. The Act provides that the seller can reserve the right to bid SGA (s.57(3)) but this must be announced
before the sale and, if the right is reserved and notified, the seller or “any one person on his behalf” may bid at the auction
(SGA s.57(6)). If the seller’s right to bid is not notified to the other bidders, it is unlawful for the seller or his nominee to
bid or for the auctioneer to accept such a bid (SGA s.57(4)) and if it happens, the sale “may be treated as fraudulent by the
buyer” (SGA s.57(5)).
