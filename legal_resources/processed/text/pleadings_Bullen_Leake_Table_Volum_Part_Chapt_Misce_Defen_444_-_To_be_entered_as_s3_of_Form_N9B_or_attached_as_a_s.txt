Miscellanceous Defences
Defence of mitigation by improvement
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9B or attached as a separate document
1.
26-F31
The Defendant purchased the motor car from Smith in good faith.
2.
Prior to the sale, Smith carried out works of repair to the vehicle by fitting a new engine. In so doing, Smith improved the
vehicle. The improvements increased the value of the vehicle by £[..........].
3.
In the premises, the Defendant is entitled to an allowance to the extent to which, at the time as at which the goods fall to
be valued in assessing damages, the value of the goods is attributable to the improvements. The allowance is sought under
s.6 of the Torts (Interference with Goods) Act 1977.
