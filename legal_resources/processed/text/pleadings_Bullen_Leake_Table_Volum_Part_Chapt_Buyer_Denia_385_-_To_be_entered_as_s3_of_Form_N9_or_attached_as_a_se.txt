Chapter 25 - Supply of Goods and Digital Content
Buyer’s Defences
Denial of claim that the buyer is unwilling to make payment
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F55
The Defendant admits that he entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Claimant agreed to sell and
deliver to the Defendant the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant denies that he has wrongfully refused to pay the purchase price either as alleged in the Particulars of Claim
or at all. By a letter dated [date] from the Defendant and a letter dated [date] from his solicitors, the Defendant has notified
the Claimant of his willingness to pay the price upon delivery of the goods (copies of those letters are annexed to this
Defence).
3.
Despite such notification, in breach of the agreement the Claimant has refused to deliver the goods to the Defendant.
4.
In the premises the Claimant is not entitled to the purchase price or any part thereof.
