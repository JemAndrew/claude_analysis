Assignment of proceedings
12-13
By CPR PD 8A Section C para.21, proceedings in the High Court under the Act (other than proceedings for a mandatory order
by way of judicial review) and actions for damages for breach of a statutory duty under the Act, are assigned to the Chancery
Division. Such proceedings and actions must be commenced by claim form, except for applications under s.367 by the FCA or
PRA for a winding up order in respect of an authorised person or an appointed representative, which are begun by petition.
The expression “the Court” is used, but not defined in the Act: no rules have ever been made for the assignment of any
proceedings under the Act to the County Court. The cause of action conferred by FSMA ss.20, 26, 27, 29, 30 and 71 is probably
not within s.15 of the County Courts Act 1984; jurisdiction may be conferred on the County Court by s.16 of that Act (money
recoverable by virtue of any enactment), but that section would not cover recovery of property (as provided for by the relevant
sections of FSMA). The parties cannot agree in writing to confer jurisdiction on the County Court pursuant to the County Courts
Act 1984 s.18, as that section excludes from its scope proceedings which, if commenced in the High Court, would be assigned
to the Chancery Division.
