Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
(a)—Post January 2012 Arbitration Agreement Required
29-122
The ICC Rules have a global reach and are of universal application. Allowing the Emergency Arbitrator Provisions to have
immediate effect, in accordance with art.6(1) of the Rules, 622  on any ICC arbitration agreement was not felt to be appropriate
by the Task Force, as the possibility of pre-arbitral Emergency Measures is far-reaching and might not necessarily have been
expected by parties having adopted ICC arbitration clauses in their contracts prior to the 2012 Rules coming into force. 623
Article 29(6)(a) therefore expressly requires that the ICC arbitration agreement post-dates the coming into force of the 2012
Rules for the Emergency Arbitrator Provisions to apply. From then on, parties are considered as being aware of the Emergency
Arbitration provisions in the current Rules. If the arbitration agreement is in a contract that is amended after 1 January 2012, in
the authors’ view the arbitration agreement will only be considered as entered into after that date if the amendment relates to
the arbitration agreement or the contract as a whole by a restatement of the contract, for example. 624
29-123
The additional standard ICC arbitration clause, entitled “Arbitration without emergency arbitrator”, which is published on the
ICC Secretariat’s website and in the ICC booklet containing the ICC arbitration and ADR Rules, 625  is designed to increase
users’ awareness of the new Emergency Arbitrator regime, and of the possibility to opt out from it.
29-124
The parties are free at any point in time to agree on the application of the Emergency Arbitrator Provisions, even if their original
agreement predates 1 January 2012, or if they had originally chosen to exclude them. The ICC Court’s President will have no
choice but to respect such agreement when checking the admissibility of the Application. 626  A more complex issue arises with
respect to arbitration agreements concluded prior to 1 January 2012 but that expressly refer to the “current version” of the ICC
Rules or the version of the ICC Rules applicable at the time of the start of the ICC arbitration. At least one Emergency Arbitrator
has upheld jurisdiction based on such a clause. 627  Although such an agreement is clearly “concluded” before the 2012 Rules
entered into force, as a matter of interpretation the parties expressed the intention to use the most recent version of the ICC
Rules. Therefore, it is reasonable to give effect to this intention by applying all of the provisions of the revised Rules.
