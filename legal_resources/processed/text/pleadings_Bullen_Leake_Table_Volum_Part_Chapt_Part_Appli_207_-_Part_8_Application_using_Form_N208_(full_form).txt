Part 8 Application using Form N208 (full form)
Claim Form (CCR Part 8)
20-F22
Claimant: AB Limited
Defendants: [CD] or [EF and GH the personal representatives of XY deceased]
Details of claim
The Claimant seeks permission to enforce a conditional sale agreement made [date] between the Claimant and the
Defendant whereby the Claimant sold [identify the goods] (“the goods”) on conditional sale to the Defendant. The
agreement was regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”) and the Claimant seeks an enforcement order
pursuant to ss.127, [86] and [92] of the CCA.
Defendant’s name and address: CD [address] or EF [address] and GH [address] the personal representatives of XY
deceased.
1.
By a conditional sale agreement in writing dated [date] between the Claimant and the Defendant [XY], the Claimant sold
the goods on conditional sale to the Defendant [to XY]. A copy of the agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
2.
The cash price of the goods was £[..........], the deposit was £[..........], the total charge for credit was £[..........] and the
balance of the total price of £[..........] was payable by x equal consecutive monthly instalments of £[..........].
3.
The agreement was regulated by the CCA.

4.
By clause 6 of the agreement, if any instalment was not paid on the due date, the Claimant should be entitled on notice
to determine the agreement and the Defendant [XY] should thereafter no longer be in possession of the goods without
the consent of the Claimant.
5.
In pursuance of the agreement, the Defendant [XY] paid the deposit and x instalments. In breach of the agreement the
Defendant failed to pay the instalment due [date] or any instalment due thereafter.
6.
By a notice of default and termination dated [date] (copy annexed to these Particulars of Claim) the Claimant required the
Defendant [XY] to remedy the breach within 14 days and gave notice that, in default of remedy by that date, the agreement
would thereupon determine.
7.
The Defendant [XY] failed to remedy the breach within seven days or at all and on [date] the agreement was duly
determined. At that date the goods was not protected goods for the purposes of s.90 of the Act and the Claimant became
entitled to repossess it.
8.
The agreement was improperly executed, contrary to s.61 of the CCA and regulations 2 and 5 of the Consumer Credit
(Agreements) Regulations 1983 in that, by a printing error, in the statement of protection and remedies available under the
CCA to borrowers pursuant to Schedule 2 to the Regulations, two of the lines of type were reversed.
9.
The Defendant has not been prejudiced by this printing error in that the Defendant has made use of the goods and has
paid x monthly instalments of rent well knowing of the error which he pointed out to the Claimant in a letter dated [date]
(copy annexed to these Particulars of Claim).
10.
The Claimant therefore seeks an order permitting it to enforce the agreement pursuant to s.127 of the CCA.

8.
On [date], XY died and on [date] EF and GH took out letters of administration to his estate.

9.
By reason of the Claimant’s said termination of the agreement, he would have been entitled to enforce the agreement
against XY by repossessing the goods and by maintaining an action against XY for the arrears of instalments and interest.
10.
The Claimant therefore seeks an order permitting it to enforce the agreement against the estate of CD pursuant to s.86
of the CCA.
Or
8.
On [date] the Claimant, as it was entitled to do, attended the premises of the Defendant at [address] and demanded delivery
up of the goods. Wrongfully and in breach of the agreement, the Defendant refused to deliver up the goods to the Claimant
and said that it would remain locked in his garage.
9.
The Claimant therefore seeks an order permitting it to enforce the agreement pursuant to s.92 of the CCA by entering on
to the Defendant’s said premises and retaking possession of the goods.
