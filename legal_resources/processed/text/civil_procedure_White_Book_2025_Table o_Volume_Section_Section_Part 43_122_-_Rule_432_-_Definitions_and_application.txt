Rule 43_2
43.2— Definitions and application 1
7B-2+
(1) In Parts 44 to 48, unless the context otherwise requires—
(a) “costs” includes fees, charges, disbursements, expenses, remuneration, reimbursement allowed to a litigant
in person under rule 48.6, any additional liability incurred under a funding arrangement and any fee or reward
charged by a lay representative for acting on behalf of a party in proceedings allocated to the small claims track;
(b) “costs judge” means a taxing master of the Senior Courts;
(ba) “Costs Office” means the Senior Courts Costs Office;
(c) “costs officer” means—
(i) a costs judge;
(ii) a district judge; and
(iii) an authorised court officer;
(d) “authorised court officer” means any officer of—
(i) a county court;
(ii) a district registry;
(iii) the Principal Registry of the Family Division; or
(iv) the Costs Office,
whom the Lord Chancellor has authorised to assess costs;
(e) “fund” includes any estate or property held for the benefit of any person or class of person and any fund to
which a trustee or personal representative is entitled in that capacity;
(f) “receiving party” means a party entitled to be paid costs;
(g) “paying party” means a party liable to pay costs;
(h) “assisted person” means an assisted person within the statutory provisions relating to legal aid;

(i) “LSC funded client” means an individual who receives services funded by the Legal Services Commission
as part of the Community Legal Service within the meaning of Part I of the Access to Justice Act 1999;
(j) “fixed costs” means the amounts which are to be allowed in respect of solicitors’ charges in the circumstances
set out in Section I of Part 45.
(k) “funding arrangement” means an arrangement where a person has—
(i) entered into a conditional fee agreement or a collective conditional fee agreement which provides for a
success fee within the meaning of section 58(2) of the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990;
(ii) taken out an insurance policy to which section 29 of the Access to Justice Act 1999 (recovery of
insurance premiums by way of costs) applies; or
(iii) made an agreement with a membership organisation to meet that person’s legal costs;
(l) “percentage increase” means the percentage by which the amount of a legal representative’s fee can be
increased in accordance with a conditional fee agreement which provides for a success fee;
(m) “insurance premium” means a sum a money paid or payable for insurance against the risk of incurring a
costs liability in the proceedings, taken out after the event that is the subject matter of the claim;
(n) “membership organisation” means a body prescribed for the purposes of section 30 of the Access to Justice
Act 1999 (recovery where the body undertakes to meet costs liabilities);
(o) “additional liability” means the percentage increase, the insurance premium, or the additional amount in
respect of provision made by a membership organisation, as the case may be; and
(p) “free of charge” has the same meaning as in section 194(10) of the Legal Services Act 2007;
(q) “pro bono representation” means legal representation provided free of charge; and
(r) “the prescribed charity” has the same meaning as in section 194(8) of the Legal Services Act 2007.
(2) The costs to which the rules in Parts 44 to 48 apply include—
(a) the following costs where those costs may be assessed by the court;
(i) costs of proceedings before an arbitrator or umpire;
(ii) costs of proceedings before a tribunal or other statutory body; and
(iii) costs payable by a client to his solicitor; and
(b) costs which are payable by one party to another party under the terms of a contract, where the court makes
an order for an assessment of those costs.
(3) Where advocacy or litigation services are provided to a client under a conditional fee agreement, costs are
recoverable under Parts 44 to 48 notwithstanding that the client is liable to pay his legal representative’s fees and
expenses only to the extent that sums are recovered in respect of the proceedings, whether by way of costs or otherwise.
(4) In paragraph (3), the reference to a conditional fee agreement is to an agreement which satisfies all the conditions
applicable to it by virtue of section 58 of the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990.

Definitions
7B-2. 1+ The definitions in para.(1) of r.43.2 apply in Pts 44 to 48, unless the context otherwise requires.
Rule 43.2 is supplemented by Section 2 of the Costs Practice Direction, where the definitions of “funding arrangement”, and
expressions relevant thereto, are elaborated (see para.7BPD-1+).
In the years since the CPR came into effect, definitions have been amended and further definitions added as a consequence
of substantial amendments made to Parts in the CPR concerned with costs. Among the most significant were the additions of
the definitions in sub-paras (k) to (o) of r.43.2(1) made by the Civil Procedure (Amendment No.3) Rules 2000 (SI 2000/1317)
when new funding arrangements for legal proceedings became possible, and the addition of paras (3) and (4) made by the Civil
Procedure (Amendment No.2) Rules 2003 (SI 2003/1242), following upon amendments to the Senior Courts Act 1981 s.51
and to CFA Regulations then in force.
In Pt 44 as enacted by the Civil Procedure (Amendment) Rules 2013 (SI 2013/262), and brought into effect on 1 April 2013,
definitions for terms used throughout the Costs Rules are set out in r.44.1(1). The particular words and phrases defined in that
rule and in r.43.2(1) as it stood before 1 April 2013, are the same, subject to a few exceptions, and include definitions for
“detailed assessment” and “summary assessment” (rr.43.3 and 43.4). The exceptions are that old r.43.2(1) contains definitions
of “funding arrangement” and of other terms related thereto (see further below).
“funding arrangement”
7B-2. 2+ The definition of “funding arrangement” (para.(1)(k)), and the definitions of other terms following it in r.43.2(1), were inserted
by the Civil Procedure (Amendment No.3) Rules 2000 (SI 2000/1317) and came into force (subject to transitional provisions) on
1 July 2000. The insertion of these definitions accompanied additions and amendments made to Pts 43 to 48 as consequences of
amendments made to the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990 by Pt 2 of the Access to Justice Act 1999 which (as outlined below)
had the effect of making new funding arrangements possible for legal proceedings. The definition of funding arrangement
includes a conditional fee agreement (CFA). Before s.58 of the 1990 Act, and delegated legislation made thereunder, came into
force on 5 July 1995, CFAs were unenforceable. Thereafter, CFAs that complied with that legislation were enforceable. Initially
they were enforceable only in personal injury and fatal accident claims, in insolvency claims, and in proceedings before the
ECHR and ECtHR, but with effect from 30 July 1998, they were rendered enforceable in all cases except criminal and specified
family proceedings. By s.27 of the 1999 Act, s.58 of the 1990 Act was substituted and s.58A (complementing s.58) was added
to that Act with effect from 1 April 2000. By that legislation, it was provided that where a court made a costs order in favour of a
party who had the benefit of a CFA which provided for a “success fee”, that order could provide that any fees payable under the
CFA were payable by the paying party. By the relevant legislation, a success fee is a fee in addition to that payable by the client
to his legal representative calculated as a percentage of the latter fee. Further, by s.29 of the 1999 Act, insurance premiums
taken out by a party against the risk of incurring costs liability were also made recoverable as costs in the case. Thus, where a
costs order was made, a paying party could be made liable for two forms of costs in addition to that payable under the normal
rules as to costs liability. Those additions are referred to in the definition of “additional liability” (para.(1)(o)), which in turn
relates to the definitions of “percentage increase” (i.e the success fee) and “insurance premium” (paras (1)(l) and (1)(m)). By
s.30 of the 1990 Act, costs incurred by bodies prescribed by regulations made by the Lord Chancellor who had undertaken to
meet costs liabilities of their members became recoverable in a manner similar to the recovery of costs by a legal representative
acting for a party under a CFA. Such a body is defined as a “membership organisation” (para.(1)(n)), and the definitions of
“funding arrangement” and “additional liability” recognise the effect of s.30.
As explained above (para.7B-0.2+), paras (a), (k), (l), (m), (n) & (o) of r.43.2(1) are provisions “relating to funding
arrangements” and apply in relation to “a pre-commencement funding arrangement”.

Application (r.43.2(2))
7B-2. 3+ Subject to exceptions, the CPR apply to all proceedings in the Civil Division of the Court of Appeal, the High Court and the
county court (see r.2.1). Costs may be assessed by the court in accordance with Pts 43 to 48 in such proceedings and, as r.43.2(2)
indicates, in some other circumstances. (In the new Pt 44, r.43.2(2) is re-enacted in the same terms; see r.44.1(2) (for information
on the application of the CPR Costs Rules, see commentary following that rule).)
Client liable “only to the extent that sums are recovered” (r.43.2(3))
7B-2. 4+ Section 51(2) of the Senior Courts Act 1981 states that rules of court may regulate “matters relating to the costs of proceedings”.
By the Access to Justice Act 1999 s.31 that sub-section was amended so as to prescribe expressly that such rules may provide,
in particular, for securing that the amount awarded to a party in respect of the costs to be paid by him to his legal or other
representative “is not limited to what would have been payable by him to them if he had not been awarded costs”. That
amendment, together with amendments made by the Lord Chancellor to CFA Regulations then in effect, paved the way for
the making of a rule (if it were deemed necessary) negativing the effects of the so-called “indemnity principle” where costs
are recoverable by a party having the benefit of a CFA governed by the amended Regulations and entered into on or after 2
June 2003. Accordingly, paras (3) and (4) were inserted in r.43.2 by the Civil Procedure (Amendment No.2) Rules 2003 (SI
2003/1242) and provide that costs whose recovery is limited in this way are nevertheless recoverable costs for the purposes
of the Costs Rules in the CPR. For information on the indemnity principle generally, see paras 44.2.5 and 47.14.7, Guide to
Summary Assessment of Costs para.34 et seq, SCCO Guide Sections 2.6 and 3.3 (Vol.2 para.1C-24 and 1C-44).
As explained above (para.7B-0.2+), paras (3) and (4) of r.43.2 are provisions “relating to funding arrangements” and apply in
relation to “a pre-commencement funding arrangement”.
