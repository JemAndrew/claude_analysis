Action for breach of condition or warranty
Non-conformity with the contract
25-24
SGA ss.13, 14 and 15 contain provisions as to the quality and condition of the goods sold. Traditionally, breach of any of
the conditions implied into the contract of sale by those sections afforded the buyer the right to reject the goods or, where
appropriate, to sue for damages for breach. This remains the law; but in consumer sales, breach of the conditions implied by
the three sections give rise to additional and alternative remedies. Part 5A of the SGA creates the concept of non-conformity
with the contract of sale (s.48A(1)(a)) which is defined by s.48F as arising if “there is in relation to the goods a breach of an
express term of the contract or a term implied by s.13, 14 or 15 [of the Act]”.
These provisions remain good law for non-consumer contracts at all times and for consumer contracts prior to 1 October 2015.
The CRA has, however, replaced SGA ss.13-15 and widened the remedies available for breach.
