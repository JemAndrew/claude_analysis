Defence to an action upon an underlying debt that the Defendant has given a promissory note not yet due
1.
10-E28
It is admitted that the Defendant was at 31 January 2000 indebted to the Claimant in the sum of £1,000 which was then
due and payable.
2.
In consideration of that debt, and in payment of it, the Defendant made and delivered to the Claimant a promissory note (a
copy of which is attached hereto marked “A”) by which the Defendant promised to pay the sum of £1,200 to the Claimant
on 31 January 2001. That note is not yet due for payment.
3.
In those circumstances it is denied that the Claimant is entitled to the sum of £1,000 as alleged or at all.
