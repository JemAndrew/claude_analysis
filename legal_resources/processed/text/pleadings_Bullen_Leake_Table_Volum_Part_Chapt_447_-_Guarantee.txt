Guarantee
27-02
A contract of guarantee is a contract whereby the guarantor agrees that, upon default by the principal debtor of his obligations
under the contract between the principal debtor and the creditor, the guarantor will perform those obligations for the creditor. A
guarantee is therefore a secondary obligation and is dependent for its efficacy on the existence and enforceability of the primary
obligation. Although the right to an indemnity can be implied or can arise by operation of law, a guarantee can be created only
by means of a contract between the creditor and the guarantor.
The right of action against a guarantor is dependent on a default by the principal debtor. The principal debtor’s liability may
or may not depend on a demand being made by the creditor but, in practice, a demand is virtually always necessary to activate
the obligation of the guarantor to make good that default.
