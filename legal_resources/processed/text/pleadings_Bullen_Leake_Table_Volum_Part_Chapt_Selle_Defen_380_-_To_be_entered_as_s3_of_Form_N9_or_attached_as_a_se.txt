Seller’s Defences
Defence based on an exemption clause where the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977, the Unfair Terms in Consumer
Contracts Regulations 1999 and the Consumer Rights Act 2015 do not apply
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
25-F50
The Defendant admits that it entered into the written contract with the Claimant annexed to the Claim Form whereby the
Defendant agreed to sell and deliver the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The sale was an international supply contract within the meaning of the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977, s.26 in that the
Claimant’s place of business is in New York, USA and the Defendant’s place of business is in London and the contract
provided for the carriage of the goods from the United Kingdom to the United States of America.
3.
By clause 6 of the contract, all conditions and warranties as to the condition, fitness or quality of the goods or as to their
conformity with any description or sample were excluded and the Defendant will rely upon that clause.
4.
In the premises, the contract was not subject to the terms alleged in paragraphs X, Y and Z of the Particulars of Claim
or any terms to the like effect.
