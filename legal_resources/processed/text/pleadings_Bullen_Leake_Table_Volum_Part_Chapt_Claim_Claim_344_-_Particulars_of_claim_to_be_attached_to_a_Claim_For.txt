Particulars of claim to be attached to a Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
25-F14
The Claimant and the Defendant each carries on the business of a dealer in rare books.
2.
By an oral agreement made between the Claimant and the Defendant on [date], the Defendant agreed to sell to the Claimant
a medieval manuscript book known as “The Canterbury Codex” (“the book”) for the sum of £[..........].
3.
The agreement was made at a meeting between the Claimant and the Defendant at [identify circumstances of sale] on [date].
4.
On [date] the Claimant duly tendered to the Defendant the sum of £[..........] but, in breach of the agreement, the Defendant
refused to deliver up the book to the Claimant.
5.
In the premises the Claimant is entitled to delivery up of the book.
6.
Further or alternatively, by reason of the Defendant’s breach of the agreement, the Claimant has suffered loss and damage.
Particulars
Difference between the contract price and the market price for the book on [date] £[..........].
The Claimant therefore claims:
 
(1)An order that the Defendant do forthwith deliver up to the Claimant the book known as “The Canterbury Codex”.
 
(2)Further or alternatively: damages for breach of contract.
 
(3)Interest under [s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981] [s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984] at the rate of x% until
judgment.
