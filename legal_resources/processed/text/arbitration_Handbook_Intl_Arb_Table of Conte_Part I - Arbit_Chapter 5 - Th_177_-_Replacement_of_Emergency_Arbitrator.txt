The Emergency Arbitrator Provisions do not provide for the possibility of a replacement of the Emergency Arbitrator. In practice,
in view of the short time span of the Emergency Arbitrator’s expectation, there should rarely be a need for such replacement.
However, in the first 80 Emergency Arbitrator Proceedings, it occurred on two occasions as a result of a resignation of the
Emergency Arbitrator. 572  Although art.15(1) of the Rules does not apply directly, on the basis of art.8(1) of the Emergency
Arbitrator Rules, the President of the ICC Court undoubtedly has the power to replace an Emergency Arbitrator under the same
conditions as those applicable for arbitrators.
