Changes to claims and requests for relief (art.23(4))
22-91
The issue of new claims and new requests for relief is discussed in art.23(4). In deciding whether to accept new claims, the
Tribunal is required to consider the nature of the claims, the stage of the arbitration and “other relevant circumstances” in
accordance with art.23(4).
22-92
In a recent unpublished non-ICC case, prior to the main evidentiary hearing the claimants applied to add a new claim in tort in
an arbitration based on the allegations in the respondent’s rejoinder. The respondent objected in particular that it would not have
a chance to prepare for such a new claim at the evidentiary hearing. The Tribunal decided that the claimants were entitled to
add the claim but that the proceedings with respect to the new tort claim were bifurcated to enable the respondent to adequately
deal with it. 275
22-93
In many cases, the authors have noted that the requests for relief are amended in the closing submissions. Frequently, the
opposing party has no objection to the amendment to the request for relief and in the authors’ view the Tribunal should deal with
the reformulated request for relief to the extent that the Tribunal is comfortable doing so on the documentary record before it.
22-94
Where the adverse party objects to the change in the request for relief, in the authors’ view the Tribunal’s must determine
whether the request for relief as reformulated raises any issues as to which the opposing party could reasonably request to either
file new evidence or make additional submissions. If so, then there is a further issue as to whether it is appropriate at that stage
in the arbitration, and given the Tribunal’s obligation to conduct the arbitration in an expeditious and cost effective manner, for
the Tribunal to re-open the proceedings to deal with the issue.
