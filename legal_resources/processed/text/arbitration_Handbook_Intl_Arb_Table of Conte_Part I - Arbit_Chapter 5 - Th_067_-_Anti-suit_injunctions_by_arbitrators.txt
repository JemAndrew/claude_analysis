Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
Anti-suit injunctions by arbitrators
22-48
The West Tankers case has generated renewed interest in anti-suit injunctions issued by arbitrators. 229  Those that argue
that Tribunals inherently have such power base their argument on the principle of Kompetenz-Kompetenz. 230  While certain
Tribunals, such as ICSID 231  and the Iran-United States Claims Tribunal, 232  have had experience with anti-suit orders issued
by arbitrators, the ICC also has had some history with this particular situation. However, other commentators have opposed
the idea, viewing anti-suit injunctions issued by arbitrators as a violation of the Kompetenz-Kompetenz of other Tribunals and
Courts as well as of international comity. 233
22-49
However, it is clear that ICC tribunals have issued anti-suit injunctions in a number of instances. 234  Since these decisions
generally relate to interim measures, they are discussed under art.28. What is important to note at this juncture is that the
European Court of Justice has upheld the enforcement of an award rendered in the EU including an anti-suit injunction with
respect to bringing court proceedings in EU courts. Therefore, Tribunals are not subject to the same limitations as EU courts
under the West Tankers doctrine.
