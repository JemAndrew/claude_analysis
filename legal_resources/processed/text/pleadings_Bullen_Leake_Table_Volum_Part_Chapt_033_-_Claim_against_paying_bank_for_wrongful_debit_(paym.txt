Claim against paying bank for wrongful debit (payment on a forged signature)
1.
9-E6
The Defendant is a bank. The Claimant holds an account with the Claimant as follows [give account details] (“the
Account”).
2.
On or about [date], the Defendant paid a cheque for £[..........] dated [date], purportedly drawn by the Claimant on the
Account and payable to AB (“the Cheque”). The Defendant debited the Account with the amount of the Cheque.
3.
The Claimant neither drew nor authorised the drawing of the Cheque, and the Claimant’s signature on the Cheque was
a forgery.
4.
Accordingly, the Defendant had no authority to pay the Cheque and was not entitled to debit the Account with the amount
of the Cheque.
5.
For these reasons, the Claimant is entitled to and claims repayment of the sum of £[..........].
6.
Further the Claimant is entitled to and claims interest [at the contractual rate applicable to the Account or] pursuant to
s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981 on the sum of £[..........] at the rate of [..........] per cent per annum from [the date of
the debit], amounting to £[..........] at [date of claim] and continuing at a daily rate of £[..........].
AND the Claimant claims:
 
(1)A declaration that the Defendant was not entitled to debit the Account with the amount of the Cheque;
 
(2)£[..........];
 
(3)Interest pursuant to [contract or] s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981, under paragraph 6 above.
