Surety’s Defences
Defence of variation of principal contract
To be entered as s.3 of Form N9 or attached as a separate document
1.
27-F12
The Defendant admits that he entered into the contract of guarantee set out in the Particulars of Claim (“the guarantee”),
whereby he guaranteed the liabilities of XY Limited to the Claimant under the loan agreement dated [date] (“the loan
agreement”).
2.
The Defendant admits receipt of the letter of demand dated [date] referred to in the Particulars of Claim. For the reasons
set out below, he is not liable to the Claimant in the sum claimed or any sum.
3.
It was an express condition of the loan agreement that the loan of £[..........] should be repaid on [date 1] together with
interest of £[..........].
4.
On [date 2] the Claimant and XY Limited varied the loan agreement to provide for the loan to be repaid by x equal monthly
instalments of £[..........] with interest of £[..........], the first instalment to fall due on [date 1].
Or
4.
On [date 2] the Claimant and XY Limited varied the loan agreement to provide that, instead of the lump sum interest of
£[ ] payable on [date 1], XY Limited should pay interest monthly throughout the loan at [give new interest terms].
5.
The Defendant did not consent to such variation of the loan agreement.
6.
In the premises the Defendant is released from all liability under the guarantee.
