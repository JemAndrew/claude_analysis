Chapter 15 - Mortgages and Charges
15-01
This chapter deals with the subject of charges as a species of mortgage, i.e. a “charge of land by deed expressed to be by way
of legal mortgage” which gives the mortgagee (chargee) the same rights and remedies as if he had a term of 3,000 years. 1  It
does not cover mortgages of chattels.
This section does not purport to deal with the notoriously complicated substantive law of mortgages and charges, but highlights
the pleading issues in the context of the CPR. For a full exposition of the law relating to mortgages and charges, reference
should be made to one of the practitioner texts, such as Fisher and Lightwood’s Law of Mortgage, 15th edn, and Halsbury’s
Laws, Vol.77. 2
This narrative will cover the essential elements of the cause of action for a mortgage claim and the most common defences to
a mortgage claim. The comments are designed to assist the pleader to identify these essential elements.
CPR Pt 55 deals exclusively with possession claims and is essential reading before any pleading in relation to a mortgage claim
is embarked upon.
Part 55 must be used where court proceedings include a possession claim brought by a landlord, a lender or a licensor (CPR
r.55.2(1)). Part 55 must also be used in a claim brought against trespassers. A claim, which is not a possession claim, may be
brought under the Pt 55 procedure if it is started in the same claim form as a possession claim. However, if a claimant bank
wishes to claim only on the debt and does not seek possession, these procedural requirements do not apply. 3
