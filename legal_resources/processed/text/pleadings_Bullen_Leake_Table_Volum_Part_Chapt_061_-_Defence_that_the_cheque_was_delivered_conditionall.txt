Defence that the cheque was delivered conditionally
1.
10-E19
It is admitted that the Defendant signed the cheque relied on by the Claimant. It is admitted that the cheque has not been
paid because the Defendant countermanded payment of it.
2.
The Defendant drew the cheque and delivered it to the Claimant in the following circumstances:
 
(i)On 30 April 2015 the Defendant asked the Claimant whether he could supply a refrigerator and deliver it to the
Claimant’s house within a week.
 
(ii)The Claimant agreed that he would attempt to obtain such a refrigerator and to deliver it within that time. He
advised that the cost of delivery would be £100 (the amount of the cheque) and asked the Defendant to provide a
cheque for that amount to him, in order to pay for delivery if it could be arranged. The Defendant drew the cheque
relied on and handed it to the Claimant for that purpose.
 
(iii)On 2 May 2015 the Claimant telephoned the Defendant and advised that he was unable to supply the refrigerator
sought by the Defendant. The Claimant and the Defendant agreed that the Defendant would look elsewhere.
3.
The delivery of the cheque was therefore conditional upon the Claimant agreeing to provide and deliver to the Defendant
the refrigerator, which condition was not fulfilled.
4.
Further, or alternatively in the circumstances there was no consideration for the Defendant’s cheque.
5.
It is therefore denied that the Defendant is liable upon the cheque as alleged or at all.
