Application Notice
Part A
26-F14
We, [..........], solicitors acting on behalf of the Claimant intend to apply for an order (a draft of which is attached) under
s.12 of the Torts (Interference with Goods) Act 1977.
 
(1)for the delivery up of [identify the goods claimed] to the Claimant’s solicitors or to a person appointed by the Court
on condition that the Claimant does not dispose of it or on such other terms as the Court may direct;
 
(2)alternatively, an order that the Defendant preserves the goods on such terms as the Court may direct and does not
dispose or otherwise part with possession of it;
pending the determination of the action or further order [with liberty to the Defendant to apply to set aside the order]
Because
 
(1)The goods referred to in paragraph 1 of the Particulars of Claim belongs to the Claimant and the Defendant’s
possession of it is wrongful.
 
(2)The Defendant is exhibiting them for sale.
 
(3)Damages are not an adequate alternative remedy.
