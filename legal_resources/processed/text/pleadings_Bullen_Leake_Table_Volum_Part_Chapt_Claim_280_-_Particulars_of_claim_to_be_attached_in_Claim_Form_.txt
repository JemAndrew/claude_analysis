Claim against assignor based on reassignment of bad debt
Particulars of claim to be attached in Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
22-F2
By a factoring agreement made on the [date] between the Claimant and the Defendant, the Defendant assigned to the
Claimant all debts then and at any time thereafter owing to it in respect of its sales of motor cars. A copy of the factoring
agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
2.
On [date], Tom Smith (“the debtor”) purchased [identify the vehicle] (“the car”) from the Defendant at a price of £[..........].
The purchase price of the car thereupon became a debt (“the debt”) owing to the Defendant and payable within 30 days.
By reason of the factoring agreement, it was assigned to the Claimant.
3.
The Claimant acting for and on behalf of the Defendant served the debtor with notice of assignment of the debt on [date].
Thereupon the Claimant paid the Defendant the sum of £[..........] being 75% of the debt, as it was required to do under
the factoring agreement.
4.
The Defendant failed to deliver the car to the debtor who refused to pay the debt.
5.
It was a term of the factoring agreement that the Defendant had delivered the car to the debtor before serving the notice
of assignment upon him. In failing to deliver the car, the Defendant was in breach of the factoring agreement and the
Claimant thereupon became entitled to serve a notice of re-assignment upon the Defendant in which event the debt set
out in the notice was re-assigned to the Defendant and the Defendant became liable to repay the Claimant all sums paid
by it to the Defendant in respect of the debt and interest thereon calculated in accordance with the provisions of clause
19 of the agreement.
6.
On [date], the Claimant served the Defendant with a notice that the debt was re-assigned and required the Defendant to
repay it the sum of £[..........] that it had paid the Defendant in respect of the debt together with interest thereon in the
sum of £[..........].
