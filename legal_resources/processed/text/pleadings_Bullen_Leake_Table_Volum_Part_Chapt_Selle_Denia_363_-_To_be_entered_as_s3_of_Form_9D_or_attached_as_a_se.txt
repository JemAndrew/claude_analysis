Seller’s Defences
Denial of late delivery and reliance on waiver or variation of delivery date or on the buyer’s breach of contract
To be entered as s.3 of Form 9D or attached as a separate document
Defence
1.
25-F33
The Defendant admits that it entered into the oral agreement with the Claimant whereby the Defendant agreed to sell and
deliver the goods referred to in the Particulars of Claim.
2.
The Defendant denies that it was a term of the agreement that the goods were to be delivered on [date]. It was orally agreed
that the date for delivery was to be [date] and the Defendant duly delivered the goods on that date.
Or
2.
The Defendant denies that there was any term of the agreement fixing a date for delivery. In the premises, the Defendant’s
obligation was to deliver the goods within a reasonable time and the Defendant will say that a reasonable time connoted
delivery within [period]. The Defendant having delivered the goods on [date] delivered them within a reasonable time.
Or
2.
The Defendant admits that it was a term of the agreement that the goods should be delivered on [date 1]. It is further
admitted that the goods were in fact delivered on [date 2].

3.
In pursuance of the agreement, the Defendant tendered delivery on [date 1] but, in breach of the agreement, the Claimant
had failed to make arrangements for their reception and the Defendant was unable to effect delivery. The earlier date on
which the Defendant was able to effect delivery acceptable to the Claimant was [date 2].
Or
3.
In a telephone conversation between the Claimant and the Defendant on [date], the Claimant informed the Defendant that
it was in no hurry for the goods and did not mind when they were delivered. In the premises, the Claimant waived the time
for delivery and the Defendant was not in breach of the agreement in delivering on [date 2].
Or
3.
In an exchange of fax messages dated [date] (copies annexed to this Defence), it was agreed between the Claimant and
the Defendant that the date for delivery should be changed from [date 1] to [date 2] and the Defendant duly delivered
on [date 2].
Or
3.
It was an express term of the agreement that the Claimant should pay the purchase price of the goods on delivery. On
[date] the Claimant informed the Defendant that he would be unable to pay on delivery whereupon the Defendant, as it
was entitled to do, refused to deliver on [date].
Or
3.
It was an express term of the agreement that the Claimant should pay the purchase price of the goods on delivery. The
Defendant tendered delivery on the agreed date but, in breach of the agreement, the Claimant failed or refused to pay the
purchase price and the Defendant, as it was entitled to do, refused to make delivery.
4.
On [date 2] the Claimant informed the Defendant that it was able and willing to pay the purchase price and the Defendant
accordingly made delivery on that date.
