Section 7 - Legal Representatives—Costs and Litigation Funding
Section 7A - Litigation Funding after 1 April 2013
Introduction
7A-1
The Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012 and regulations made under it have a significant effect
in relation to funding arrangements made on or after 1 April 2013. In particular, success fees payable under conditional fee
agreements made after that date are not recoverable between the parties (except in limited classes of cases which are temporarily
excluded from the reforms), lower limits are set for success fees in personal injury claims and the recovery of after the event
insurance premiums is severely limited.
The amendments made by the 2012 Act will not apply to claims for damages in respect of diffuse mesothelioma until the Lord
Chancellor has carried out a review of the likely effect of the amendments in relation to such proceedings, and published a
report of the conclusions of the review. The commencement of the amendments was deferred in relation to publication and
privacy proceedings: see the Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012 (Commencement No.5 and Saving
Provision) Order 2013 (SI 2013/77), which lists the proceedings excluded and provides a definition of “publication and privacy
proceedings”. However in respect of such proceedings s.44 (success fees) but not s.46 (after the event insurance premiums)
came into force on 6 April 2019: Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012 (Commencement No.13)
Order 2018 (SI 2018/1287). The commencement of the amendments was deferred until 6 April 2016 in relation to certain
insolvency proceedings: the Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012 (Commencement No.12) Order 2016
(SI 2016/345) art.2 (see further Vol.1 para.48.0.2.4).
This section describes the law relating to funding arrangements made after 1 April 2013.
Parties may continue to enter into conditional fee agreements with their lawyers. However an amendment to the Courts and
Legal Services Act 1990 (s.58A(6)) provides that success fees under conditional fee agreements may not be recovered between
the parties.
The Conditional Fee Agreements Order 2013 provides that success fees may not exceed 100% of the base costs. In claims for
personal injuries, success fees are capped at 25% of the general damages for pain, suffering and loss of amenity and damages
for past pecuniary loss in proceedings at first instance and at 100% of those heads of damage on appeal.
Premiums for after the event insurance policies are not recoverable save insofar as provided by regulations made under s.58C
of the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990 and save in limited classes of cases in respect of which the commencement of the
amendments has been deferred. Sections 29 and 30 of the Access to Justice Act 1999 have been repealed (s.29 is repealed except
in respect of claims for damages in respect of diffuse mesothelioma and publication and privacy proceedings). The repeal of
s.29 was deferred until 6 April 2016 in respect of certain insolvency proceedings. The Recovery of Costs Insurance Premiums
in Clinical Negligence Proceedings (No.2) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/739) permit the recovery of that part of a premium which
insures against the liability to pay for experts’ reports on liability and causation in clinical negligence proceedings if the financial
value of the claim for damages exceeds £1,000. The Supreme Court has confirmed that, in the absence of agreement or specific
statutory provision to the contrary, a party cannot recover an ATE premium as part of the costs of legal expenses: McGraddie
v McGraddie [2015] UKSC 1.
Damages-based agreements, which previously could only be used in employment claims, can now be used to fund proceedings
of the same types as conditional fee agreements. The Damages-Based Agreements Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/609) set out the
requirements of a valid agreement and limit the amount of costs payable by the client.
For commentary on litigation funding before 1 April 2013, see Civil Procedure 2019, Vol.2, Section 7A1.
