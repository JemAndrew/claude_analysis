Particulars of claim to be included in the Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
20-F34
The first Defendant is a dealer in [specific nature of business] and the second Defendant is a finance company.
2.
By a written agreement dated [date] (“the supply agreement”) the first Defendant agreed to supply and install [identify
the goods] (“the goods”) as set out in the schedule to the supply agreement for a total sum of £[..........]. A copy of the
agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
3.
It was an express term of the supply agreement that [set out any term relied on] and, by s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act
1979, there were implied terms implied into of supply agreement that the goods should be reasonable fit for the purpose
and of satisfactory quality.
[Or, when in force]
3.
It was an express term of the supply agreement that [set out any term relied on] and, by s.9 of the Consumer Rights Act
2015, there was a term implied into the supply agreement that the quality of the goods should be satisfactory [and under
s.10 that the goods should be reasonably fit for purpose].
4.
By a written agreement dated [date], (“the loan agreement”) the second Defendant agreed to advance the sum of £[..........]
to the Claimant for the purpose of paying the sums due under the supply agreement. A copy of the loan agreement is
annexed to this Claim Form.
5.
The loan agreement was a borrower-lender-supplier agreement within s.75 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”).

6.
In purported pursuance of the supply agreement, the first Defendant supplied and installed the goods and the Claimant
paid the said price of £[..........]:
 
(a)as to £[..........] in cash;
 
(b)as to £[..........] by means of the moneys advanced under the loan agreement which were paid by the second
Defendant directly to the first Defendant at the Claimant’s request.
7.
In breach of the supply agreement, the goods did not conform to the expressed terms nor were they reasonably fit for the
purpose or of satisfactory quality.
Particulars
 
(a)[give details of matters relied on]
8.
By reason of the breaches of the supply agreement, the Claimant has suffered loss and damage in the sum of £[..........]
being (e.g. the cost of repairing the said defects).
9.
By reason of s.75(1) of the CCA, the second Defendant is jointly and severally liable with the first Defendant to the
Claimant in respect of the said breaches of the supply agreement.
The Claimant therefore claims against the first and second Defendants jointly and severally:
 
(1)Damages.
 
(2)Interest at the rate of x% pursuant to s.69 of the County Courts Act 1984 to judgment.

Note
If there is a viable claim against the supplier as well as the lender it is unlikely that CCA s.75A could apply and thus there is
no point in providing an alternative version of this precedent: see para.20-F24 above.
