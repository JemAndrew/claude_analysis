Reply by lender to a defence of the supplier based on the Hire-purchase Act 1964, Part III
No statutory form for reply
1.
20-F71
By way of reply, the Claimant denies the validity of the defence.
2.
The Claimant admits:
 
(a)the said hire-purchase agreement between XY Limited and PQ;
 
(b)the purported sale of the car by PQ to GH.
3.
The Claimant denies that the purported sale by PQ to GH operated to pass title to the car to QR in accordance with the
Hire-Purchase Act 1964, Part III either as alleged in the defence or at all:
 
(a)GH was not a private purchaser: at all material times he was a motor dealer carrying on business as “GH Motors”;
 
(b)GH did not act in good faith: the purported sale was a sham and no money changed hands;
 
(c)GH had full knowledge of the existence of the hire-purchase agreement prior to his purported purchase of the
car, having made enquiries of H-P Information Limited which informed him of the existence of the hire-purchase
agreement.
