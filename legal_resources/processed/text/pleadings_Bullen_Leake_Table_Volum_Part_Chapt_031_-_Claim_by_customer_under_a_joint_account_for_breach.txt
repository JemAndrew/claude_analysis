Claim by customer under a joint account for breach of mandate
1.
9-E4
The Defendant is a bank..
2.
The Claimant, together with AB, holds a joint account (“the Account”) with the Defendant pursuant to a written mandate
authorising the Defendant to honour payment instructions only where such instructions are given jointly by the Claimant
and AB. A copy of the mandate is attached to these Particulars of Claim marked “A”.
3.
By a letter of instruction dated [date], AB purportedly instructed the Defendant to transfer the sum of £[..........] from the
Account to AB’s account at the XY Bank by way of a CHAPS transfer (“the Instruction”).
4.
Wrongfully and in breach of mandate, the Defendant honoured and complied with the Instruction, and has purported to
debit the Account with the sum of £[..........].
5.
In the premises, the Defendant was not entitled so to debit the Account, and the Claimant is entitled to and claims the
sum of £[..........].
6.
Further the Claimant is entitled to and claims interest [at the contractual rate applicable to the Account or] pursuant to
s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981 on the sum of £[..........] at the rate of [..........] per cent per annum from [the date of
the debit], amounting to £[..........] at [date of claim] and continuing at a daily rate of £[..........]; alternatively on such sum
at such rate and for such period as the Court may determine.
AND the Claimant claims:
 
(1)A declaration that the Defendant was not entitled to debit the Account in accordance with the Instruction;
 
(2)£[..........];
 
(3)Interest pursuant to [contract or] s.35A of the Senior Courts Act 1981, under paragraph 6 above.
