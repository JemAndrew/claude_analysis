1.
21-F14
It is admitted that by an agreement contained in or evidenced by a letter dated [date] from the Claimant to the Defendant,
the Claimant agreed to act for the Defendant as his sole selling agent in the marketing of his property known as [identify
the property] for a x month period ending on [date].
2.
It is further admitted that on [date], the Defendant sold the property through the agency of the [identify the relevant agency]
at a price of £[..........]. The said sale was effected through an introduction that was effected on [date].
3.
In the premises, it is denied that the Defendant acted in breach of his agreement with the Claimant.
4.
If, which is denied, the Defendant was in breach of contract as alleged or at all, the Claimant suffered no loss thereby in
that during the period of the sole agency the Claimant was unable to arrange the introduction of a single person to view the
property. Further, in a telephone conversation on [date], the Claimant told the Defendant that there was not the slightest
chance of anyone showing any interest in the property bearing in mind its location and general appearance. In the premises,
it is denied that the Claimant would have introduced a buyer to the Defendant during the period of its sole agency.
