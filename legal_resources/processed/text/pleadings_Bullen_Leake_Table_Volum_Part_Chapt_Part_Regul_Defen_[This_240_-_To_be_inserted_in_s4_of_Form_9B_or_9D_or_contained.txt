Regulated agreements: making of the contract
Defences arising out of the failure of the agreement to contain the “prescribed terms” under s.61 of the CCA and the
Regulations
[This precedent is now only relevant to agreements concluded prior to 6 April 2007 when subsections (3) to (5) of CCA
s.127 were repealed. For agreements made after 6th April 2007, Form 20–E54 should be used instead.]
To be inserted in s.4 of Form 9B or 9D or contained in a separate document
1.
20-F53
The Defendant denies that the document referred to in the Particulars of Claim constitutes a valid agreement enforceable
by the Claimant.
2.
The document purports to be an agreement regulated by the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (“the CCA”) and thus governed
by the Consumer Credit (Agreements) Regulations 1983 (“the Regulations”).
3.
The document was not properly executed contrary to s.61(1) of the CCA in that:
 
(a)The document was not signed by the Defendant contrary to s.61(1)(a) of the CCA.
 
(b)The document did not contain a term stating the amount of the credit, contrary to the Regulations, Schedule 6,
paragraph 1.
 
(c)The document did not state the rate of interest on the credit to be provided under the agreement, contrary to
paragraph 4 of Schedule 6 to the Regulations.
 
(d)The document did not state how the Defendant was to discharge his obligations under the agreement to make the
repayments, contrary to paragraph 5 of Schedule 6 to the Regulations.
4.
In the premises, the purported agreement is unenforceable under s.65 of the CCA and the Court cannot make an enforcement
order under s.65(1) of the CCA by reason of s.127(3) of the CCA.
