Chapter 5 - The Arbitral Proceedings
Evidentiary measures
28-29
Under art.28, a Tribunal may issue interim and conservatory measures that are intended to ensure that evidence remains
available. These measures may overlap with, but are to be distinguished from, orders for production that are issued under art.25.
The general rule, as is reflected for example in the IBA Representation Guidelines, is that a party should maintain evidence that
relates to the dispute. A Tribunal should not be required to issue an order in that respect. However, if a party has a reasonable
concern that such evidence will not be maintained, then it is a proper subject to a request for relief under art.28.
28-30
Examples of the type of conservatory or interim measures regarding evidence include the following:
 
(a)An order that parties preserve all electronic copies of documents where the issue was whether certain images of
documents had been forged or altered. 486
 
(b)A direction that the respondent provide access to a power plant for expert inspection. 487
 
(c)An order that no documents be disclosed in parallel proceedings without the Tribunal’s consent. 488
 
(d)An order that a party use its best efforts to obtain access to documents at a work site in a construction case.
 
(e)An order that a party seek to obtain release of documents held by a government pursuant to a criminal investigation.
