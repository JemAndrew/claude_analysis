Hire-purchase and conditional sale agreements
Claim by hirer where goods have been wrongfully repossessed, contrary to s.91 of the CCA (regulated agreement)
Particulars of claim to be included in the Claim Form N1 or served subsequently
1.
20-F32
By a written agreement dated [date], the Defendant let to the Claimant on hire-purchase [identify the goods] (“the goods”).
A copy of the agreement is annexed to this Claim Form.
2.
The cash price of the goods was £[..........], the deposit was £[..........], the total charge for credit was £[..........] and the hire-
purchase balance of £[..........] was payable by x equal consecutive monthly instalments of £[..........]. The total purchase
price was £[..........].
3.
The said agreement was a regulated borrower-lender-supplier agreement within the meaning of the Consumer Credit Act
1974 (“the CCA”).
4.
In pursuance of the agreement, the Claimant paid the deposit and instalments totalling £[..........]. Consequently the goods
became and at all times thereafter remained protected goods for the purposes of s.90 of the CCA.
5.
In breach of ss.90 and 91 of the CCA, on or about [date], on the pretext that the Claimant had failed to pay an instalment
of rent, the Defendant without the consent the Claimant or any order of the Court repossessed the goods.
6.
In the premises, pursuant to s.91 of the CCA:
 
(a)the agreement automatically terminated on the said date;
 
(b)the Claimant was released from all liability under the agreement;
 
(c)the Claimant became entitled to recover from the Defendant all moneys paid under the agreement, namely £[..........].
