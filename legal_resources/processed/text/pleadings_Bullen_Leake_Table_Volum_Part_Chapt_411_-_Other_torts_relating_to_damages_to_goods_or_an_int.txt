Chapter 26 - Title to Goods—Torts Against Goods
Other torts relating to damages to goods or an interest in goods
26-19
The introduction into the definition of unlawful interference with goods of “any other tort so far as it results in damage to goods
or to an interest in goods” is wide in its scope. The categories of torts may include claims in nuisance within the rule in Rylands
v Fletcher as well as claims involving slander of title to goods and passing-off. Examples of this type of claim are not included
in the present section. The common feature of all claims based on unlawful interference is the uniform relief available.
